# Form W-7: ITIN Guide for Canadian Landlords with Illinois Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is an IRS application that assigns a unique nine-digit taxpayer identification number—an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)—to foreign nationals who do not qualify for a US Social Security Number (SSN) but have a US tax filing requirement.

An ITIN functions similarly to an SSN for federal tax purposes. It allows the IRS to track your US tax liability, file returns, and comply with withholding obligations. For Canadian landlords, obtaining an ITIN is mandatory before filing a US non-resident tax return (Form 1040-NR) or complying with Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) rules.

The ITIN is not a work authorization document and does not grant you employment rights in the United States.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Illinois

As a Canadian resident earning rental income from Illinois property, you are classified as a **non-resident alien** for US federal tax purposes. The US tax system requires all individuals with US-source income—including rental income—to obtain a taxpayer identification number and file appropriate returns.

### Federal Filing Obligation

Under Article IV of the Canada-US Tax Treaty, you must file a Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) to report:
- Gross rental income from your Illinois property
- Mortgage interest, property taxes, and allowable deductions
- Depreciation (if claiming)
- Net rental income subject to US tax

The federal tax rate on rental income depends on your total income and filing status, but typically ranges from 10% to 37% (marginal rates).

### Illinois State Filing Obligation

Illinois imposes a **flat 4.95% state income tax** on all income, including non-resident rental income. Non-resident landlords must file Illinois Form IL-1040-NR (Non-Resident and Part-Year Resident Income Tax Return). While Illinois allows a credit for federal taxes paid (under Article XXIV of the Canada-US Tax Treaty), an ITIN is required to properly document your Illinois state tax filing as well.

### FIRPTA Withholding Requirement

If you plan to sell your Illinois rental property, FIRPTA (Section 1445 of the Internal Revenue Code) requires the buyer's closing agent to withhold 15% of the sale proceeds for federal tax liability. To reduce or eliminate this withholding, you must obtain a Certificate of Withholding from the IRS, which requires an ITIN.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must apply for an ITIN if you meet **all** the following conditions:

1. You are a non-resident alien (not a US citizen or permanent resident)
2. You do not have and are ineligible for a US Social Security Number
3. You have a US tax filing requirement (including Illinois rental income)
4. You are required to have a US taxpayer identification number

Canadian citizens who are residents of Canada automatically meet conditions 1 and 2. Condition 3 is satisfied by owning rental property in Illinois. Therefore, most Canadian landlords with Illinois property must file Form W-7 before filing their first Form 1040-NR.

**Filing Timeline:** You should apply for an ITIN before the tax filing deadline for the year in which you earned rental income. The IRS recommends applying at least 4–6 weeks before you file your return, though technically you can apply concurrently with your 1040-NR.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, assemble:
- **Valid passport or national identity document** (notarized copy)
- **Proof of Canadian residency** (driver's license, provincial ID, or property deed from Canada)
- **Proof of US tax filing requirement** (signed lease agreement, property deed, or title insurance policy for the Illinois property)
- **Completed Form W-7** (most current version from IRS.gov)

All documents must be in English or accompanied by certified English translations.

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7

**Part A – Applicant Information:**
- Enter your full legal name (as it appears on your passport)
- Enter your Canadian address (your principal residence)
- Enter your date of birth and country of citizenship (Canada)
- Check the box: "I am applying for an ITIN because I am not eligible for an SSN"

**Part B – Type of Applicant:**
- Select the box corresponding to your filing requirement. For rental income, select: **"Nonresident alien required to file a US income tax return"** or **"Nonresident alien with US source income"**

**Part C – US Address:**
- Enter the address of your Illinois rental property (the primary reason for the ITIN)
- Alternatively, enter your US tax representative's address (if you have one)

**Part D – Identification Document:**
- Indicate the document type (passport) and country of issue (Canada)
- Provide the document number and expiration date

**Part E – Residency Status:**
- Confirm your country of residence (Canada) and that you do not intend to reside permanently in the US

### Step 3: Have Documents Certified and Notarized

The IRS requires that copies of your identity documents be authenticated by:
- A notary public, **or**
- A US consulate or embassy, **or**
- An IRS Acceptance Agent (often found at tax preparation offices)

Canadian notaries are accepted by the IRS. You can have your passport and supporting documents notarized through a Canadian notary, and these certifications are valid for IRS purposes.

### Step 4: Submit Your Application

**Mail Option (Recommended for Canadians):**
Send your completed Form W-7, notarized documents, and IRS Form 8233 (if claiming tax treaty benefits—see below) to:

Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operations
P.O. Box 149342
Austin, TX 78714-9342

Allow **4–8 weeks** for processing by mail.

**In-Person Option:**
If you visit the US, you can apply in person at an IRS office or designated acceptance agent. This accelerates processing to 2–3 weeks. However, from Canada, this is typically not practical.

### Step 5: Receive Your ITIN

The IRS will mail your ITIN assignment notice (Form W-7-C or a letter) to your Canadian address. The ITIN is immediately usable for filing purposes once assigned.

## Illinois-Specific Considerations

### Illinois Property Tax Impact on Filing

Illinois has one of the highest property tax rates in the US at an average effective rate of **2.27%**. Combined with the 4.95% Illinois income tax and federal tax, your total tax burden on rental income can exceed 20% before deductions.

When reporting on your Illinois Form IL-1040-NR:
- **Deduction:** Real property taxes paid are fully deductible against rental income
- **Timing:** Use the cash basis (taxes paid in the year, not accrued) unless you elect accrual accounting for all income

### Claiming the Tax Treaty Benefit

The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article X) provides that rental income is taxed in the country where the property is located. However, you may be eligible for reduced withholding rates on certain income. To claim treaty benefits, you must:

1. File Form W-7 (to obtain the ITIN)
2. File **Form 8233 (Exemption from Withholding on Compensation for Independent Personal Services)** or a **Form 6166 letter** with your initial 1040-NR filing

This ensures you are not over-withheld and establishes your treaty-protected status.

### Coordination with Canadian T1 Return

Your Canadian personal income tax return (Form T1) must also report worldwide income, including the net rental income from Illinois. Use Form T2209 to claim a foreign tax credit for the US federal and Illinois state taxes paid. The IRS ITIN, tied to your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN) on your T1, ensures CRA and IRS records align.

### Deadlines

- **Form W-7 application:** File at least 4–6 weeks before filing your Form 1040-NR
- **Form 1040-NR deadline (US):** June 15, 2024 (for 2023 income) if filing from Canada; otherwise April 15
- **Form IL-1040-NR deadline:** Same as Form 1040-NR (June 15 for Canadian residents)
- **Form T1 deadline (Canada):** June 15, 2024 (for 2023)
