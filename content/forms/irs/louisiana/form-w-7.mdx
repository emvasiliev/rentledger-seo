# Form W-7 for Canadian Landlords with Louisiana Rental Property

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7, **Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)**, is the IRS application used by non-US citizens and non-resident aliens who require a US taxpayer identification number but are ineligible for a Social Security Number (SSN). The ITIN functions as a nine-digit identifier (formatted XXX-XX-XXXX) exclusively for US federal tax filing and reporting purposes.

For Canadian landlords, the ITIN becomes mandatory when you own rental real estate in the United States, including Louisiana. Without an ITIN, you cannot file US federal tax returns (Form 1040-NR), pay FIRPTA withholding taxes on property sales, or satisfy IRS reporting requirements on US-sourced income.

## Louisiana Context: Why Your ITIN Matters

Louisiana presents a specific tax challenge for Canadian property owners. The state imposes:

- **Louisiana state income tax** of 4.25% on net rental income for non-residents
- **Louisiana property tax** averaging 0.56% of assessed value annually
- **FIRPTA withholding obligations** at the federal level when you eventually sell the property

To file both your **federal Form 1040-NR** (Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) and your **Louisiana Form IT-540NR** (Louisiana Non-Resident Tax Return), the IRS requires your ITIN before you can file. Additionally, any buyer of your Louisiana property must withhold 15% of the sale price under the Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) unless you obtain a non-recognition of gain certificate—a process that also requires your ITIN.

On your Canadian T1 return, you'll report your US rental income and claim foreign tax credits for both federal and Louisiana state taxes paid, making proper US tax filing essential to avoid double taxation and maximize your Canadian tax position.

## Who Files Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you:

- Are a Canadian citizen or resident of Canada
- Do not have and are ineligible to obtain a US Social Security Number
- Own rental property in Louisiana and generate US-source rental income
- Plan to file a US federal income tax return (Form 1040-NR)
- Face FIRPTA withholding on a potential property sale
- Are required to report US income to the IRS under the Canada-US Tax Treaty

Canadian residents of Louisiana and those with Green Cards should apply for SSNs instead; the ITIN is specifically for non-resident aliens.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete and File Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, assemble:

- **Valid passport or national identity document** (your Canadian passport, certified copy)
- **Proof of Louisiana rental property ownership** (deed, title insurance policy, property tax statement from Louisiana, or purchase agreement)
- **Evidence of non-resident status** (proof of Canadian residence: utility bill, provincial government ID, Canadian bank statement)
- **US taxpayer identification purpose documentation** (rental income documentation, evidence of FIRPTA obligations, or a draft 1040-NR)

The IRS requires certified copies of documents—typically certified by the issuing authority or notarized. Louisiana property tax assessments from the specific parish (county) are strong supporting evidence.

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately

**Section 1: Type of Applicant**
- Check **Box c**: "Nonresident alien individual"

**Section 2: Personal Information**
- Provide your legal name exactly as it appears on your passport
- Enter your date of birth (mm/dd/yyyy format)
- Provide your Canadian mailing address (the address where the IRS will mail your ITIN)
- Include your email and phone number if available

**Section 3: ITIN Reason Code**
- Enter code **P1**: "Nonresident alien required to file a US return"
  - This is the standard code for Canadian landlords filing 1040-NR for rental income
  - Alternative: **P2** applies if you are claiming FIRPTA withholding relief only

**Section 4: Dependent/Spouse**
- Leave blank unless applying for multiple family members on one application

**Section 5: Acceptance Agent Authorization**
- You may authorize a CPA, attorney, or tax professional to submit the form on your behalf
- A **Certified Acceptance Agent (CAA)** or IRS office can process your application

### Step 3: File Form W-7

You have three filing options:

**Option A: IRS by Mail**
- Mail Form W-7 with original or certified documents to:
  - IRS ITIN Operation, Austin TX 73301, USA
- Processing time: 30–45 days
- Risk: Documents may be lost in mail

**Option B: IRS Office (In-Person)**
- Visit an IRS office in Louisiana (Baton Rouge or New Orleans) or your home US state
- Bring originals of all supporting documents
- Processing often faster than mail
- Limited availability

**Option C: Certified Acceptance Agent (CAA)**
- Canadian tax firms, cross-border CPAs, and law firms can be CAAs
- They electronically submit Form W-7 to the IRS with certified documents
- Processing time: 21–30 days
- Recommended for Canadian residents (most reliable and fastest)

Most Canadian landlords use CAAs—firms that specialize in cross-border taxation can expedite processing and ensure compliance with Louisiana-specific requirements.

## Louisiana-Specific Considerations

### State Tax Reporting Once You Have Your ITIN

Once the IRS issues your ITIN, you must file:

- **Form 1040-NR (U.S. Income Tax Return for Nonresident Alien Individuals)** federally
- **Louisiana Form IT-540NR (Nonresident Individual Income Tax Return)** to the Louisiana Department of Revenue

Louisiana taxes rental income at **4.25%** on your net rental profit (gross rental income less depreciation, mortgage interest, property taxes, maintenance, and utilities). Importantly, Louisiana does not allow non-residents a blanket exemption—filing is mandatory if you have Louisiana-source income exceeding statutory minimums.

The Louisiana tax year aligns with the federal calendar year (January 1–December 31), with a federal extension automatically extending your Louisiana deadline.

### Property Tax Implications

Louisiana's ad valorem (property tax) rates average **0.56%** of assessed value annually but vary by parish:

- Orleans Parish (New Orleans): ~1.0%
- East Baton Rouge Parish: ~0.56%
- Jefferson Parish: ~0.65%

These property taxes are **deductible** on your US Form 1040-NR and create foreign tax credits claimable on your Canadian T1 return. Keep detailed records of annual property tax payments.

### FIRPTA and the ITIN

If you sell your Louisiana property, the buyer's title company or attorney must withhold **15% of the gross sale price** as FIRPTA withholding unless you obtain a **Certification of Non-Recognition of Gain** from the IRS. To request this certificate, you must first have an ITIN and file Form 8288-B. This process cannot begin without your ITIN, making early application critical if you anticipate a sale.

## Common Mistakes Canadian Landlords Make with Form W-7

1. **Incorrect Document Certification**: The IRS rejects uncertified or self-notarized copies. Use your Canadian bank or the issuing government body for certification.

2. **Mismatched Name Spelling**: If your name differs between your passport and property deed, reconcile this before submitting. The IRS will reject inconsistent applications.

3. **Missing Louisiana Property Connection**: Failing to clearly document your Louisiana rental property on the application delays processing. Attach a copy of the deed or property tax bill directly to Form W-7.

4. **Filing Too Late**: Waiting until you have a buyer lined up for your property to apply for an ITIN can delay FIRPTA compliance. Apply when you first purchase the property.

5. **Not Using a Qualified CAA**: Some tax preparers file Form W-7 incorrectly, resulting in IRS requests for additional information. Use a cross-border CAA experienced with Louisiana property owners.
