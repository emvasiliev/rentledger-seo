# Form 1040-NR for Canadian Landlords with Iowa Rental Property

## What is Form 1040-NR?

Form 1040-NR is the United States Individual Income Tax Return for Non-Resident Aliens. It is the primary federal tax return filed by non-US residents (including Canadian citizens and permanent residents) who have income that is "effectively connected" with a US trade or business—most commonly, net rental income from US real property.

For Canadian landlords with Iowa rental properties, the 1040-NR becomes essential when you elect under **Internal Revenue Code Section 871(d)** to treat your rental income as effectively connected income (ECI). This election allows you to deduct operating expenses, mortgage interest, property taxes, and depreciation against your rental income—substantially reducing your US tax liability compared to the flat 30% withholding tax that would otherwise apply.

## How Form 1040-NR Applies to Iowa Rental Properties

### Federal Tax Treatment

When you own rental property in Iowa, the IRS considers this rental income to be US-source income subject to US taxation. Without the Section 871(d) election, rental income is taxed at a flat rate of 30% with no deductions permitted (beyond the standard deduction of $12,950 for 2023).

By filing Form 1040-NR and making the Section 871(d) election, you:

- Report gross rental income from your Iowa property
- Deduct all ordinary and necessary business expenses (mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, repairs, utilities, property management fees, depreciation)
- Pay tax on net income at progressive federal tax rates (10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, etc., depending on your total income)

This election typically results in significantly lower federal tax than the default 30% withholding.

### Iowa State Income Tax Requirement

**Iowa imposes a 6% state income tax on net rental income earned by non-residents.** This means you must also file **Iowa Form IA 1040** (the Iowa Resident and Non-Resident Individual Income Tax Return) or the non-resident version. Iowa follows federal taxable income as a starting point, so the deductions you claim on your federal 1040-NR directly reduce your Iowa state tax base.

Additionally, Iowa imposes property taxes on real property. The current statewide average effective property tax rate in Iowa is **1.57%** of property value, though rates vary significantly by county. These property taxes are deductible on both your federal 1040-NR and Iowa return.

## Who Must File Form 1040-NR

You must file Form 1040-NR if you meet all of the following criteria:

1. **You are a non-resident alien** for US tax purposes (a Canadian citizen who does not hold US permanent residency or green card status)
2. **You have effectively connected income (ECI)** from US sources, such as:
   - Net rental income from Iowa real property (after deducting expenses)
   - Other US business income
3. **Your ECI exceeds the standard deduction** ($12,950 for 2023, adjusted annually for inflation)
4. **You are electing Section 871(d) treatment** to claim deductions against your rental income

If your rental income is below the standard deduction threshold after expenses, you may still benefit from filing (to offset income with deductions and foreign tax credits), though filing may not be required.

## Step-by-Step How to Complete Form 1040-NR

### Step 1: Gather Required Information

Before starting, assemble:

- Gross rental income from your Iowa property for the tax year
- Documentation of all deductible expenses (mortgage interest statement from your lender, property tax receipts, insurance statements, repair and maintenance invoices, property management fees, utility bills)
- Depreciation calculation (building depreciation using 27.5-year straight-line method; land is not depreciable)
- US individual identification number (ITIN) if you don't have a Social Security number
- Your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN) for reference
- Any withholding taxes paid to the IRS or Iowa

### Step 2: Complete Part I – Identification and Dependents

- Line 1a: Enter your name as it appears on your passport
- Line 1b: Enter your ITIN (you must obtain an ITIN before filing if you don't have a US Social Security number)
- Line 1c: Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Line 1d: Indicate whether you are a resident or non-resident alien for the entire tax year (check "Non-resident alien")
- Lines 1e–1g: Address in Canada (your home country)
- Line 2: Check the box confirming you do not claim dependents (unless children are US residents or citizens)

### Step 3: Complete Part II – Income

This is the critical section for landlords:

- **Line 2**: Wages (leave blank unless you have W-2 income)
- **Line 5**: Interest income (from US bank accounts or bonds)
- **Line 6**: Dividends (from US investments, if applicable)
- **Schedule E (Supplemental Income and Loss)**: Attach Schedule E to report rental income and expenses:
  - Part I, Line 1a: Address of the Iowa rental property
  - Line 3: Gross rental income received
  - Lines 8–27: All deductible expenses (mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, repairs, utilities, property management, advertising, legal and accounting fees)
  - Line 28: Depreciation (from Form 4562)
  - Line 29: Total expenses
  - Line 21: Net rental income (or loss)

Transfer the net rental income from Schedule E, Line 21, to Form 1040-NR, Line 9 (Other income).

### Step 4: Complete Part III – Adjustments and Deductions

- **Line 12**: Standard deduction for non-resident aliens ($12,950 in 2023)
- Do **not** attempt to claim itemized deductions; non-resident aliens on the 1040-NR are restricted to the standard deduction

### Step 5: Calculate Taxable Income and Tax

- Subtract the standard deduction from your total income to arrive at taxable income
- Apply 2023 tax brackets for non-resident aliens (generally the same as resident aliens but with fewer deductions available)
- Calculate federal income tax owed or refund due

### Step 6: Complete Part IV – Other Taxes and Withholding

- Indicate any US tax withheld from your rental income during the year
- Report any estimated tax payments made

### Step 7: Make the Section 871(d) Election

Include a statement with your return electing under Section 871(d) to treat your rental income as effectively connected income. The statement should read:

> "The taxpayer elects under Internal Revenue Code Section 871(d) to treat fixed or determinable annual or periodical gains, profits, or income derived from real property located in the United States as effectively connected income."

## Iowa-Specific Considerations

### Iowa State Return Filing

File **Iowa Form IA 1040** (Non-Resident Individual Income Tax Return) with the Iowa Department of Revenue by the same April 15 deadline. Iowa uses your federal taxable income as a starting point and applies the 6% state income tax rate.

Your Iowa return should reflect:
- Same rental income reported on federal return
- Same rental expense deductions
- Same depreciation deduction
- Iowa state income tax rate of **6%**

Any Iowa taxes withheld or paid through estimated quarterly payments reduce your final state tax liability.

### Property Tax Deductibility

Iowa property taxes on your rental property are fully deductible on both federal and state returns as ordinary rental expenses. Ensure you have documentation of all property tax payments made to your county assessor.

### County-Specific Variations

Iowa's property tax varies by county (ranging from approximately 1.2% to 1.9% of assessed value). Your specific tax liability depends on the county where your property is located. Include the actual amount paid, not an estimated rate.

### No Iowa City/Local Income Tax

Unlike some states, Iowa does not impose local city or county income taxes, simplifying your filing requirements to federal and state only.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Failing to Make the Section 871(d) Election Properly**

Many Canadian landlords file the 1040-NR but do not explicitly make the election in writing. Without this election, the IRS may treat the income as passive foreign investment income subject to 30% withholding. Always include a signed statement electing Section 871(d).
