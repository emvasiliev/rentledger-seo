# Form W-7: ITIN Application Guide for Canadian Landlords with Idaho Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the official IRS application to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number issued by the IRS to non-US citizens and non-resident aliens who have US tax filing obligations but do not qualify for a Social Security Number (SSN).

As a Canadian landlord, you cannot use your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN) on US tax returns. The IRS requires a unique US taxpayer identifier—an ITIN—to file Form 1040-NR (U.S. Income Tax Return for Nonresident Aliens), report rental income from your Idaho property, and comply with FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding requirements.

## How Form W-7 Applies Specifically to Idaho Landlords

### Federal Tax Obligations

If you own rental property in Idaho generating income, you must file Form 1040-NR with the IRS. Your ITIN becomes your identifying number on this return. You'll report:
- Gross rental income from your Idaho property
- Deductions (mortgage interest, property taxes, maintenance, depreciation, HOA fees)
- Net rental income subject to a flat 30% withholding rate on FIRPTA dispositions or graduated rates if you file Form 1040-NR

The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article XIII) provides relief from double taxation on real property income. As a Canadian resident, you may claim a foreign tax credit on your Canadian T1 General return for US federal taxes paid, allowing you to avoid being taxed on the same income twice.

### Idaho State Tax Obligations

Idaho imposes a **5.8% flat state income tax** on rental income earned within the state. Non-resident individuals must file Form 43 (Idaho Nonresident and Part-Year Resident Income Tax Return) annually.

**Critical point**: You must have an ITIN to file both your federal 1040-NR and your Idaho state return. Without one, you cannot legally report your rental income or claim deductions in Idaho.

### Property Tax Considerations

Idaho's average effective property tax rate is **0.69%** on assessed property value. While property taxes are a deductible expense on Form 1040-NR (Schedule E), you'll pay these to your local Idaho county assessor separately from income tax.

## Who Files Form W-7

You file Form W-7 if you are:
- A Canadian resident (non-US citizen)
- Generating US-source income (rental income from Idaho property)
- Required to file a US tax return
- Lacking a valid Social Security Number

**Non-resident alien status**: The IRS considers you a non-resident alien for tax purposes if you don't meet the Substantial Presence Test (generally, being in the US fewer than 183 days in the current year and prior two years, weighted). Most Canadian landlords qualify as non-resident aliens.

You may file Form W-7 independently or attach it to your first Form 1040-NR filing.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### 1. Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, assemble:
- Valid Canadian passport or national identity document
- Proof of Canadian residency (utility bill, rental agreement, or CRA Notice of Assessment, no older than 12 months)
- Your Canadian SIN (for reference only; it cannot serve as your US tax ID)
- Details about your Idaho rental property (address, purchase date)

### 2. Complete Section 1: Personal Information

Enter your full legal name exactly as it appears on your Canadian passport. Provide your Canadian mailing address (your principal residence in Canada). The IRS will mail your ITIN approval letter to this address.

**Important**: Use the same name consistently across all US tax documents.

### 3. Complete Section 2: Type of Applicant

Select the appropriate category. Most Canadian rental property owners select:
- **Box b**: "Nonresident alien individual"

This classification is required because you are not a US citizen and do not meet the Substantial Presence Test.

### 4. Complete Section 3: Reason for Application

Check the box for:
- **"Other" (specify)**: State "Filing US Federal and State Income Tax Returns – Rental Property Income from Idaho"

Alternatively, if you're applying in connection with a FIRPTA withholding event (property sale), note "FIRPTA Withholding."

### 5. Complete Sections 4–5: Income and Filing Information

- **Line 4a**: Indicate the type of US income: "Rental Income – Real Property"
- **Line 4d**: State "Idaho, USA"
- **Line 5**: Confirm you will file Form 1040-NR and Idaho Form 43

### 6. Attach Required Documents

Submit copies (not originals) of:
- Passport biographical page
- Proof of Canadian residency
- A completed Form 8233 (if applicable) or a letter confirming your intent to file 1040-NR

**Do not send originals.** The IRS requires certified copies or notarized documents for certain jurisdictions, but Canadian documents generally do not require notarization if properly certified by the issuing authority.

### 7. Submit Your Application

Mail Form W-7 with supporting documents to the applicable IRS address:

**For international applicants (Canada):**
```
Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operation
P.O. Box 149342
Austin, TX 78714-9342
USA
```

Alternatively, if you file Form 1040-NR, submit Form W-7 as an attachment with your return to the address specified in the Form 1040-NR instructions.

## Idaho-Specific Considerations

### Nexus and Sourcing

Idaho taxes rental income at source. Your rental income from an Idaho property is entirely Idaho-source income, subject to the 5.8% state tax regardless of your residency status. You have a "nexus" (connection) to Idaho through your property ownership, making state filing mandatory.

### Combined Filing Status

If you're married and your spouse is a US citizen or resident alien, you may elect to file jointly on Form 1040-NR. Your spouse may have a different ITIN status; clarify your situation with a cross-border tax advisor before filing.

### Real Property Withholding (FIRPTA)

If you sell your Idaho rental property, the buyer must withhold **15% of the net proceeds** under FIRPTA (or 10% if you meet certain tests). Your ITIN is essential for this withholding calculation. The withholding agent uses your ITIN to report the transaction on Form 8288-B to the IRS.

### Estimated Tax Payments

Once you receive your ITIN, the IRS may require you to make quarterly estimated payments if your US tax liability exceeds $1,000. Use Form 1040-ES (NR) to calculate quarterly amounts due April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Providing Incomplete Supporting Documents**: Ensure all copies are legible and certified. Illegible documents delay processing by 4–6 months.

2. **Mismatching Names Across Documents**: Your name on Form W-7, your passport, and your 1040-NR must be identical. Inconsistencies trigger IRS requests for clarification.

3. **Filing Without Understanding Residency Status**: Don't assume you're a non-resident. The Substantial Presence Test has specific calculations. Incorrect classification affects both federal and Idaho filing obligations.

4. **Forgetting Idaho Form 43**: Obtaining an ITIN addresses federal filing only. You still must file Idaho Form 43 separately with the Idaho State Tax Commission. Failure to file invites state penalties.

5. **Delaying Application Until After Year-End**: Apply for your ITIN *before* December 31 of the tax year in which you earn income. Processing takes 6–12 weeks. Delaying risks missing filing deadlines.

6. **Omitting Foreign Tax Credit Coordination**: When preparing your Canadian T1, ensure you claim the Foreign Tax Credit (Schedule 1, Line 40500) for US taxes paid. This prevents double taxation on your Idaho income.

## Key Deadlines

- **Initial Application**: File Form W-7 *with* your first Form 1040-NR or upon acquiring the rental property
- **Form 1040-NR Filing Deadline**: June 15 (for non-residents; April 15 if you elect US residency status)
- **Idaho Form 43 Deadline**: June 15
- **Estimated Tax Payments**: Quarterly (April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15)
