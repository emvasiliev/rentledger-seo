# Form W-7 for Canadian Landlords: Alaska Rental Property Tax Guide

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, officially the **Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)**, is your entry point to the US federal tax system if you're a Canadian resident without a Social Security Number (SSN). An ITIN is a nine-digit taxpayer identification number formatted as ###-##-#### that the IRS issues to non-residents who have US tax filing obligations.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN isn't optional—it's essential. Whether you own a single cabin in Juneau or multiple residential units in Anchorage, the IRS requires you to file Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) and obtain an ITIN to report rental income, claim deductions, and satisfy **FIRPTA** (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding requirements.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Alaska Landlords

Alaska presents a unique tax advantage for Canadian property owners: **Alaska has no state income tax**. This eliminates state-level filing requirements, but it does not eliminate your federal obligations. You will still owe US federal income tax on Alaska rental income at ordinary rates (currently 10–37% depending on income level).

**ITIN necessity in Alaska:**

1. **Federal rental reporting**: You must report rental income, mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, depreciation, and operating expenses on Form 1040-NR Schedule E (or Form 8949 if you have capital gains from a property sale).

2. **FIRPTA withholding**: If you sell Alaska rental property, the buyer's closing attorney or title company must withhold 15% of the gross sales price under FIRPTA rules. To minimize withholding or claim exemptions, you need an ITIN on file with the IRS before closing.

3. **Foreign Tax Credit coordination**: Canadian rental income is taxable in both countries. You'll report the same income on your Canadian T1 General return. An ITIN allows you to file Form 1040-NR and potentially claim a foreign tax credit (Form 1118) for Canadian income taxes paid, reducing double taxation.

4. **Treaty benefits under Article VI**: The **Canada-US Income and Franchise Taxes Treaty** (1980, as amended) allows you to claim tax treaty benefits that may reduce withholding rates or allow deductions not otherwise available to non-residents. An ITIN is prerequisite to claiming these benefits.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must apply for an ITIN if you:

- Are a Canadian citizen or resident (non-US citizen) without a US SSN
- Own rental property in Alaska generating net income or loss
- Plan to file Form 1040-NR for US tax years in which you hold the property
- Have a US filing obligation because of FIRPTA withholding or rental income reporting
- Need to provide an ITIN to your title company or closing attorney for a property sale

You do **not** need an ITIN if:

- You own Alaska property but have zero rental income and no US source income
- Your only US activity is passive investment without rental activity
- You hold property exclusively through a US entity (C-corporation or LLC) that files its own returns

## Step-by-Step Instructions for Completing Form W-7

### **Step 1: Gather Required Documentation**

Before completing Form W-7, collect:

- Valid Canadian passport (front and back copies)
- Proof of Canadian residence (provincial driver's license, utility bill from Canada dated within 12 months, or provincial health card)
- Proof of US tax filing obligation (documentation showing Alaska rental property ownership: deed, property tax statement, or lease agreement)
- Form 1040-NR completed for the relevant tax year (filed with Form W-7, or filed separately first)

### **Step 2: Complete Form W-7 (Current Year Version)**

Form W-7 is a two-page IRS form. Complete it as follows:

**Part I—Personal Information:**
- Line 1a: Enter your full name as shown on your Canadian passport
- Line 1b: Enter your date of birth
- Line 1c: Select "Individual" under entity type
- Line 2: Enter your Canadian mailing address (do not use a US address unless you have one)
- Line 3: Enter your Canadian phone number or US contact if applicable
- Line 4: Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Line 5: Leave blank (this field is for existing ITIN holders)

**Part II—Type of ITIN Application:**
- Check the appropriate box based on your reason:
  - Box "a" (Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return) — **This is your box.** Check it.
  - Provide your Form 1040-NR filing status (Single, Married Filing Jointly, etc.)

**Part III—Dependents:**
- Leave blank unless you claim dependents on Form 1040-NR (unlikely if you're a non-resident alien filing under that form type)

**Part IV—Certification:**
- Sign and date the form
- Include your passport number

### **Step 3: Gather Supporting Schedules**

Attach to Form W-7:

- **Completed Form 1040-NR** for the tax year in which you have Alaska rental income
- **Schedule E** (Supplemental Income or Loss) detailing:
  - Physical address of the Alaska rental property
  - Rental income for the tax year
  - Deductible expenses (property tax, mortgage interest, repairs, utilities, insurance, depreciation)
  - Net rental profit or loss
- **Proof of US tax filing obligation**: Copy of your Alaska property deed or recent property tax bill showing your ownership

### **Step 4: Authenticate Your Identity**

The IRS requires identity verification. You have three options:

1. **In-person at an IRS office**: Bring original documents to an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center. (Anchorage has an IRS office; Juneau and Fairbanks do not—remote filers may need to travel or use option 2/3.)

2. **Authorized third-party representative**: Have a US tax professional (CPA, tax attorney, or IRS Enrolled Agent in the US) submit Form 8821 (Tax Information Authorization) with your Form W-7.

3. **IRS video appointment** (new option): The IRS now offers virtual identity verification appointments. Schedule through IRS.gov.

**Most practical for Canadian landlords**: Work with a US tax professional who can submit Form W-7 with proper documentation on your behalf and conduct video verification.

### **Step 5: File Form W-7**

**File with Form 1040-NR** by the US tax deadline:

- **April 15** (or June 15 with Form 4868 extension) for tax year 2024
- Mail to: **IRS, ITIN Processing Unit, Austin, TX 73301**

Alternatively, if you have an existing US tax professional, they may file Form W-7 with your return electronically if they participate in ITIN e-filing (most large CPA firms do).

**Processing time**: 4–6 weeks after receipt. The IRS will mail your ITIN number to your Canadian address on Form W-7-C (ITIN Assignment Notice).

## Alaska-Specific Considerations

### **No State Tax but Federal Obligation Remains**

Alaska's lack of state income tax simplifies your filing—you won't file an Alaska state return. However, your federal obligation under FIRPTA and Form 1040-NR is unchanged. Budget for federal tax liability only.

**Effective property tax in Alaska**: 1.19% statewide average. This is deductible on your Form 1040-NR Schedule E as a rental expense.

### **FIRPTA Withholding on Alaska Property Sales**

If you sell Alaska rental property, the buyer's closing agent must withhold 15% of the gross sales price (or up to 35% if you lack an ITIN). Once you obtain an ITIN and file Form 8288-B (U.S. Withholding Tax Return for Disposition by Foreign Person of US Real Property Interests), you can reduce withholding or claim a refund of excess withholding.

Provide your ITIN to the title company **before closing** to avoid maximum withholding.

### **Remote Filing Challenge**

Alaska is geographically remote. If in-person IRS verification is required and you cannot travel to Anchorage, explicitly request video appointment verification when you mail Form W-7.
