# Form W-7 (ITIN Application) for Canadian Landlords with Colorado Rental Property

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7, officially titled "Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number," is the application form you must file with the US Internal Revenue Service (IRS) to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted like a Social Security Number (SSN) but exclusively for individuals who do not have and are ineligible for a US SSN but have a US tax filing requirement.

As a Canadian non-resident alien landlord with US rental property, an ITIN becomes essential when you are required to file US federal income tax returns, comply with Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) rules, or satisfy state tax obligations.

## Why Canadian Landlords Need an ITIN in Colorado

### Federal Tax Requirement
If you own rental property in Colorado, you must file a US federal Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) or Form 1040-NR-EZ (if eligible) for any tax year in which you have US-source income. Rental income is classified as US-source income and is subject to federal taxation.

### Colorado State Income Tax
Colorado imposes a flat state income tax of **4.4%** on rental income earned by non-residents. You must file a Colorado state return (Form 104-N, the nonresident income tax return) reporting rental revenue, deductible expenses, and calculating Colorado tax liability. To complete the Colorado state return, you will need either an SSN or an ITIN.

### FIRPTA Withholding
If you sell Colorado rental property, the buyer (or their agent) is required under FIRPTA to withhold 15% of the gross sale price as a federal tax deposit. To claim a refund or file a final return after the sale, you must have an ITIN and file Form 8288-B (Statement of US Real Property Gain or Loss) along with Form 1040-NR.

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Benefit
The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article XXII) allows Canadian residents to claim foreign tax credits on their Canadian T1 return for US taxes paid. Having an ITIN ensures your US tax filings are properly recorded and creditable against Canadian federal tax.

## Colorado Property Tax Considerations

Colorado's average effective property tax rate is **0.51%**, though rates vary by county and municipality. Property tax is deductible on US Form Schedule E (Supplemental Income and Loss) when calculating net rental income for federal purposes. You do not need an ITIN specifically for property tax assessment (local assessor's offices accept alternative identification), but you will need one for income tax filing.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if:

- You are a non-resident alien (as defined by the IRS)
- You have no US Social Security Number and are ineligible to obtain one
- You have a US tax filing requirement (e.g., rental income from Colorado property)
- You are subject to FIRPTA withholding on a planned property sale
- Your US tax return requires an ITIN (federal, state, or both)

**You do not need an ITIN** if you are a US citizen, US permanent resident (green card holder), or lawful US resident alien with employment authorization.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Completing Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before submitting Form W-7, collect:

- **Valid passport** (Canadian passport is acceptable proof of identity)
- **Proof of Canadian residence** (driver's license, utility bill, property tax notice, or municipal lease agreement dated within 12 months)
- **Evidence of US tax filing requirement** (rental property deed, property tax bill, or lease agreement showing your ownership of Colorado property)
- **Completed Form W-7** (printed and signed)
- **Copy of your 1040-NR** (if filing W-7 with your tax return) or a statement explaining your tax filing requirement

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately

**Line 1a: Your Name**  
Enter your full legal name as it appears on your Canadian passport.

**Line 1b: Country of Citizenship**  
Enter "Canada."

**Line 2: Mailing Address in the United States**  
Provide your Colorado property address or the address of a US representative (accountant, tax professional, or property manager) who can receive IRS correspondence. This is critical for ITIN approval and future IRS notices.

**Line 3: Country of Residence**  
Enter "Canada."

**Line 5: Date of Birth**  
Enter in MM/DD/YYYY format (US convention).

**Line 6: Reason You Are Applying for an ITIN**  
Select the applicable box:
- **Box a** (you are a nonresident alien required to file a US tax return) is the correct selection for most Canadian landlords with Colorado rental income.

**Line 7: Visa Type**  
If you have entered the US on a visitor visa (such as a B-1 tourist visa), enter the visa code. If you have not traveled to the US, enter "NONE." This does not affect ITIN eligibility.

**Line 8: Declaration**  
Sign and date the form. Signatures must match your passport.

### Step 3: Document Certification

**Critical requirement:** One or both of the following must accompany your Form W-7:

1. **Certified copy of your Canadian passport** (certified by an acceptable third party—see IRS acceptable certifiers list)
2. **Notarized copy of your Canadian passport** (notarized by a Canadian notary public or US-based notary)

The IRS does **not** accept uncertified or unsigned copies. Working with a Canadian accountant or cross-border tax professional can streamline this step, as they often maintain relationships with notaries and can ensure proper certification.

### Step 4: Submit Your Application

#### Filing Timeline

You have two options for timing your W-7 submission:

**Option A: File W-7 with your Form 1040-NR**  
In your first year of Colorado rental income, submit Form W-7 and Form 1040-NR together to the IRS. This is the most common approach. Send both forms and supporting documents to:

```
Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operations
Austin, TX 73301
USA
```

**Option B: File W-7 Before Filing 1040-NR**  
If you expect FIRPTA withholding or want to establish your ITIN before tax return season, submit W-7 separately 4–6 weeks before filing your 1040-NR. This reduces processing delays at tax return time.

#### Processing Time

The IRS typically processes Form W-7 within **4–6 weeks** if the application is complete and all supporting documents are included. Incomplete applications are rejected and returned with an explanation.

#### Do Not Use ITIN Until Approved

Do not use a proposed ITIN number on tax returns or financial documents until the IRS formally approves your application. Using an unapproved ITIN can result in return rejection and processing delays.

### Step 5: Track Your Application

After submitting Form W-7, request an IRS transcript or letter confirming your ITIN issuance. You can call the IRS ITIN hotline at **1-866-999-0002** (available Monday–Friday, 7 AM–7 PM US Eastern Time) or create an account on IRS.gov to monitor your application status.

## Colorado-Specific Considerations

### State Return Filing Requirement

Colorado requires **all non-residents with Colorado-source income** to file Form 104-N. Your ITIN must appear on this return. Colorado does not accept federal ITIN refusal or non-issuance as a reason to defer filing. File Form 104-N by **April 15** (or extended due date of June 15 if you file Form 4868 for a federal extension).

### County Recorder and Property Transfer

When you acquire Colorado rental property, you may need to disclose your ITIN to the county recorder's office during deed recording. Colorado County Assessors will request identification for property tax assessment. An ITIN is acceptable identification; however, some county offices may also request your full name and date of birth.

### Withholding and FIRPTA

If you sell your Colorado property, the buyer's closing agent must withhold 15% of the gross sale price under FIRPTA and remit it to the IRS using **Form 8288** (US Withholding Tax Return for Dispositions by Foreign Persons of US Real Property Interests). To claim a refund or negotiate a reduced withholding rate, you must file **Form 8288-B** with your Form 1040-NR within **two years**
