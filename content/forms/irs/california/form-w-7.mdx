# Form W-7: ITIN Guide for Canadian Landlords with California Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the official IRS application to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number issued by the U.S. Internal Revenue Service to individuals who are not eligible for a Social Security Number but are required to file U.S. tax returns or comply with U.S. tax withholding rules.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN is the U.S. tax identification number that replaces a Social Security Number when filing Form 1040-NR (Non-resident Alien Income Tax Return) and when reporting California rental income to state authorities.

## How the ITIN Applies to Canadian Landlords in California

### Why You Need an ITIN

As a Canadian resident owning rental property in California, you are classified as a **non-resident alien** for U.S. federal tax purposes. The IRS requires you to:

1. **File Form 1040-NR** annually to report U.S. source rental income
2. **Obtain an ITIN** to comply with tax filing requirements
3. **Comply with FIRPTA withholding rules** if you sell the property
4. **File California Form 540-NR** (Non-resident Income Tax Return) to report California-source rental income

The ITIN becomes your unique U.S. tax identifier for all these filings.

### California's Tax Impact on Your Rental Income

California taxes **all rental income** derived from California real estate, regardless of whether you are a resident or non-resident. Key points:

- **California tax rate**: California's top marginal rate reaches **13.3%** on high-income earners. Your effective rate depends on your total income and California's progressive tax brackets.
- **Non-resident filing requirement**: You must file **California Form 540-NR** and report 100% of California-source rental income.
- **Withholding requirement**: California may require the tenant's property manager or payor to withhold California income tax from rental payments under California's non-resident withholding rules.

Without an ITIN, you cannot properly file either your federal Form 1040-NR or your California Form 540-NR.

### Canada-U.S. Tax Treaty Considerations

The Canada-U.S. Income Tax Treaty (Article XIII) addresses real property income. While non-resident aliens from non-treaty countries typically face a 30% federal withholding on U.S. real property rental income, **Canadian residents benefit from reduced withholding rates** under the treaty—typically 25% on real property gains and generally no withholding on ongoing rental income (subject to exceptions).

To claim treaty benefits, you will need your ITIN on your IRS Form W-8BEN (Certificate of Foreign Status of Beneficial Owner).

## Who Files Form W-7?

You should file Form W-7 if you:

- Are a non-U.S. citizen and non-resident alien
- Own rental property in California (or any U.S. state)
- Do not have a Social Security Number
- Need a U.S. tax identification number to file Form 1040-NR or Form 540-NR
- Are subject to FIRPTA withholding (if selling the property)
- Want to claim treaty benefits using Form W-8BEN

**Timeline**: You should apply for your ITIN **before** filing your first Form 1040-NR. If you are subject to FIRPTA withholding (property sale), obtain your ITIN immediately before the sale closes.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, assemble the following:

- Your valid passport (required primary document)
- Proof of Canadian residency (utility bill, bank statement, property tax assessment, dated within 12 months)
- Documentation of your U.S. tax filing requirement (e.g., rental property ownership, lease agreement, property deed)
- A copy of your proposed U.S. tax return (Form 1040-NR) or other documentation of U.S. tax obligation

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Itself

**Part I: Personal Information**
- Enter your full legal name (as it appears on your passport)
- Enter your date of birth and country of birth
- Provide your Canadian mailing address (if residing in Canada)

**Part II: Reason for Applying**
- Check box **c**: "U.S. resident alien filing a tax return"
- Alternatively, check the box corresponding to your specific filing requirement (e.g., FIRPTA withholding)
- Provide your **ITIN-needed date** (the date by which you need the number)

**Part III: U.S. Residency Status**
- As a non-resident alien, you are not a U.S. resident for tax purposes
- Indicate your Canadian residency status

**Part IV: Declaration**
- Sign and date the form
- Have your signature **notarized** or certified by an authorized representative (see "Submission Methods" below)

### Step 3: Prepare Required Attachments

Attach originals or **certified copies** of:
- Your passport (photo ID page)
- Proof of Canadian residency
- A copy of your proposed 2024 Form 1040-NR or other U.S. tax document

### Step 4: Submit Form W-7

**Three Submission Options**:

1. **Mail to IRS**: Send your completed W-7 and supporting documents to the IRS ITIN Unit (address varies by location; check Form W-7 instructions for your region). Processing takes 6–12 weeks by mail.

2. **In-Person Appointment**: Attend an IRS ITIN Appointment at a participating IRS office or Acceptance Agent location in the U.S. You must have your documents certified by a Certified Public Accountant (CPA), enrolled agent, or attorney, or you can appear in person.

3. **Through a U.S. Tax Professional**: A CPA, tax attorney, or Enrolled Agent can file Form W-7 on your behalf as a "Authorized Representative." This is often the most practical option for Canadian landlords, as the representative can certify documents and expedite processing.

**Processing Time**: 6–12 weeks if mailed; same-day processing if completed in-person through an IRS office.

## California-Specific Considerations

### California Form 540-NR and ITIN Requirement

You must file **California Form 540-NR** (Non-resident Income Tax Return) with the **California Franchise Tax Board (FTB)** and report:

- All California-source rental income (line 8a, Form 540-NR)
- California rental property taxes (deductible under California law)
- Depreciation, mortgage interest, repairs, and other deductions

You **must provide your ITIN** on line 4 of Form 540-NR. The FTB will not process your return without a valid ITIN.

### California Non-Resident Withholding (Form 592-B)

If your property manager or tenant payor is aware that you are non-resident, California may impose **non-resident rental income withholding**. The payor may be required to withhold a portion of rental income. Withholding rates are set under California Revenue and Taxation Code Section 18662. Your ITIN will be referenced on the **Form 592-B** (Statement by a Non-Resident Taxpayer Claiming Withholding Credits) to claim credit for withholding paid.

### Property Tax and ITIN

California property taxes are assessed at an effective rate of approximately **0.76%** of assessed value. Your property tax bills will not require an ITIN; however, your ITIN is needed to report deductible property taxes on Form 540-NR.

### California's "Unruh Civil Rights Act"

California law prohibits discrimination based on citizenship status. You have the same property owner rights as U.S. citizens, including the right to obtain an ITIN for tax compliance.
