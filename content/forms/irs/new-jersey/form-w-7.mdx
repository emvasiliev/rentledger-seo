# Form W-7: ITIN Guide for Canadian Landlords with New Jersey Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS application used by non-resident aliens to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number that functions like a Social Security Number (SSN) for US tax purposes, but exclusively for individuals who are not eligible for an SSN.

As a Canadian citizen without a US SSN, you cannot file US federal tax returns or comply with US tax withholding obligations without an ITIN. The IRS issues ITINs primarily to:

- Non-resident aliens filing US tax returns
- Dependents of US citizens or residents
- Spouses of US citizens or residents
- Individuals subject to FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in New Jersey

When you own rental property in New Jersey, you have triggered US tax residency requirements that make an ITIN mandatory. Here's why:

**Federal Requirement**: All rental income from New Jersey property must be reported on Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return). The IRS will not process a 1040-NR without a valid ITIN or SSN.

**New Jersey State Requirement**: New Jersey requires non-resident state income tax returns (NJ-1040) on rental income. New Jersey's state income tax on rental income is taxed at rates up to **10.75%**, depending on your total income bracket. The state of New Jersey does not accept ITIN applications directly; however, your federal ITIN will satisfy New Jersey's identification requirements for state return filing.

**FIRPTA Withholding**: If you sell your New Jersey rental property, the buyer's closing agent must withhold **15% of the gross sales price** under FIRPTA rules (or up to 21% if certain conditions apply). To facilitate this withholding and claim any refund of excess tax, you must provide your ITIN to the closing agent.

**Property Tax Implications**: While New Jersey's average effective property tax rate of **2.49%** is relatively lower than neighboring states, property tax bills are still issued in the property owner's name. Some municipalities require a US tax ID or ITIN for property record purposes.

## Who Files Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

- A Canadian resident (or non-US resident) who owns rental property in New Jersey
- Unable to obtain a US Social Security Number
- Required to file Form 1040-NR to report rental income, royalties, or capital gains
- Subject to FIRPTA withholding on a property sale

You do **not** file Form W-7 if you are a US citizen or green card holder—those individuals must apply for an SSN through the Social Security Administration instead.

## Step-by-Step: Completing Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, collect:

- **Passport or travel document**: Your Canadian passport is the primary acceptable identification
- **Tax return documentation**: Proof of your rental income (lease agreements, property tax bills, or a draft 1040-NR)
- **Property ownership proof**: New Jersey deed, property tax bill, or mortgage statement showing your name
- **Completed IRS Form W-7**: Available at irs.gov

### Step 2: Complete Part I – Applicant Information

Enter your legal name exactly as it appears on your Canadian passport. Include your Canadian address. If you have a US address (property address or attorney's office), you may enter it, but your primary residence address in Canada should be listed.

**Do not use a nickname or abbreviated name.** The IRS will match this information to your Canadian passport and subsequent US tax filings.

### Step 3: Complete Part II – Type of Applicant

Select the box that applies to your situation:

- **Box (c)**: "Nonresident alien individual" — This is the correct selection for a Canadian rental property owner
- Provide your country of citizenship: Canada

### Step 4: Complete Part III – Reason for Application

Check the box(es) that apply:

- **"Filing a US tax return"**: Select this if you are filing Form 1040-NR for rental income
- **"Providing ITINs for dependents or spouses"**: Leave blank (not applicable for property owners)
- **"Other"**: Specify FIRPTA withholding if applicable

### Step 5: Certify and Sign

Sign and date the form in black ink. Canadian landlords filing from Canada may use the "Authorizing agent" section to have a US tax representative (CPA or tax attorney) sign on their behalf. This is common for cross-border filers.

## New Jersey-Specific Considerations

### State Income Tax Filing Alignment

New Jersey will not recognize an ITIN application directly, but once you receive your ITIN from the IRS, use it on your **NJ-1040 Nonresident Return**. New Jersey's Division of Taxation coordinates with federal filings; your federal ITIN is sufficient for state purposes.

Rental income reported on your Form 1040-NR will flow to your New Jersey state return. The state taxes:

- Gross rental income minus allowed deductions (mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, depreciation)
- Tax rate: Up to 10.75% depending on income level
- New Jersey does **not** recognize the Canada-US Tax Treaty exemptions for state purposes; you will owe full state tax

### Property Tax Assessments and ITIN

New Jersey municipalities issue property tax bills to the owner of record. While not all municipalities mandate a US tax ID, some may request ITIN information during assessment appeals or property transfers. Having your ITIN assigned before refinancing or contesting property taxes streamlines these processes.

### FIRPTA and Escrow in New Jersey Closings

If you sell your New Jersey rental property, the closing agent (attorney or title company) must withhold FIRPTA tax. Provide your ITIN to the closing agent at least **10 days before closing**. New Jersey does not have an additional state-level withholding requirement for non-resident property sales, but federal FIRPTA rules apply uniformly.

## Completing Form 1040-NR: The Companion Filing

Your ITIN is obtained to file **Form 1040-NR, US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return**, not the standard Form 1040. Key differences:

- Schedule NEC (Nonresident Earned Income Credit) may not apply
- Capital gains are reported at a flat 30% rate (unless reduced by treaty)
- Deductions are limited; the standard deduction does not apply
- The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article XIII) permits a reduced withholding rate on capital gains if you meet specific tests

Simultaneously, you will file your **Canadian T1 General** (personal income tax return) with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA). You can claim the US taxes paid as a **foreign tax credit** on your Canadian return, reducing double taxation.

## Common Mistakes Canadian Landlords Make

1. **Using a nickname or anglicized name**: The IRS will reject Form W-7 if your legal name doesn't match your passport exactly. Use your full legal name as registered with Canada CRA.

2. **Mailing Form W-7 without an Identity Verification Process**: The IRS may request certified copies of your passport. Use Form W-7 Schedule Request (if the IRS contacts you) or include a certified photocopy of your Canadian passport with your mailed submission.

3. **Filing Form 1040-NR before receiving the ITIN**: The IRS will delay both applications. Obtain your ITIN **first**, then file the 1040-NR using your newly assigned ITIN.

4. **Neglecting New Jersey state return requirements**: Filing federal Form 1040-NR does not automatically satisfy New Jersey state obligations. File both federal and state returns even if tax owed is minimal.

5. **Failing to notify property closing agents of ITIN**: If selling, provide your ITIN to the closing attorney at least 10 days prior to closing to avoid delays.

## Key Deadlines for Canadian Landlords

- **Form W-7 filing**: File with your first 1040-NR or before a property sale occurs
- **Form 1040-NR deadline**: **June 15** (extended from April 15) for non-resident aliens
- **New Jersey NJ-1040 deadline**: **June 15** (same as federal for non-residents)
- **FIRPTA documentation**: Provide ITIN to closing agent 10 days before property sale closing
- **Foreign Tax Credit (Canadian T1)**: File by **June 15** (Canadian tax year ends December 31)
