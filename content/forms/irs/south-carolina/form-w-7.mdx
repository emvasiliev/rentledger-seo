# Form W-7 for Canadian Landlords with South Carolina Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN), is the IRS form you use to request a unique nine-digit tax identification number when you're a non-resident alien without a US Social Security Number. The ITIN serves the same purpose as an SSN for tax filing—it identifies you to the IRS for reporting income, filing tax returns, and complying with US tax withholding obligations.

As a Canadian landlord earning rental income from South Carolina property, you are classified as a non-resident alien for US federal tax purposes. This classification triggers two critical requirements: (1) filing an annual Form 1040-NR (Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return), and (2) complying with FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding rules, which require 15% withholding when your property is sold. Both requirements necessitate an ITIN.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Your South Carolina Rental Situation

**Federal taxation context**

South Carolina does not impose state income tax on non-residents—it only taxes residents. However, you are still subject to federal US income tax on your rental income. The IRS requires all non-resident aliens with US-source income to obtain an ITIN and file Form 1040-NR annually by June 15 (the automatic filing deadline for non-residents).

**Why ITIN is essential in your situation**

1. **Annual 1040-NR filing**: Your South Carolina rental income (gross rents minus allowable deductions) must be reported to the IRS. Without an ITIN, you cannot file this return.

2. **FIRPTA compliance**: If you sell your South Carolina property, a buyer or their title company is required to withhold 15% of the sale price and remit it to the IRS (or potentially 10% under certain treaty provisions). This withholding is credited against your final US tax liability. The IRS processes this withholding using your ITIN.

3. **Establishing US tax residency file**: Even if you expect no net US tax owing due to deductions or losses, filing with an ITIN establishes your compliance history with the IRS, which simplifies future transactions and reduces audit risk.

**Treaty considerations**

Under Article XXII of the Canada-US Tax Treaty, you are entitled to relief from double taxation. The treaty allows Canadian residents to claim a foreign tax credit on their Canadian T1 return for US taxes paid. However, you must first file a US 1040-NR to establish the US tax liability you're crediting in Canada. An ITIN is the prerequisite to filing this return and obtaining proof of US tax paid (necessary for your Canadian CRA filings).

## Who Must File Form W-7 in Your Circumstances

You file Form W-7 if you meet all of the following:

- You are a Canadian citizen or permanent resident (non-US citizen)
- You have US-source income (rental income from your South Carolina property qualifies)
- You do not have and are not eligible for a US Social Security Number
- You have a federal income tax filing requirement or need an ITIN for FIRPTA purposes

Most Canadian landlords with US rental property fall into this category. You do not need to be physically present in the US to file Form W-7; the process is entirely mail-based or, in limited cases, in-person at an IRS office.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete and File Form W-7

**Step 1: Obtain and review the form**

Download Form W-7 and its instructions from the IRS website (irs.gov). The form is typically 1–2 pages; the instructions are more detailed and essential reading.

**Step 2: Complete Form W-7 accurately**

Key fields for your situation:

- **Line 1**: Check box (c)—"Individual taxpayer seeking to file a US tax return"
- **Lines 2–4**: Enter your full legal name, date of birth (MMDDYYYY), and country of citizenship (Canada)
- **Line 5**: Enter your Canadian mailing address
- **Line 6**: Enter your Canadian phone number
- **Line 7**: Select your visa status or immigration status. As a Canadian, mark "Visa status not required" or leave blank (you are not immigrating; you are a non-resident)
- **Line 8**: Describe your US income source—write "Rental income from residential property, South Carolina"
- **Line 9**: Check the box indicating you are applying for an ITIN to file a US tax return (not for employment)

**Step 3: Prepare required documentation**

The IRS requires proof of identity and proof of residency:

- **Proof of identity**: Certified copy of your Canadian passport or driver's license (must be certified by a government agency or authorized representative)
- **Proof of residency**: A recent utility bill, bank statement, or lease agreement showing your Canadian address and dated within the past 12 months

Alternatively, you can have these documents certified by a US consulate or certified accountant to satisfy the IRS's verification requirements.

**Step 4: Complete Form 1040-NR (concurrently)**

Most importantly, you **must file Form W-7 with your first Form 1040-NR** or submit them concurrently. Do not file Form W-7 in isolation; the IRS reviews it in the context of your income filing requirement. Your 1040-NR schedules (Schedule E for rental income, Schedule A for deductions if applicable) demonstrate why you need the ITIN.

**Step 5: Mail your complete application**

Submit the following package by mail to the IRS address specified in the Form W-7 instructions (typically an IRS ITIN unit):

- Original or certified Form W-7 (not a photocopy of the form itself, but certified supporting documents)
- Certified copies of proof of identity and proof of residency
- Form 1040-NR and all schedules (Schedule E, Schedule A, Schedule C if applicable)
- Payment for any US tax owing (if applicable)

Do not email or fax these documents to the IRS; ITIN applications must arrive by mail.

**Step 6: Allow processing time**

The IRS typically processes ITIN applications within 6–8 weeks from receipt. You will receive correspondence confirming your ITIN assignment. Keep this letter for your records and future tax filings.

## South Carolina-Specific Considerations

**State income tax filing**

While South Carolina does not impose income tax on non-residents, a critical distinction exists: if you are deemed a South Carolina resident for state purposes (e.g., you spend more than 183 days in SC annually or own property and perform maintenance work), you may owe South Carolina state income tax at the rate of 6.5% on your rental income. This would require a South Carolina Form SC 1040 filed concurrently with your federal 1040-NR.

Most Canadian landlords who own investment property in SC but reside in Canada do not trigger SC residency; however, verify your circumstances with a cross-border tax professional to confirm.

**Property tax withholding on sale**

South Carolina property tax is assessed at an effective statewide rate of 0.57%, but withholding on a non-resident's property sale is a federal FIRPTA matter, not a state matter. When you sell your SC property, the buyer's attorney or title company will withhold 15% of the sales proceeds and remit to the IRS using your ITIN. Ensure your ITIN is registered with the IRS before listing your property for sale to prevent delays in the sale closing process.

**Atlantic Canadian landlord trend**

South Carolina is a popular investment destination for Atlantic Canadian retirees and investors from Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, and Prince Edward Island due to its warm climate and affordable property. If you fit this profile, ensure your ITIN application is completed well in advance of any sale or material income event. The IRS processes high volumes of ITIN applications during tax season (January–April), so file earlier in the year if possible.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Filing Form W-7 without Form 1040-NR**: The IRS will reject standalone W-7 applications. Always file them together.

2. **Submitting non-certified identity documents**: Photocopies of your passport or driver's license alone are insufficient. Obtain certified copies from a Canadian government office or have them certified by a US consulate.

3. **Providing a US address instead of Canadian address**: Your mailing address on W-7 must be your Canadian address. The IRS will mail your ITIN assignment letter to this address.

4. **Missing South Carolina state tax obligations**: If you're uncertain whether you owe South Carolina state income tax, consult a professional. Filing Form 1040-NR without addressing potential SC state filing creates compliance gaps.
