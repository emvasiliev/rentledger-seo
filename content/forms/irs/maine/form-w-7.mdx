# Form W-7: ITIN Application Guide for Canadian Landlords with Maine Rental Property

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the official IRS application used to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number that functions similarly to a Social Security Number (SSN) for tax purposes, but is issued to individuals who are not eligible for an SSN.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves as your US taxpayer identification number when filing federal income tax returns, state returns, and when managing FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding obligations on US rental property.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Maine

### The Maine Rental Income Scenario

As a Canadian resident owning rental property in Maine, you face a multi-layered tax filing obligation:

- **Federal level**: You must file Form 1040-NR (U.S. Non-resident Alien Income Tax Return) to report Maine rental income to the IRS
- **State level**: Maine requires non-residents to file Form ME 1040NR (Maine Non-resident Income Tax Return) and pay state income tax at a rate of **7.15%** on net rental income
- **Property tax**: Maine's average effective property tax rate is **1.36%**, applied annually by municipal assessors

Without an ITIN, you cannot file any of these returns legally. The IRS will not process a 1040-NR without a valid taxpayer identification number.

### FIRPTA Compliance

If you sell or dispose of Maine rental property, you also trigger FIRPTA withholding requirements. When a US buyer purchases your property, they (or their agent) must withhold **15% of the gross sale price** and remit it to the IRS as estimated tax payment on your behalf. To report this withholding and claim credits on your return, you must have an ITIN.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You should file Form W-7 if you are:

- A Canadian citizen or permanent resident with no US SSN
- Earning rental income from Maine property
- Required to file US federal tax returns (Form 1040-NR) or state returns (Maine Form ME 1040NR)
- Selling or subject to FIRPTA withholding on a Maine property sale
- Unable to obtain a US SSN due to visa or immigration status limitations

You do **not** need an ITIN if you hold a valid US work visa (H-1B, TN, L-1) that makes you eligible for an SSN, though you should consult with a cross-border tax specialist to confirm your eligibility status.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing W-7, assemble:

- Your valid Canadian passport or travel document
- Proof of Canadian residency (utility bill, property tax assessment, or driver's license dated within 3 years)
- Proof of your Maine rental property ownership (deed, mortgage statement, or property tax bill)
- Documentation of US tax filing requirement (lease agreement, property management statements, or evidence of FIRPTA sale)
- Your complete legal name as it appears on your passport

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7, Section 1 (Personal Information)

- **Line 1a-1c**: Enter your full legal name, date of birth, and country of citizenship (Canada)
- **Line 2**: Enter your Canadian mailing address
- **Line 3**: Leave blank if you have never had an SSN; check "c" if you previously applied for an SSN but were denied
- **Line 4**: Select your immigration status; most Canadian landlords select "Nonresident alien" or "Other"

### Step 3: Complete Section 2 (Type of ITIN Applicant)

Check the box that applies to your situation:

- **Box a**: If filing a US tax return (most common for Maine landlords) — this applies to Form 1040-NR filers
- **Box b**: If required by US tax law to obtain an ITIN for other purposes
- **Box c**: If sponsoring a dependent or claiming dependent benefits

For Maine rental income, **Box a** is typically your answer.

### Step 4: Complete Section 3 (Visa Type and Immigration Status)

- Indicate that you are a Canadian resident alien (non-citizen)
- If applicable, note that you are seeking the ITIN for tax treaty purposes under Article IV of the Canada-US Income Tax Treaty
- Provide your visa type (or "None" if you are not in the US)

### Step 5: Attestation and Signature

- Review the declaration carefully; you are attesting under penalty of perjury that the information is true and correct
- Sign and date the form
- Ensure your signature matches your passport signature

### Step 6: Gather Originals and Certification

The IRS requires **certified copies** of your identity and residency documents. You have two filing options:

**Option A: Certified by the IRS Acceptance Agent** (Recommended for most Canadian landlords)
- Use an IRS-authorized acceptance agent (often found at US consulates, banks, or tax firms with locations in Canada)
- The agent reviews your originals and certifies copies, then submits your application to the IRS
- Processing time: 2–4 weeks after submission

**Option B: Certified by a US Official Abroad**
- Obtain certification from a US consulate or embassy in Canada
- Mail the certified documents directly to the IRS ITIN Processing Unit in Philadelphia
- Processing time: 6–8 weeks

**Option C: Domestic Filing (if in the US)**
- If you are temporarily in the US, you can apply in person at an IRS office
- Bring originals; an IRS employee will certify the copies
- Immediate assistance available

## Maine-Specific Considerations

### Maine State Tax Filing Requirements

Maine does not recognize federal ITIN status as sufficient for state tax purposes. When you file your Maine Form ME 1040NR:

- You must provide your **federal ITIN** as your Maine tax identification number
- Maine's Department of Revenue will cross-reference your federal ITIN with federal filings
- Failure to file the Maine return can result in penalties of **5–25%** of unpaid tax, plus interest at the federal short-term rate plus 3%

### Timeline Coordination

If you are in your **first tax year** owning Maine rental property:

1. **Year 1 (Property acquisition year)**: Apply for ITIN immediately upon taking title
2. **By April 15 of Year 2**: File both federal (1040-NR) and Maine (ME 1040NR) returns
3. Claim a 6-month extension (Form 4868) if needed, extending your deadline to October 15

### Atlantic Canadian Proximity and Professional Help

Maine is popular with landlords from Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, and Prince Edward Island due to geographic proximity. Many Canadian tax professionals and accountants who specialize in cross-border taxation have established relationships with Maine property accountants and can facilitate ITIN applications. Consider engaging a cross-border specialist early.

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Benefits

Under Article IV of the Canada-US Income Tax Treaty, you are entitled to claim a foreign tax credit on your Canadian T1 return for Maine state income taxes paid. When you file your Canadian return with CRA:

- Report your gross Maine rental income
- Claim a foreign tax credit for the 7.15% Maine state tax paid
- Claim any federal US tax paid on your 1040-NR
- The federal foreign tax credit is available on Form 2116 (or Schedule 1 for recent tax years)

This prevents double taxation on the same income.

## Common Mistakes When Filing Form W-7

**Mistake 1: Incomplete Documentation**
Many first-time applicants submit the W-7 without certified copies of their passport or proof of residency. The IRS will reject the application and return it, delaying your ITIN by 6–8 weeks.

**Mistake 2: Signature Mismatch**
If your W-7 signature does not reasonably match your passport signature, the IRS may reject the application as a security precaution.

**Mistake 3: Omitting Proof of US Tax Obligation**
Failing to include documentation showing why you need the ITIN (e.g., a lease agreement, property deed, or property management statement) can slow processing. Clearly label this documentation.

**Mistake 4: Incorrect Address Format**
The IRS processes ITIN applications internationally and expects precise address formatting. Write your full Canadian address on Section 1, including postal code.
