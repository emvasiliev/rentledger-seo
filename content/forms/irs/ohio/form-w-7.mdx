# Form W-7: ITIN Guide for Canadian Landlords with Ohio Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is an IRS application that assigns a unique nine-digit tax identification number—an **Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)**—to individuals who cannot obtain a Social Security Number (SSN) but have a US tax filing requirement.

An ITIN functions identically to an SSN for US federal tax purposes. It appears on your US tax returns, allows the IRS to track your tax account, and is essential for filing non-resident alien returns and satisfying FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding obligations.

## Why Canadian Landlords Need an ITIN

As a Canadian citizen or permanent resident owning rental property in Ohio, you are a **non-resident alien** for US tax purposes. This classification triggers multiple US tax obligations:

1. **Federal income tax filing**: You must file Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) annually to report rental income and expenses
2. **State income tax filing**: Ohio requires non-resident state returns (Form IT 1040-NR) for rental income
3. **FIRPTA compliance**: When you eventually sell your Ohio property, a buyer's agent or closing attorney must withhold 15% of the sales price (or potentially less under certain conditions) as FIRPTA withholding. This withholding is credited against your final US tax liability and requires an ITIN to process

Without an ITIN, you cannot file US tax returns, and buyers cannot legally complete property transactions without FIRPTA clearance.

## How It Applies Specifically in Ohio

Ohio presents a multi-layer tax environment for non-resident landlords:

**Federal taxation**: Rental income is subject to federal income tax at rates up to 37% (depending on your overall income). You claim deductions for mortgage interest, property taxes, maintenance, property management fees, and depreciation on Schedule E and Form 1040-NR.

**Ohio state taxation**: Ohio imposes a **3.99% flat tax** on all income, including rental income from Ohio property. Non-residents file Ohio Form IT 1040-NR specifically for out-of-state income sources. This state tax is *additional* to federal tax and applies to your net rental income (gross rental income minus allowable Ohio deductions).

**Property tax**: Ohio's average effective property tax rate is **1.59% of assessed property value** annually. While you cannot deduct Ohio property taxes on your US federal return if you claim the standard deduction, they are typically deductible at the state level (Ohio allows a property tax credit).

**Treaty considerations**: The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article XXIV) provides a foreign tax credit mechanism. You can claim a credit on your US 1040-NR for Ohio state income taxes paid, reducing your overall US federal liability dollar-for-dollar (up to your US tax liability). This credit prevents double taxation on the same income.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must apply for an ITIN if you:

- Are a non-resident alien with income from US sources (rental income qualifies)
- Do not have and are ineligible for a US Social Security Number
- Have a US tax filing requirement (rental property ownership satisfies this)
- Are not claiming dependency on another person's US tax return

Canadian landlords with Ohio rental property universally meet these criteria.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### **Step 1: Gather Required Documentation**

Before completing W-7, assemble:
- Your valid Canadian passport (copy)
- Your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN)
- Proof of US tax filing requirement (e.g., rental property deed, lease agreements, Form 1040-NR draft)
- Your US mailing address or US agent/representative address

### **Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately**

**Part I – Personal Information**
- Enter your full legal name as it appears on your Canadian passport
- Provide your date of birth
- Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- List your Canadian mailing address
- If you have a US mailing address, provide it in the US address field

**Part II – Type of Applicant**
Select the box that applies. Most Canadian landlords check:
- Box **b** ("Nonresident alien individual") or
- Box **c** ("Dependent of US citizen/resident alien") if applicable

For rental property purposes, "Nonresident alien individual" is standard.

**Part III – US Tax Return Information**
Indicate the type of US return you are filing:
- Form 1040-NR (Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) – mandatory for rental income
- State return: Ohio Form IT 1040-NR
- If applicable, indicate if you are filing due to FIRPTA requirements

**Part IV – Identification Document Information**
Enter details from your Canadian passport exactly as they appear:
- Document type (Passport)
- Country of issue (Canada)
- Passport number
- Issue and expiration dates

### **Step 3: Certify and Sign**

Sign and date the form. If filing on behalf of another (e.g., spouse filing jointly), the authorized representative may sign.

### **Step 4: Include Required Attachments**

Attach:
- A certified copy of your Canadian passport (notarized by a Canadian notary public or certified by a US notary using the IRS Certification of Authenticity form)
- A copy of your Form 1040-NR draft or a sample lease agreement proving US tax filing requirement
- Cover letter explaining your situation (optional but recommended)

### **Step 5: Submit via IRS ITIN Application Process**

**For Canadian residents, you have two options:**

**Option A: Mail via Certified Mail**
Send the complete W-7 package to:
```
Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operation
Austin, TX 73301
USA
```

Use certified mail with return receipt. Processing time: 120 days.

**Option B: File through an Authorized IRS Agent**
Canadian banks, tax preparers, or cross-border accounting firms (such as those specializing in US-Canada taxation) can submit W-7 applications on your behalf. Many charge a fee ($150–$300 USD) but accelerate processing.

**Option C: Virtual Interview (Limited Availability)**
The IRS offers video interviews through IRS Form W-7 online application portal for applicants in select countries. Check IRS.gov for current availability in Canada.

## Ohio-Specific Considerations

**Ohio state ITIN requirement**: While Ohio technically accepts federal ITINs for state filing purposes, you must file your Ohio Form IT 1040-NR using your new federal ITIN once assigned. Do not file Ohio state returns until your federal ITIN is received.

**Ohio withholding on rents**: Ohio does not impose separate state withholding on rental payments to non-residents. However, if you hire a US property manager, they may be required to withhold on your behalf under certain circumstances—clarify this with your property manager.

**FIRPTA withholding in Ohio**: When selling Ohio property, the buyer or closing attorney must withhold 15% of the gross sales price as FIRPTA withholding. You provide your ITIN to the closing attorney at closing. After filing your US tax return the year of sale, you claim the withholding as a credit and may receive a refund if withholding exceeds your tax liability.

**Property tax deduction**: Ohio's real estate taxes (the 1.59% effective rate) may be deductible on your Ohio state return, but federal tax treatment depends on your election. If you itemize deductions (Schedule A, Form 1040-NR), you can deduct property taxes directly. Most non-residents claim the standard deduction and cannot deduct state property taxes federally.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Filing before obtaining an ITIN**: Do not file your 1040-NR without an ITIN. The IRS will reject the return. Apply for your ITIN first (typically takes 120 days by mail).

2. **Submitting incomplete passport copies**: Copies must be certified by a notary and must include both front and back. Uncertified or partial copies cause application delays.

3. **Misidentifying applicant type**: Clearly state you are a "Nonresident alien" on Part II. Ambiguity causes processing delays.

4. **Forgetting Canadian address**: Always provide your valid Canadian mailing address so the IRS mails your ITIN notice to you.

5. **Not tracking the application**: Certified mail provides receipt confirmation. Keep this documentation for 7 years. Photograph it and store digitally.

6. **Confusing ITIN with SSN**: An ITIN is *not* a Social Security Number. Never use it to open US bank accounts or apply for US credit—
