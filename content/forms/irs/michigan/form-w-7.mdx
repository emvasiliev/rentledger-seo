# Form W-7 for Canadian Landlords Owning Michigan Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, the Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN), is used by non-resident aliens to obtain a US taxpayer identification number when they don't have a Social Security Number (SSN). An ITIN is a nine-digit number formatted as XXX-XX-XXXX that allows you to comply with US tax filing requirements, report rental income, and satisfy FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding obligations.

As a Canadian landlord earning rental income from Michigan property, you are classified as a non-resident alien for US tax purposes. The IRS requires you to have a valid ITIN to:

- File Form 1040-NR (U.S. Non-resident Alien Income Tax Return)
- Report Michigan rental income and deductions
- Comply with FIRPTA withholding when you eventually sell the property
- Establish a tax filing record with the IRS

## How Form W-7 Applies to Michigan Landlords

### Michigan Rental Income Requirements

Michigan imposes a state income tax of 4.25% on all income earned within the state, including rental income from non-residents. When you own rental property in Michigan (whether it's a single-family home near Detroit or a multi-unit property), both the federal IRS and the Michigan Department of Treasury require you to report this income.

You will need:

1. **Federal ITIN** (via Form W-7) to file Form 1040-NR with the IRS
2. **Michigan Tax ID** to file Form MI-1040NR (Michigan Non-resident Income Tax Return)

The two systems are separate. Your ITIN is exclusively for federal US tax purposes; Michigan may issue you a separate state identification number.

### FIRPTA Compliance

If you plan to sell your Michigan rental property, FIRPTA requires the buyer's agent or title company to withhold 15% of the sale price as a federal tax deposit. To claim exemption or reduce withholding, you must provide the IRS with an ITIN. Without one, the full 15% withholding applies, and you'll need to file a Form 1040-NR to recover overpaid withholding.

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Considerations

The Canada-US Tax Treaty provides certain relief for Canadian residents. Specifically, under Article X (Real Property), income from real property may be taxed in the country where the property is situated. This means Michigan can tax your rental income at its 4.25% rate. However, you remain subject to federal US taxation on this income.

**Key point:** The treaty does not eliminate your filing requirement—it determines which country has primary taxing rights. You must still file both a Canadian T1 return (reporting worldwide income) and a US 1040-NR (reporting Michigan rental income).

### Foreign Tax Credit Planning

When you file your Canadian T1 return, you can claim a foreign tax credit for US taxes paid on Michigan rental income. This prevents double taxation. To claim this credit, you need:

- Documentation that you filed Form 1040-NR with a valid ITIN
- Proof of taxes paid to the US (IRS Form 1040-NR with payment records)

## Who Must File Form W-7

**You should file Form W-7 if:**

- You are a Canadian citizen or permanent resident
- You own rental property in Michigan generating income
- You do not have a US Social Security Number
- The IRS has assigned you no ITIN previously

**You do NOT need Form W-7 if:**

- You already hold a valid ITIN from prior US tax filings
- You have a US Social Security Number (rare for Canadian landlords)

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Obtain the Form
Download Form W-7 from the IRS website (irs.gov) or request it by mail. The form is short—typically one page plus instructions.

### Step 2: Complete Section 1 (Applicant Information)
- **Name:** Enter your full legal name exactly as it appears on your Canadian passport
- **Mailing address:** Use your Canadian address (you are a non-resident)
- **Date of birth:** Enter as MM/DD/YYYY
- **Country of citizenship:** Enter "Canada"

### Step 3: Complete Section 2 (Type of Applicant)
Check the appropriate box. For a Canadian landlord, you will likely select:
- "Nonresident alien individual" (most common)

This signals to the IRS that you are not a US resident and are applying for federal tax filing purposes.

### Step 4: Complete Section 3 (Reason for Applying)
Select the relevant reason for your ITIN application:
- "Completing Form 1040-NR for income from Michigan rental property" or
- "Real estate transaction in Michigan (FIRPTA withholding)"

If you're filing proactively before a sale, use the first option. If a sale is imminent, use the second.

### Step 5: Sign and Date
You must sign the form personally. Electronic signatures are not accepted on the original IRS-filed copy.

### Step 6: Attach Original ID Documentation
The IRS requires original or certified copies of identification. Acceptable documents include:
- **Canadian passport** (preferred and most secure)
- **Canadian provincial driver's license** (with notarization)
- **Canadian birth certificate** (with notarization)

**Critical:** Mail original documents, not photocopies. The IRS will return them after verification.

### Step 7: File with the IRS
Mail Form W-7 and your identification documents to the IRS office serving your jurisdiction:

**For Canadian residents:**
- Mail to: Internal Revenue Service, ITIN Operations, Austin Service Center, 8800 Centro Park Drive, Austin, TX 78754, USA

Processing typically takes 6–8 weeks. You may track your application using IRS Form W-7 tracking.

## Michigan-Specific Considerations

### Michigan's 4.25% Rental Income Tax

Michigan taxes non-resident rental income at its standard rate of 4.25%, applied to your Michigan gross rental income. File Form MI-1040NR with the Michigan Department of Treasury by April 15 (or your filing deadline). The state does not require an ITIN; it accepts federal ITINs for identification purposes.

### Estimated Tax Payments

Both the IRS and Michigan may require quarterly estimated tax payments if you owe over $500. For the IRS, use Form 1040-ES; for Michigan, use Form MI-1040ES. Your ITIN enables you to make these payments electronically or by check.

### Property Tax Implications

Michigan's average effective property tax rate is 1.54%, applied by your county assessor. This is separate from income tax and does not require federal filing. However, property taxes are deductible on your Form 1040-NR, reducing your federal taxable income.

### Windsor-Detroit Border Advantage

Many Ontario landlords invest in Michigan rental property due to proximity to the Windsor-Detroit corridor. This concentration has led to specialized cross-border tax services. However, each property requires separate tax reporting, and each landlord needs a single ITIN. If you own multiple Michigan properties, use the same ITIN on all 1040-NR and FIRPTA documentation.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Submitting photocopies instead of originals:** The IRS will reject your application. Always send original or certified ID documents.

2. **Mismatching name formats:** If your Canadian passport shows "Robert James Smith" but you apply as "Bob Smith," the IRS may delay or deny your ITIN. Use the exact legal name.

3. **Filing too late:** Ideally, apply for your ITIN before your first rental income is received. If a property sale is imminent, apply immediately to secure ITIN before FIRPTA withholding occurs.

4. **Forgetting to report the ITIN on 1040-NR:** Once you receive your ITIN, include it on every Form 1040-NR and state return. Omitting it can delay processing.

5. **Not keeping records:** The IRS will mail your ITIN approval letter. Keep this document permanently for tax filing and FIRPTA transactions.

6. **Applying with expired ID:** Ensure your Canadian passport is current. Expired documents may be rejected.
