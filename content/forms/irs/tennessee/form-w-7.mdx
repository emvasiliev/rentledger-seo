# Form W-7 for Canadian Landlords in Tennessee: Complete Guide

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS application form used by non-US citizens and non-resident aliens to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number issued by the IRS that functions similarly to a Social Security Number (SSN) for tax filing and reporting purposes.

For Canadian landlords owning rental property in the United States, an ITIN is essential to:
- File US non-resident alien tax returns (Form 1040-NR)
- Report rental income from US properties
- Comply with FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding obligations
- Claim deductions and credits related to US rental operations

Unlike a SSN, an ITIN does not authorize work in the United States and does not provide Social Security benefits or Medicare eligibility.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Tennessee

### Why Tennessee Matters for Your US Tax Burden

Tennessee presents a favorable tax environment for Canadian rental property owners because the state has **no state income tax**. This is a significant advantage compared to many other US states where you would owe both federal and state rental income taxes.

However, do not mistake the absence of state income tax for a complete absence of property-related taxes. Tennessee levies a property tax at an average effective rate of **0.71%** of assessed property value. This is relatively low compared to the national average of approximately 0.85%, which means your annual holding costs in Tennessee are modest.

When you own rental property in Tennessee:
- **Federal taxes apply**: You must file Form 1040-NR and report rental income to the IRS
- **State taxes do not apply**: Tennessee does not tax rental income or capital gains
- **Property taxes apply**: You will owe annual property taxes to the county where your property is located
- **FIRPTA rules apply**: When you eventually sell your Tennessee property, FIRPTA withholding rules require the buyer's closing agent to withhold 15% of the gross sale price unless an exemption applies

An ITIN obtained through Form W-7 enables you to properly file your Form 1040-NR and claim the ITIN when managing FIRPTA compliance.

### The Role of the Canada-US Tax Treaty

The Canada-US Tax Treaty (technically the Income and Gains Protocol) provides certain beneficial provisions for Canadian residents. Article XXII addresses taxation of income from real property. While the treaty does not eliminate US federal tax on rental income, it may:

- Allow you to claim foreign tax credits on your Canadian T1 return for US federal taxes paid
- Provide reduced withholding rates on certain income types (though not rental income, which remains subject to standard rates)

You will claim the US federal income tax paid as a foreign tax credit on your Canadian T1 return (Schedule 11 in most provinces) to prevent double taxation.

## Who Files Form W-7?

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

1. **A non-resident alien** (which applies to Canadian citizens and permanent residents who are not US residents for tax purposes)
2. **Required to file a US tax return** (which includes non-resident aliens with US rental income)
3. **Without a Social Security Number** (the typical situation for Canadians)
4. **Filing for the first time with the IRS** regarding US rental property

You do not need an ITIN if:
- You are a US citizen or green card holder (SSN holder)
- You have no US tax filing requirement

Most Canadian landlords in Tennessee meet all criteria and must obtain an ITIN to remain compliant with IRS requirements.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documents

Before completing Form W-7, assemble:
- Your valid Canadian passport
- Your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN)
- Your Canadian address
- Details about your Tennessee rental property (address, acquisition date)
- Information about your US rental income
- Proof of your reason for needing the ITIN (typically a copy of your draft Form 1040-NR showing rental income)

### Step 2: Complete the Form W-7 Itself

**Part A – Applicant Information**
- Enter your full legal name as it appears on your Canadian passport
- Provide your Canadian home address
- Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Provide your date of birth and place of birth

**Part B – IRS Uses**
- Check the box indicating your reason for applying: typically **(b) Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return**
- This is the primary reason for Canadian landlords

**Part C – Dependent Information**
- Leave blank unless you are claiming dependents on your US return (generally not applicable)

**Part D – Signature**
- Sign and date the form
- Include your SIN (not a SSN, since you do not have one)

### Step 3: Gather Certification Documents

The IRS requires **certified copies** of identity documents. You have three options:

**Option 1: IRS Acceptance Agent**
- The simplest method for Canadian landlords
- The IRS maintains a list of Acceptance Agents (often CPAs, enrolled agents, or tax professionals)
- The Acceptance Agent will review your original passport, certify a copy, and submit your W-7 to the IRS on your behalf
- Costs typically range from $50–$150 USD

**Option 2: US Embassy or Consulate**
- You can have the Canadian Embassy in Washington, D.C., or a US consulate in Canada certify your documents
- This requires travel or mailing documents internationally
- More time-consuming than using an Acceptance Agent

**Option 3: Notary Public**
- A notarized copy of your passport may be acceptable in limited circumstances
- Verify current IRS acceptance policy, as requirements change

**Most Canadian landlords use Option 1** (IRS Acceptance Agent) because it is fastest and most reliable.

### Step 4: Submit Your Application

**Via an IRS Acceptance Agent:**
- Provide your completed Form W-7, passport copy (certified), and a copy of your draft Form 1040-NR showing your rental income
- The Acceptance Agent submits the package to the IRS
- Processing typically takes 4–6 weeks

**Via US Mail (without an Acceptance Agent):**
- Mail your completed Form W-7 with certified copies to the IRS ITIN processing center specified on the form
- Processing typically takes 6–8 weeks
- Include a cover letter explaining you are a non-resident alien with US rental income

**Do not send original documents** — the IRS requires copies only.

### Step 5: Receive Your ITIN

The IRS will mail your ITIN assignment letter to your Canadian address. Your ITIN will be a nine-digit number formatted as XXX-XX-XXXX. 

Use this ITIN on:
- Form 1040-NR (filed annually to report rental income)
- Form 4255 (if claiming the Residential Energy Credit, though unlikely for rental properties)
- FIRPTA withholding statements provided to your property's buyer at sale

## Tennessee-Specific Considerations

### Property Tax Reporting

While Tennessee has no income tax, your county assessor will track your property ownership. With an ITIN, you can properly report your Tennessee property address to the IRS. This ensures consistency between property tax records and federal tax filings.

### FIRPTA and Tennessee Property Sales

If you sell your Tennessee rental property, FIRPTA requires the buyer's closing attorney to withhold **15% of the gross sale price** unless an exemption applies. The withholding is remitted to the IRS and credited against your final US tax liability.

Example: If your Tennessee property sells for $300,000, FIRPTA withholding is $45,000.

Your ITIN must appear on all FIRPTA withholding statements and forms (Form 8288 series) filed by the closing agent.

### Rental Deduction Opportunities in Tennessee

Tennessee landlords can deduct:
- Mortgage interest (on loans secured by the rental property)
- Property taxes (the 0.71% annual rate)
- Insurance premiums
- Repairs and maintenance
- Depreciation (Form 4562)
- Rental agent commissions or property management fees
- Utilities (if you pay them)

These deductions reduce your federal taxable rental income, which reduces your overall US tax burden. Calculate deductions carefully because they also affect your Canadian foreign income reported on your T1 return.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: Providing Uncertified Document Copies**
The IRS will reject your W-7 if document copies are not certified by an Acceptance Agent, embassy, or notary. Do not send photocopies.
