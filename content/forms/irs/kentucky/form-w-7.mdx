# Form W-7 for Canadian Landlords: Your ITIN Guide for Kentucky Rental Properties

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is your gateway to obtaining an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)—a nine-digit identification number required by the IRS for non-US citizens who must file US tax returns or comply with US tax withholding obligations.

As a Canadian landlord owning Kentucky rental property, you are classified as a "non-resident alien" for US tax purposes. Without a Social Security Number, an ITIN becomes your primary identifier for all US federal tax reporting and compliance activities related to your Kentucky real estate holdings.

## Why Kentucky Landlords Need an ITIN

Kentucky presents a specific tax compliance scenario for Canadian property owners:

**Federal FIRPTA and Withholding Requirements**
If you sell your Kentucky rental property, the purchaser or their agent is required to withhold 15% of the sale proceeds under FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act). To claim an exemption or adjustment to this withholding, you must provide a valid ITIN to the IRS.

**Kentucky State Income Tax Filings**
Kentucky imposes a flat 4.5% state income tax on rental income derived from Kentucky sources. Non-resident landlords must file Kentucky Form 740-NR (Nonresident/Part-Year Resident Income Tax Return) annually. The IRS coordinates state filings, and many states (including Kentucky) cross-reference ITIN numbers with federal filings.

**Federal Form 1040-NR Requirements**
You must file Form 1040-NR (U.S. Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) to report US-source rental income. This form requires a valid ITIN or SSN. Without an ITIN, your return cannot be processed.

**Property Tax Administration**
While Kentucky's county property tax assessors do not directly require an ITIN (average effective rate: 0.86% of property value), establishing clear federal tax identification helps coordinate your federal and state filings and prevents administrative delays.

## How Form W-7 Fits Into Your Overall US Tax Picture

**Relationship to Your Canadian T1 Return**
You will simultaneously file:
- **Canadian T1 General** (reporting worldwide income, including US rental income)
- **US Form 1040-NR** (reporting US-source rental income only)
- **Kentucky Form 740-NR** (reporting Kentucky rental income)

The ITIN enables all three jurisdictions to recognize your tax identity consistently. Canada and the US have a comprehensive tax treaty (Canada-US Tax Treaty) that provides foreign tax credits, allowing you to offset US and Kentucky taxes paid against your Canadian federal and provincial tax liability on the same income—avoiding double taxation.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you:

- Are a Canadian resident with no US Social Security Number
- Own rental real estate in Kentucky generating rental income
- Are subject to FIRPTA withholding on a Kentucky property sale
- Require a US taxpayer identification number for IRS compliance

You do **not** need a separate ITIN if you already hold a valid US Social Security Number or EIN (Employer Identification Number), though this is rare for Canadian landlords.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing W-7, assemble:

- **Valid passport** (your Canadian passport, unexpired or expired within last 5 years)
- **Proof of foreign status** (Canadian driver's license, provincial health card, or permanent residence card)
- **Proof of US tax obligation** (draft Form 1040-NR, signed lease agreements, or property purchase deed showing your name)
- **Completed Form W-7** (original application, signed and dated)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately

**Line 1: Name (as it appears on your passport)**
Enter your legal name exactly as shown on your Canadian passport. Consistency across all US tax forms is critical.

**Line 2: Mailing Address**
You may provide your Canadian mailing address (where the IRS will send your ITIN notice). Do **not** use a PO Box; use your full street address.

**Line 3: Country of Citizenship/Residence**
Enter "Canada."

**Line 4: Passport Number and Country of Issuance**
Enter your Canadian passport number and "Canada."

**Line 5a–5c: Type of ITIN Applicant**
Select **(c) Nonresident Alien Required to File a US Tax Return**. This is the correct category for landlords filing 1040-NR.

**Line 6: US Tax Return Information**
Indicate the tax year for which you need the ITIN (typically the year you acquired the Kentucky property or expect rental income). For example, if you purchased the property in 2024, enter 2024.

**Attach Documentation:**
Attach certified copies (not originals) of:
- Passport photo page
- Proof of Canadian residency (driver's license or provincial ID)
- Form 1040-NR (draft or completed) signed by you

### Step 3: Sign and Date

Sign Form W-7 in the presence of **either**:
- A qualified IRS Acceptance Agent (banks, accountancy firms, or immigrant service organizations in your area)
- A US embassy or consulate official (if you are in Canada)
- An IRS official (rare)

**Do not mail unsigned forms.** The IRS will reject unsigned applications.

### Step 4: Mail the Application

**Mail to:**
Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operation
Austin, TX 73301
USA

Processing typically takes **6–8 weeks** from receipt. The IRS will mail your ITIN notice (Form W-7c or a formal letter) to your Canadian address.

**Do not use Canadian postal codes on the envelope.** Use ZIP codes or leave the postal code field blank and write "CANADA" prominently.

## Kentucky-Specific Considerations

**State-Level Coordination**
Kentucky's Department of Revenue cross-references ITIN numbers with federal IRS filings. When you file Kentucky Form 740-NR, the state verifies your ITIN with the IRS. Delays in obtaining your ITIN may delay Kentucky state tax processing and potentially trigger automated notices.

**Kentucky Property Tax Notices**
County tax assessors in Kentucky (typically county Property Valuation Administrators) maintain property tax records separately from the IRS. While they do not require an ITIN to assess property, your federal ITIN should be recorded with the deed or property management records to ensure coordinated mail delivery and prevent notices from being misdirected.

**FIRPTA State Coordination**
Under FIRPTA, a 15% withholding applies to your Kentucky property sale proceeds. Kentucky does not impose an additional state-level FIRPTA withholding; however, the federal withholding agent will need your ITIN to file Form 8288-B (U.S. Withholding Tax Return for Dispositions by Foreign Persons of US Real Property Interests) with the IRS. Without your ITIN, the transaction may be delayed or held in escrow.

**Tax Treaty Benefits**
The Canada-US Tax Treaty allows Canadian residents to claim a foreign tax credit on their Canadian tax return for Kentucky state income taxes paid. You must report your Kentucky 740-NR filing and taxes paid on your Canadian T1 return to claim this credit. Your ITIN enables the IRS and CRA to cross-verify these amounts.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Incomplete or Unsigned Applications**
Many applicants mail Form W-7 without obtaining a qualified signature. The IRS will immediately reject unsigned forms, delaying your ITIN by weeks. Always have your signature witnessed by an IRS Acceptance Agent or US consular official **before** mailing.

**2. Mislabeling Documentation**
Do not submit original documents (especially your passport). Always submit certified copies. Use a notary public in Canada to certify copies if required.

**3. Inconsistent Name Spelling**
If your name on Form W-7 differs from your name on Form 1040-NR or your property deed, the IRS may delay processing or require clarification. Ensure exact matching across all forms.

**4. Providing a Canadian Postal Code Only**
The IRS mail system uses US ZIP codes. If you provide only a Canadian postal code (e.g., "ON M5H 2N2"), the US postal service will reject delivery. Write "CANADA" clearly or consult your accountant for mailing instructions.
