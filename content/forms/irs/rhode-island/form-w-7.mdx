# Form W-7: ITIN Guide for Canadian Landlords with Rhode Island Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS document that allows non-resident aliens without a US Social Security Number to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted like a Social Security Number (SSN), required to file US federal tax returns and comply with US tax withholding obligations.

For Canadian landlords who own rental property in the United States, obtaining an ITIN is essential for federal tax compliance. Without an ITIN, you cannot legally file Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) or claim exemptions under the Canada-US Tax Treaty.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Rhode Island Landlords

As a Canadian landlord owning rental property in Rhode Island, you face a dual-filing obligation:

**Federal Level:** You must report US-source rental income to the IRS on Form 1040-NR and provide an ITIN.

**Rhode Island State Level:** Rhode Island requires non-resident individuals to file Form RI-1040-NR (Nonresident Income Tax Return) and report rental income at the state tax rate of 5.99%. This is separate from federal filing but uses the same income figure.

The ITIN serves both purposes—it identifies you to the IRS for federal returns and can be referenced on your Rhode Island state return. Additionally, if you eventually sell the Rhode Island property, FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding rules require the buyer's closing attorney or title company to withhold 15% of the gross sale price. Your ITIN must be on file before the sale closes to avoid complications or ensure proper withholding reconciliation.

## Who Files Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

- A Canadian citizen or permanent resident
- A non-resident alien (as defined by IRS rules—generally, not physically present in the US for 183 days in the current year and prior two years)
- Owning real property that generates US-source rental income
- Lacking a valid SSN

You do **not** need Form W-7 if you already hold an ITIN (check your prior correspondence with the IRS) or if you have obtained a US SSN through authorized employment.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Part 1: Personal Information

- **Line 1a:** Enter your full legal name exactly as it appears on your passport
- **Line 1b:** Enter your name in your native script if different (optional but helpful for verification)
- **Line 1c:** Enter your date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY format)

### Part 2: Mailing Address

- Enter your Canadian mailing address (the IRS will send correspondence here)
- Provide a US address if you have one, but this is not required for non-resident alien landlords

### Part 3: Reason for Applying

- **Checkbox:** Select "Other" and write "US Rental Property Ownership and Nonresident Alien Tax Filing"
- This clarifies to the IRS that you're applying specifically for income tax purposes, not for a financial account or sponsorship

### Part 4: Foreign Address and Country Information

- Enter your Canadian home address
- Country of citizenship: Canada
- Country of residence: Canada (even if you have a US address, your tax home is Canada)

### Part 5: Identification Documentation

- **For Canadian applicants:** Provide a photocopy of your valid Canadian passport, driver's license, or provincial ID
- **Notarization:** Acceptable copies can be certified by a notary public in Canada or the US, or certified by a Canadian authority

## Rhode Island-Specific Considerations

### State Tax Filing Requirement

Rhode Island does not recognize ITINs for state purposes in the same way the IRS does, but it does accept them on RI-1040-NR forms. When you file your Rhode Island state return, use the ITIN you obtain from the IRS. The state Department of Revenue will process your return accordingly.

**Important:** File your federal Form 1040-NR **before** filing RI-1040-NR. The federal return is due **April 15**, and Rhode Island typically aligns its deadline (though RI has no separate extension policy—federal extension automatically applies if you file an extension with the IRS).

### Property Tax Context

While Rhode Island's effective property tax rate averages 1.63%, this is a local town assessment and does not depend on having an ITIN. Property tax bills are tied to deed ownership. However, the ITIN is essential for federal and state **income** tax on rental receipts.

### Rental Income Reporting

Rental income in Rhode Island is subject to:
- **Federal tax** (graduated rates, currently 10%–37%)
- **Rhode Island state tax** (flat 5.99% on non-resident rental income)

Your net rental income (after mortgage interest, property management, repairs, and depreciation) is taxable in both jurisdictions. An ITIN ensures you file correctly and avoid penalties.

### FIRPTA Considerations

If you sell the Rhode Island property, FIRPTA requires a 15% withholding from the sale proceeds. The escrow agent or closing attorney will request your ITIN. Without it on file, the withholding calculation may be delayed or require additional documentation. Obtain your ITIN **before** listing the property for sale or entering into a purchase-and-sale agreement.

## Completing Form W-7: Detailed Sections

### Part 6: Tax Residency

- Confirm your country of tax residence is **Canada**
- Indicate you are filing a US return solely because of US real property income

### Part 7: Visa Status (if applicable)

- As a non-resident, leave visa information blank unless you've entered the US on a visitor visa for business purposes
- Attach IRS Publication 519 if you believe you may qualify as a "resident alien" due to physical presence, but most Canadian landlords remain non-residents

### Part 8: Signature

- Sign and date the form
- Have your signature **notarized** by a Canadian notary public or US notary (a photocopy certified by a notary is acceptable)

## Submission Requirements

**Original and Copy:** Submit the original W-7 with:
- A **certified copy** of your identification (passport strongly preferred)
- A **notarized statement** (or certified photocopy of your passport, which serves as verification)

**Where to Send:**
- Mail to: IRS ITIN Operations, Austin, TX 73301, USA
- Or apply in person at a US embassy or consulate in Canada (some consulates offer in-person acceptance)

**Processing Time:** 4–6 weeks after receipt; up to 120 days in peak periods.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Submitting an unnotarized identification copy** — Always have the copy certified or notarized
2. **Using a nickname instead of legal name** — The IRS requires your exact legal name from your passport
3. **Applying too late** — File W-7 at least 3 months before your first 1040-NR deadline to avoid extension necessity
4. **Not explaining the reason for the ITIN** — Clearly state "Non-resident alien rental property ownership" to expedite processing
5. **Incomplete Part 3** — Vague reasoning can trigger IRS requests for clarification and delay approval
6. **Failing to update contact information** — Use your Canadian address consistently; any changes require letter notification to IRS
