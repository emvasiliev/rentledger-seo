# Schedule E for Canadian Landlords: Complete West Virginia Rental Property Guide

## What is Schedule E?

Schedule E (Supplemental Income and Loss) is the US Internal Revenue Service form used to report rental real estate income and associated expenses. For Canadian landlords owning property in West Virginia, Schedule E becomes necessary when you elect to treat your US rental income as "effectively connected income" (ECI) under Internal Revenue Code Section 871(d).

Without this election, non-resident aliens face a flat 30% withholding tax on gross rental receipts—with no deduction for expenses. The Section 871(d) election allows you to deduct actual operating expenses (mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, utilities, property management fees, insurance, and depreciation), potentially resulting in significant tax savings.

## How Schedule E Applies Specifically in West Virginia

West Virginia presents a unique dual-taxation scenario for Canadian landlords. You must navigate both federal US taxation and West Virginia state taxation simultaneously.

### Federal Level (Schedule E on Form 1040-NR)

Schedule E captures rental income and expenses and flows to your Form 1040-NR (Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return). Your net rental income from West Virginia property becomes subject to US federal income tax at graduated rates (currently ranging from 10% to 37% on ordinary income, depending on your total worldwide income). If you have made a Section 871(d) election, you report actual expenses rather than accepting a 30% withholding.

### West Virginia State Level

West Virginia imposes a personal income tax of **6.5%** on rental income derived from in-state real property. Even if you're a non-resident alien, West Virginia requires you to file a non-resident state income tax return (West Virginia Form IT-100-R, Non-Resident Income Tax Return) if you earned rental income from West Virginia real estate during the tax year.

West Virginia provides no resident exemption for federal-level deductions. You must calculate your Schedule E net income (after federal deductions), then apply West Virginia's 6.5% tax rate to that same net figure or West Virginia's adjusted gross income.

### Property Tax Considerations

West Virginia's effective property tax rate averages **0.59%** of property value, with rates varying by county. Property taxes paid on your rental property are fully deductible on Schedule E (Part I, line 8) and reduce both your federal taxable income and West Virginia taxable income.

## Who Files Schedule E as a Canadian Landlord

You must file Schedule E if:

1. **You own rental property in West Virginia** and generated gross rental income during the tax year
2. **You are a non-resident alien** (for US tax purposes) — meaning you are not a US citizen, green card holder, or permanent resident
3. **You have made a Section 871(d) election** to treat rental income as effectively connected income, allowing expense deductions rather than accepting flat 30% withholding

Canadian citizens and permanent residents of Canada are generally classified as non-resident aliens for US tax purposes unless they hold US green cards or citizenship. The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article IV) defines residency; Canadian residents are typically not US residents for tax purposes solely by virtue of Canadian citizenship.

You do not file Schedule E if you elected to pay the 30% flat withholding tax on gross rents without deducting expenses (the alternative to Section 871(d)).

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Schedule E for West Virginia Property

### Part I: Income and Expenses from Rental Real Estate

**Line 1a (Address of property):** Enter your West Virginia property address.

**Line 1b (Type of property):** Select "Single Family Dwelling," "Apartment," "House," or "Other," as applicable.

**Line 1c (How acquired/Depreciation method):** Indicate the year acquired and depreciation method (typically MACRS for residential property).

**Line 3 (Rents received):** Enter total gross rent collected or accrued during the tax year from your West Virginia property. Do not net any expenses here.

**Line 5 (Advertising):** Include costs of online listings, real estate websites, newspaper ads, or property listing services.

**Line 6 (Auto/travel):** Include vehicle mileage related to property management (IRS standard mileage rate applies) or travel to inspect the property.

**Line 8 (Taxes):** Enter West Virginia property taxes paid. This is a critical deduction in West Virginia, averaging 0.59% of property value.

**Line 9 (Utilities):** Include electricity, gas, water, sewer, trash, or internet if you provide these to tenants.

**Line 10 (Repairs):** Include routine maintenance, painting, fixing leaks, or replacing worn components. Do not include capital improvements (those are depreciated over time).

**Line 11 (Supplies):** Small tools, cleaning supplies, or office supplies for property management.

**Line 12 (Taxes and licenses):** Business licenses or occupancy permits specific to rental operations.

**Line 13 (Mortgage interest):** Deduct mortgage interest (not principal). This is often a landlord's largest deduction.

**Line 14 (Other interest):** Interest on loans used to improve the property.

**Line 17 (Management fees):** If you pay a property manager or management company in West Virginia.

**Line 18 (Maid/cleaning):** Professional cleaning services between tenants.

**Line 20 (Insurance):** Landlord insurance, liability coverage, or fire insurance.

**Line 21 (Depreciation):** Non-Canadian landlords may claim depreciation on the building (not land). Residential property depreciates over 27.5 years using MACRS. Canadian landlords should note that depreciation claimed on US property reduces your Canadian adjusted basis for future calculations.

**Line 23 (Utilities):** Utilities paid directly by you (if not claimed on line 9).

**Line 28 (Total expenses):** Sum all deductions.

**Line 29 (Net income/loss):** Gross rent minus total expenses.

### Reconciliation to Form 1040-NR

Schedule E Part I, line 29 (net income or loss) flows to Form 1040-NR, line 9 (rental income or loss). This amount is subject to US federal income tax and forms the basis for West Virginia state tax calculation.

## West Virginia-Specific Considerations

### State Return Filing Requirement

You must file West Virginia Form IT-100-R (Non-Resident Income Tax Return) by **April 15** (or **June 15** if you requested an extension). West Virginia typically provides a pass-through pass for federal deductions; your Schedule E net income is the same figure reported to West Virginia. The 6.5% state tax applies on this net amount.

### Property Tax Deductibility

West Virginia's low effective property tax rate (0.59%) is a significant advantage. Ensure you claim all property taxes on Schedule E, line 8. This deduction reduces both federal and West Virginia taxable income dollar-for-dollar.

### No State Treaty Relief

West Virginia does not have reciprocal tax treaties with Canada. You cannot claim West Virginia tax as a foreign tax credit on your Canadian T1 return; instead, you claim it under the standard foreign tax credit rules on Form 1118 (Foreign Tax Credit Calculation).

### Coordination with Canadian T1 Return

Canadian residents must report worldwide income, including US rental income, on their Canadian T1 (Form 1) in the year earned (regardless of when paid). You will report the US rental income on Schedule 4 (Employment and Other Income) of your T1, convert it to Canadian dollars using the average annual exchange rate from the Bank of Canada, and claim a foreign tax credit for both US federal and West Virginia state taxes paid (Form 1118).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Failing to make the Section 871(d) election formally.** The election must be made on a timely-filed Form 1040-NR (including extensions). Without it, you default to the 30% withholding rule.

**2. Confusing repairs with capital improvements.** Repairs are immediately deductible; replacements or improvements that add value or extend asset life must be depreciated. A new roof replacement is often capitalized; repairing a single shingle is deductible.

**3. Omitting West Virginia property taxes.** Landlords sometimes overlook state and county property taxes or forget to add special assessments. Ensure all property taxes are captured on line 8.

**4. Double-deducting mortgage interest.** Mortgage interest claimed on Schedule E cannot be claimed again on Schedule A (itemized deductions) on Form 1040-NR. One deduction only.
