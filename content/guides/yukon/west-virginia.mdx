## Cross-Border Rental Property Tax Guide: Yukon Residents Owning Property in West Virginia

Owning rental property in West Virginia as a Yukon resident creates a unique tax situation. You are subject to taxation by three different jurisdictions: Canada (via the CRA), the United States (via the IRS), and West Virginia. Each has its own filing requirements, deadlines, and tax rates. Understanding these obligations is essential to avoiding penalties and optimizing your after-tax rental income.

This guide walks through the specific rules that apply to your situation and provides actionable steps to stay compliant.

## Why This Combination Matters

As a Yukon resident, you are a Canadian tax resident and must report worldwide income to the CRA. The property you own in West Virginia generates US-source income, which the IRS will tax at federal and state levels. This creates the potential for double taxation—paying tax on the same income in both Canada and the US—unless you properly claim foreign tax credits.

West Virginia imposes a flat 6.5% non-resident state income tax on rental income. The US federal rate depends on your election choice (see Section 871(d) below). Your Yukon marginal rate will range from 32% to 48%, depending on your total income. Currency exchange rates affect your Canadian tax liability because rental income received in USD must be converted to CAD.

The result: careful planning and proper filing reduce your overall tax burden significantly.

## Canadian Tax Obligations (CRA)

### Report on Form T776

All rental income and expenses must be reported on **Form T776 (Statement of Real Estate Rentals)** in your annual T1 tax return. This is mandatory even if you have a loss.

On Form T776, report:
- Gross rental income converted to CAD at the Bank of Canada average exchange rate for the year (1 USD = 1.36 CAD for 2025, though you may use the actual rate for each transaction if consistent)
- All deductible expenses (mortgage interest, property tax, insurance, repairs, property management fees, utilities you pay, advertising)
- Depreciation is **not** deductible in Canada (unlike the US), so do not claim it

### Form T1135 (Foreign Property)

If the fair market value of your West Virginia property exceeds **CAD $100,000** at any time during the tax year, you must file **Form T1135 (Foreign Investment Income Tax Return)** by your tax return due date (June 15 for most individuals).

Report the property's fair market value in CAD, the foreign income generated, and taxes paid to the US and West Virginia.

### Foreign Tax Credit (FTC)

You can claim a foreign tax credit on **Form T2209** for US federal and West Virginia state taxes paid on this rental income. This credit reduces your Canadian tax dollar-for-dollar (up to your Canadian tax on that income).

The calculation:
- **US federal tax paid**: Multiply your taxable rental income by your effective federal rate (depends on whether you elect Section 871(d))
- **West Virginia tax paid**: Multiply your net rental income by 6.5%
- Both amounts must be converted to CAD

The CRA will only credit taxes paid to a foreign government, not amounts withheld but not yet paid to the IRS or state revenue department. If taxes are refundable, include only the net amount you expect to owe.

### Filing Deadline

Your Canadian tax return is due **June 15, 2026** for 2025 income (though payment is due **April 30, 2026**).

## US Federal Tax Obligations (IRS)

### Obtain an ITIN

If you do not have a US Social Security Number, you must apply for an **Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)** using **Form W-7**. Submit this when you file your first US tax return. Processing takes 4–6 weeks. Once you have an ITIN, use it on all future US filings.

### Section 871(d) Election

This is critical for your situation. Without an election, the IRS will withhold **30% of gross rental income** as default non-resident withholding. With a **Section 871(d) election**, you elect to be taxed on **net rental income** (income minus deductions) at regular US federal rates (currently 10% to 37%, depending on income bracket).

The Section 871(d) election is made by:
1. Filing **Form 1040-NR (U.S. Non-resident Alien Income Tax Return)** reporting net rental income on **Schedule E (Supplemental Income and Loss)**
2. Attaching a statement to your return declaring the election

Once made, this election applies to all future tax years unless you revoke it.

**Result of the election:**
- You deduct mortgage interest, property tax, insurance, repairs, depreciation, and other expenses before calculating tax
- Your effective tax rate on rental income drops substantially (typically 15%–25% vs. 30% withholding)
- You must file Form 1040-NR even if you have a loss

### Schedule E Depreciation

On Schedule E, you can deduct **depreciation** on the building (not land). If the property cost USD $200,000 with USD $50,000 attributable to land and USD $150,000 to the building, depreciate the building over 27.5 years.

Annual depreciation: USD $150,000 ÷ 27.5 = USD $5,454.55

Depreciation reduces your taxable income but recaptures at 25% when you sell (see FIRPTA below).

### US Filing Deadline

File **Form 1040-NR by June 15, 2026** for 2025 income. If you miss this, you may file by **October 15, 2026**, with extension.

## West Virginia State Tax Obligations

### Non-resident State Return

West Virginia requires non-residents who earn income from WV sources to file **West Virginia Non-resident Income Tax Return (Form IT-104NR)** reporting rental income at a flat **6.5% tax rate**.

The tax is calculated on **net rental income** (gross minus deductible expenses, including depreciation). File this return with your federal Form 1040-NR or separately by **June 15, 2026**.

### Property Tax

West Virginia's average property tax rate is approximately **0.59% of fair market value** annually. This is deductible against both US and Canadian taxable income.

Example: If your property is valued at USD $250,000, expect roughly USD $1,475 in annual property tax.

## Withholding and Part XIII (Canada)

If you do not file an **NR6 certificate** with the property manager or payor, the CRA may require **25% withholding on gross rents** as Part XIII tax on non-resident investment income.

**How to avoid this:**
- File **Form NR6 (Undertaking—Non-resident of Canada)** with your property manager
- This certifies you are a non-resident (which you are not—you *are* a Canadian resident)
- **In your situation, you should NOT file NR6** because you are a Yukon resident

Instead, ensure the payor reports rental payments without withholding, and you report the full amount on your T776 and claim the foreign tax credit for US/WV taxes paid.

## Selling the Property (FIRPTA Overview)

If you sell the West Virginia property, special US rules apply.

**Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA):**
- A buyer must withhold **15% of the net sale price** and remit it to the IRS
- You file **Form 8288 (U.S. Withholding Tax Return for Disposition by Foreign Persons of U.S. Real Property Interests)** within 20 days of sale
- You report the sale on Form 1040-NR, including:
  - Sale proceeds in USD converted to CAD
  - Adjusted basis (original cost + improvements − depreciation claimed)
  - Depreciation recapture taxed at 25%
  - Capital gain taxed at federal rates (0%, 15%, or 20%)

You will also owe West Virginia capital gains tax (same 6.5% rate) on the gain.

The CRA will allow a foreign tax credit for FIRPTA withholding and any US capital gains tax paid.
