# Form W-7: ITIN Application for Canadian Landlords with Missouri Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN), is the official IRS document that allows non-US citizens without a Social Security Number (SSN) to obtain a unique nine-digit taxpayer identification number. For Canadian landlords, this ITIN becomes your US tax identifier for filing Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) and managing FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding obligations.

An ITIN is not a work authorization document—it is strictly for tax reporting purposes. The number itself resembles an SSN (9 digits in the format 9XX-XX-XXXX) but is issued only to foreign nationals required to file US tax returns.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Missouri

If you own rental property in Missouri, you are considered a non-resident alien for US federal tax purposes (unless you meet specific presence tests under the Canada-US Tax Treaty). Missouri imposes state income tax on rental income at a rate of **4.95%** on net rental income, plus you face federal taxation on the same income.

### Federal Taxation

You must file Form 1040-NR (Non-Resident Alien Return) to report:
- Gross rental revenue from Missouri property
- Deductible expenses (mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, repairs, depreciation)
- Net rental income subject to US federal tax (ranging from 10% to 37% depending on income level)

### Missouri State Taxation

Missouri requires non-residents who earn income from Missouri sources to file Form MO-1040(NR) (Missouri Non-Resident Income Tax Return). Your Missouri rental income is taxed at **4.95%**, with allowable deductions similar to federal treatment. Missouri property tax on residential rental property averages **1.01%** of assessed value annually.

### FIRPTA Obligations

If you plan to sell the Missouri property, the buyer's agent or attorney must withhold **15% of the gross sale price** under FIRPTA rules (26 U.S.C. § 1445). To report the sale and potentially recover overwitheld amounts, you need an ITIN. Without one, you cannot file the required Form 8288-B (FIRPTA return).

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Considerations

Under Article IV of the Canada-US Tax Treaty, you are a Canadian resident for purposes of the treaty. However, you remain liable for US tax on US-source income (rental income from Missouri property). The treaty provides a foreign tax credit mechanism: when you file your Canadian T1 return, you can claim a non-resident portion of Canadian tax paid as a foreign tax credit against your US tax owing, reducing double taxation.

## Who Files Form W-7?

You must file Form W-7 if you are:
- A Canadian citizen or resident without a US SSN
- Required to file a US tax return (1040-NR) due to Missouri rental income
- Required to provide an ITIN for FIRPTA withholding or sale reporting
- Unable to obtain an SSN (which is generally reserved for those with work authorization)

**Timeline for filing:** Apply for the ITIN before or concurrently with your first 1040-NR filing. If you are selling property, apply immediately so FIRPTA withholding agents have the number before closing.

## Step-by-Step Instructions for Completing Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Prepare the following:
- Valid passport (Canadian passport is acceptable primary identification)
- Proof of tax residency in Canada (copy of most recent Canadian T1 return or CRA Notice of Assessment)
- Form W-7 (available at irs.gov)
- Your mailing address in Canada

**Do not submit original documents.** The IRS requires certified copies or photocopies of documents originating outside the US.

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately

**Line 1(a):** Enter your legal name exactly as it appears on your passport.

**Line 1(b):** Enter your date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY format).

**Line 1(c):** Citizenship: Select "Canada."

**Line 2:** Select the reason you need an ITIN. For Missouri rental property, mark **"Other (specify)"** and write "Form 1040-NR filer—non-resident alien with US rental property income." If applicable, also note FIRPTA requirement.

**Line 3:** Enter your country of residence (Canada).

**Line 4:** Mailing address: Provide your Canadian mailing address.

**Line 5:** Telephone (optional): Include a Canada-based number if you have one.

**Line 6:** Country of residence (should mirror Line 3).

### Step 3: Submit Application

**Option A: Mail submission** (most common for international applicants)
Send Form W-7 with supporting documents to:

```
Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operation
P.O. Box 149342
Austin, TX 78714-9342
USA
```

**Option B: In-person submission**
If you travel to the US, apply at an authorized IRS Acceptance Agent location. ITIN compliance centers exist in major cities, though availability for international applicants may be limited.

**Processing time:** Expect 15–21 days for mailed applications, though international submissions may take up to 6 weeks due to document verification.

### Step 4: Await IRS Confirmation

The IRS will mail your ITIN to your Canadian address. Once received, use it on all US tax forms: 1040-NR, Schedule E (rental income), Form 8288-B (if selling property), and Missouri Form MO-1040(NR).

## Missouri-Specific Considerations

### Nexus and State Filing Requirements

Once you have Missouri rental income, you establish a tax nexus in Missouri for state purposes. You must file Missouri Form MO-1040(NR) annually, reporting:
- Rental income (gross rents)
- Deductible expenses (property tax, insurance, maintenance, depreciation)
- The net income is taxed at **4.95%**

Note: Missouri taxes depreciation recapture at ordinary income rates (up to 25% federal), but depreciation itself reduces your Missouri taxable income dollar-for-dollar.

### Property Tax Considerations

Missouri residential property tax assessments vary by county. For rental property, assessor's offices classify the property as "residential rental" (typically 12% of assessed value). With an effective rate of **1.01% statewide**, a $300,000 rental home incurs approximately $3,030 in annual property tax.

Missouri allows rental property owners to appeal assessments through the county assessor's office, with a deadline typically 30 days after assessment notice. This deduction directly reduces your Missouri taxable income.

### Withholding on FIRPTA Sales

If you sell the Missouri property and the buyer does not withhold 15% under FIRPTA, you remain liable for the full federal and state tax. When you file Form 8288-B with your ITIN, report the sale date, property address, sale price, and any amounts withheld. If overwitheld, you can claim a refund on your 1040-NR.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Incomplete Documentation**
The IRS rejects applications with missing supporting documents. Always photocopy both sides of your passport and include proof of Canadian residency (T1 return or Notice of Assessment).

**2. Mismatched Name Spelling**
Ensure your name on Form W-7 matches your passport exactly. Any variance (e.g., "John James Smith" vs. "J. James Smith") triggers rejection and reprocessing delays.

**3. Applying Too Late**
Apply for the ITIN *before* filing your first 1040-NR or *immediately* when you become aware of a planned property sale. Filing both the W-7 and 1040-NR simultaneously can delay processing of both documents.

**4. Not Using ITIN on Follow-Up Returns**
Once issued, always use your ITIN on subsequent 1040-NR and Missouri MO-1040(NR) filings. The IRS cross-references ITINs annually; failing to use it consistently flags returns for additional scrutiny.

**5. Failing to Report FIRPTA Sales**
If you receive FIRPTA withholding but never file Form 8288-B or report the sale on your 1040-NR, the IRS will issue notices and demand payment. Always file Form 8288-B when Missouri property is sold.
