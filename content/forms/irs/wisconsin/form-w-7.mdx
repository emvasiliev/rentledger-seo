# Form W-7: ITIN Application Guide for Canadian Landlords with Wisconsin Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the official IRS application used by non-US citizens and non-resident aliens to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted like a Social Security Number (SSN) but used exclusively for tax reporting and compliance purposes when you don't qualify for an SSN.

For Canadian landlords, the ITIN serves as your US tax identifier when filing Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) and is essential for Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) withholding compliance on property sales.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Wisconsin

As a Canadian resident who owns rental property in Wisconsin, you are classified as a **non-resident alien** for US federal tax purposes. This classification triggers several US tax obligations:

**Federal Requirements:**
- File Form 1040-NR annually to report US-source rental income
- Report gross rental receipts, deductible expenses (mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, utilities), and net taxable income
- Claim treaty-based benefits under the Canada-US Tax Treaty to potentially reduce withholding taxes
- Comply with FIRPTA rules if you sell the property

**Wisconsin State Requirements:**
- File Wisconsin Form 1NPR (Nonresident and Part-Year Resident Income Tax Return) because Wisconsin taxes non-residents on Wisconsin-source income
- Wisconsin applies a flat 7.65% income tax rate on your net rental income
- Wisconsin also taxes property tax deductions differently for non-residents; you must include Wisconsin property taxes paid as a deduction on your 1040-NR before calculating Wisconsin taxable income

**FIRPTA Implications:**
When you eventually sell your Wisconsin property, the buyer's agent or closing attorney must withhold 15% of the sale price as FIRPTA withholding (under Internal Revenue Code §1445). Your ITIN is required to file Form 8288-B (Statement of FIRPTA Withholding) and claim any excess withholding on your tax return.

## Who Must File Form W-7?

You must file Form W-7 if:

- You are a Canadian resident (non-US citizen)
- You own Wisconsin rental property generating US-source income
- You do not have a US Social Security Number
- You need to file Form 1040-NR to report rental income
- You expect to claim FIRPTA withholding credits or file related tax forms

You do **not** need Form W-7 if:
- You hold a valid US Social Security Number
- You have previously obtained an ITIN (file Form W-7 only once; reuse the same ITIN for future returns)

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### **Part I: Identification Information**

**Line 1a–1c (Name and Date of Birth):**
Enter your full legal name exactly as it appears on your Canadian passport or government-issued ID. Use the English spelling of your name. Enter your date of birth in MM/DD/YYYY format.

**Line 1d (Country of Citizenship):**
Enter "Canada."

**Line 1e (Type of US Visa):**
Since you are a Canadian resident without a US visa, select "None (Resident of Canada)." Do not claim a visa category.

**Line 1f (Passport Number and Country):**
Enter your Canadian passport number and select "Canada" as the issuing country. Your passport is your primary identification document for ITIN purposes.

### **Part II: Mailing Address**

**Line 2a–2d (Address in the US or US Territory):**
If you have a US address, enter it here. Many Canadian landlords use their Wisconsin property address or a US property management company's address. If you have no US address, enter your Canadian address and note "Canada" as the country.

**Line 3 (Mailing Address):**
Provide the address where the IRS should send your ITIN. This is typically your Canadian home address unless you prefer correspondence at your Wisconsin address.

### **Part III: Reason for Application**

**Line 4 (Reason You Are Applying):**
Select the reason that applies:
- **"Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return"** — This is the primary box for rental income filers
- **"US source income required to file (FIRPTA or other withholding)"** — Check this if you're applying primarily for FIRPTA withholding purposes

Since you own Wisconsin rental property, check the first box. You may check both if you anticipate selling the property soon.

**Line 5 (Check if you or your spouse is requesting an ITIN for dependent purposes only):**
Leave blank unless claiming dependent children.

### **Part IV: US Residency Status**

**Line 6 (Resident Alien or Non-Resident Alien Status):**
Select "Non-Resident Alien." This confirms your tax classification.

**Line 7 (Expected US Income and Tax Obligation):**
Indicate you expect US-source rental income. Provide a brief description: "Rental income from Wisconsin property" and estimate your annual gross rental income (if known).

### **Part V: Declaration and Signature**

**Line 8 (Signature and Date):**
Sign and date the form. The signature must match your government-issued ID (passport). If your spouse is applying jointly, both parties sign.

**Line 9 (Attestation by IRS Employee or Authorized Representative):**
Leave blank; the IRS will complete this section.

## Wisconsin-Specific Considerations

### **State Income Tax Integration**

Wisconsin's 7.65% tax rate applies to your net Wisconsin-source rental income. When you file Form 1040-NR with your ITIN, you must also file Wisconsin Form 1NPR. Your ITIN serves as your identifier on both federal and state returns.

**Key Point:** Wisconsin property tax payments are deductible on your federal 1040-NR before calculating Wisconsin taxable income. However, Wisconsin does not allow a separate state property tax deduction; the deduction flows through your federal calculation.

### **Average Property Tax Rate in Wisconsin**

Wisconsin's average effective property tax rate is 1.76% of property value. This is a significant deduction for landlords. Factor this into your expense calculations when filing Form 1040-NR Schedule E (Rental Real Estate and Royalty Income).

### **Treaty Benefits**

Under the Canada-US Tax Treaty (specifically Article XXIII), you may claim reduced withholding on certain US-source income. An ITIN alone does not automatically grant treaty benefits; you must file Form W-8BEN (Certificate of Foreign Status of Beneficial Owner) with your tenant's payor (if applicable) or claim benefits on Form 1040-NR.

### **Foreign Tax Credit on Canadian Return (T1)**

Once you file Form 1040-NR and pay US federal and Wisconsin state taxes, you report these payments to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) on your Canadian T1 return. You can claim a non-resident tax credit for foreign taxes paid on Schedule 1. This prevents double taxation on the same rental income.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Submitting Form W-7 Without Required Documentation**
The IRS requires certified copies of your Canadian passport (front and back) or government-issued ID. Mail certified originals or certified copies; photocopies alone are insufficient. Your document must be certified by a notary public or a foreign government official.

**2. Incorrect Visa or Residency Status Selection**
Do not select "Student Visa," "Work Visa," or other categories if you don't hold them. Select "None (Resident of Canada)" for clarity that you're a Canadian resident without US immigration status.

**3. Omitting the Reason for Application**
The IRS must understand why you need an ITIN. Always specify "Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return" and describe your Wisconsin rental income.

**4. Filing W-7 Prematurely or Without Intent to File**
File Form W-7 concurrently with or immediately before filing Form 1040-NR. Filing W-7 without intent to file a US return may trigger IRS inquiry.

**5. Using an Incorrect Address**
If you list a US address, ensure it is current. The IRS mails ITINs to the address provided; delayed or incorrect mail results in delayed compliance.

**6. Assuming ITIN Approval Equals Filing Obligation**
Obtaining an ITIN does not create a US tax filing requirement—your ownership of Wisconsin property and generation of rental income does. However, once you have an ITIN, you must file Form 1040-NR annually while you own the property
