# Form W-7: ITIN Application Guide for Canadian Landlords in Vermont

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is an IRS form that allows non-US citizens and non-resident aliens to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted like a Social Security Number (SSN) but designated specifically for tax compliance purposes.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves two critical functions:
1. **Tax filing identification**: Required to file Form 1040-NR (U.S. Income Tax Return for Nonresident Aliens) with the IRS
2. **FIRPTA compliance**: Necessary for satisfying Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) withholding obligations when selling US rental property

Without an ITIN, the IRS will not process your US tax returns, and you cannot properly document your tax position on Vermont rental income.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Vermont

### The Vermont Tax Landscape

Vermont imposes state income tax on rental income at a top marginal rate of 8.75%, plus property taxes averaging 1.9% of assessed value. As a non-resident of Vermont, you must file:

- **Federal**: Form 1040-NR (US Income Tax Return for Nonresident Aliens)
- **State**: Vermont Form AT-107 (Nonresident Income Tax Return) or Form IT-203 (if applicable)

Both require a valid taxpayer identification number—either an SSN or ITIN.

### The Canada-US Tax Treaty Connection

The Canada-US Tax Treaty provides important relief mechanisms for Canadian residents. Article 15 (Personal Services Income) and Article 21 (Other Income) detail how rental income from Vermont property is taxed. However, obtaining an ITIN does not exempt you from US taxation; rather, it ensures you file correctly and can claim treaty benefits where applicable.

Canadian landlords can claim **foreign tax credits** on their Canadian T1 return for US taxes paid on Vermont rental income, reducing double taxation. But you must first file the US return with a valid ITIN to generate the tax records needed for this credit.

### Popular Among Quebec Landlords

Vermont's proximity to Quebec makes it an attractive investment location. Quebec residents often own Vermont residential or commercial rental properties. If you own property within Vermont's borders—regardless of residency—you must obtain an ITIN if you lack an SSN.

## Who Files Form W-7?

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

- A Canadian citizen or resident without a US Social Security Number
- Required to file a US federal income tax return (Form 1040-NR for rental income)
- Subject to FIRPTA withholding on a property sale
- Seeking to establish US tax compliance and reporting

**You do NOT need an ITIN if:**
- You hold a valid US Social Security Number
- You are a US citizen or green card holder

## Step-by-Step Guide to Completing Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, collect:

- Your **Canadian passport** (or other valid national identification)
- **Proof of Canadian residency** (utility bill, provincial health card, or tax return)
- **IRS Form W-7** (latest version from IRS.gov)
- Details on why you need the ITIN (rental income from Vermont property)

### Step 2: Complete Section 1 – Personal Information

Enter your full legal name, date of birth, and country of citizenship (Canada). Use your Canadian mailing address. This is where you establish your non-resident alien status.

### Step 3: Complete Section 2 – Type of Applicant

Select **Box c: "Other Nonresident Alien Individual"** for rental property ownership. You may also check **Box d** if you anticipate filing Form 1040-NR to report rental income.

The IRS uses this information to determine your ITIN category and validity period.

### Step 4: Complete Section 3 – Identification Details

Provide your Canadian SIN (Social Insurance Number) in the appropriate field, clearly marked. This helps the IRS link your records to Canadian tax authorities if needed.

### Step 5: Complete Section 4 – Reason for Application

In the "Reason You Are Applying for an ITIN" section, clearly state:
- **"Non-resident alien filing US federal income tax return (Form 1040-NR) to report rental income from Vermont real estate"**
- Optionally reference: **"FIRPTA withholding compliance and future property disposition"**

Be specific. Vague descriptions delay processing.

### Step 6: Sign and Certify

Sign and date Form W-7. The signature must match the name on your passport. If you use a representative (tax accountant or CPA), they must also sign Part 4.

### Step 7: Submit with Supporting Documents

**File by mail only** (no electronic filing for W-7). Include:

1. **Completed Form W-7**
2. **Certified copy of your passport** (copy certified by a notary public, US embassy/consulate, or certain financial institutions)
3. **Proof of residency** in Canada
4. **Cover letter** explaining your Vermont rental property

Mail to the IRS ITIN Unit:

**Internal Revenue Service  
ITIN Operation  
Austin, TX 73301  
USA**

Processing time: **4–6 weeks** after receipt. Plan accordingly.

## Vermont-Specific Considerations

### State Tax Nexus and Form AT-107

Once you obtain your ITIN, Vermont will require you to file **Form AT-107** (Vermont Nonresident Income Tax Return) if your Vermont-source rental income exceeds $400 in a tax year. Your ITIN must appear on this state return as well.

Vermont's 8.75% top rate, combined with federal taxation, means effective rates on Vermont rental income can exceed 30% when combined with Canada's inclusion rate for foreign investment income.

### Property Tax Implications

Vermont property taxes average **1.9% of assessed value**. This is a deductible expense on both your Form 1040-NR (Schedule E) and your Canadian T1 return, reducing net rental income calculations on both sides of the border.

### FIRPTA and Future Property Sales

If you plan to sell Vermont rental property, FIRPTA requires a **15% withholding** on the gross sale price (or lower if you qualify for an exemption). Your ITIN is essential for proper FIRPTA documentation and ensuring withholding is handled correctly. Without an ITIN, withholding complications can delay closing or create escrow disputes.

### Quebec Landlords: Currency Considerations

Many Quebec landlords report rental income in Canadian dollars on their T1 return. The IRS requires USD reporting on Form 1040-NR. Use the average exchange rate for the tax year when converting. Keep supporting documentation of conversion rates used.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Submitting an uncertified photocopy of your passport**: The IRS rejects these. Use a certified copy only.

2. **Vague "reason for application"**: Simply stating "tax return filing" without mentioning Vermont rental property delays processing. Be specific.

3. **Forgetting to sign Form W-7**: An unsigned form is returned unprocessed.

4. **Filing W-7 after Form 1040-NR**: Apply for your ITIN **before** filing your first US tax return. Submitting 1040-NR without an ITIN causes rejection.

5. **Not keeping copies**: Retain copies of everything you mail to the IRS. ITIN files can be lost in transit.

6. **Mismatched names**: Ensure your name on Form W-7 matches your passport exactly, including spelling and accent marks.
