## Rental Property Tax Guide: Ontario Residents Owning in West Virginia

If you're an Ontario resident generating rental income from a property in West Virginia, you're navigating a complex three-way tax system: Canadian federal and provincial rules, US federal regulations, and West Virginia state requirements. Each jurisdiction views your rental income differently, and missing obligations in any one can trigger penalties, lost deductions, or excessive withholding. This guide walks you through your filing obligations, tax rates, and practical compliance steps.

## Why This Combination Matters

West Virginia is a low-tax state relative to many US jurisdictions, but the real complexity arises from **cross-border withholding**. If you don't properly notify the IRS and CRA, you could face:

- **Part XIII withholding** of 25% on gross rents by the tenant's paying agent under Canadian rules
- **US federal withholding** of 30% on gross rents under Section 1446 (FIRPTA-like rules for non-residents)
- **Double taxation** on the same income if you don't claim foreign tax credits correctly

The key to minimizing this is understanding which agency has jurisdiction, which forms to file, and **when** to file them to prevent automatic withholding.

## Canadian Tax Obligations: CRA Filing Requirements

### Reporting on Form T776

As an Ontario resident, you must report all worldwide rental income to the CRA, including your West Virginia property. Use **Form T776 (Statement of Real Estate Rentals)** to report:

- **Gross rents** in Canadian dollars (converted at the Bank of Canada average annual rate; 1 USD = 1.36 CAD for 2025 examples)
- **Allowable expenses**: mortgage interest, property tax, insurance, repairs, property management fees, condo fees (if applicable), utilities, advertising
- **Capital cost allowance (CCA)** if you choose to claim depreciation on the building

**Critical point**: Do not report net income only. The CRA requires a detailed breakdown of gross income and expenses on T776. If a US paying agent withholds 25% or 30%, you'll still report the full gross rent on T776 and claim the withheld amount as a foreign tax credit (discussed below).

### Foreign Property Information: Form T1135

If your West Virginia property's fair market value exceeds **CAD $100,000** at any time during the tax year, you must file **Form T1135 (Foreign Property Declaration)** by your tax filing deadline (June 15 for individuals, though tax is due April 30).

Report:
- **Adjusted cost basis** in CAD
- **Fair market value** in CAD
- Type of property (rental real estate)
- Country of location (United States)

Failure to file T1135 when required triggers a **$2,500 minimum penalty**, plus potential 5% penalties on underreported income.

### Part XIII Withholding and the NR6 Certificate

If you have not filed an **NR6 certificate** with CRA, a US tenant's paying agent may remit 25% of your gross rental income directly to the CRA as Part XIII withholding. This is automatic if you're identified as a non-resident.

**To avoid 25% withholding**, you can file an NR6 certificate (also called a "Non-Resident Withholding Tax Exemption Certificate") with CRA. This requests exemption from Part XIII withholding if you've filed Canadian returns and are in good standing. Processing typically takes 4–6 weeks. Once approved, you receive a certificate number to provide to the paying agent.

**Note**: Filing NR6 does not exempt you from US federal withholding (30%) unless you file the appropriate election with the IRS (see below).

## US Federal Tax Obligations: IRS Filing Requirements

### Obtaining an ITIN

To file a US tax return as a non-resident alien (NRA), you must first obtain an **Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)**. Apply using **Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number)** at a US embassy or consulate in Canada, or by mail to the IRS.

Processing takes 4–8 weeks. Your ITIN is required on all subsequent US tax filings and for the Section 871(d) election (below).

### Form 1040-NR: Your US Tax Return

File **Form 1040-NR (U.S. Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return)** annually if you have US-source rental income. Key elements:

- **File by April 15** of the following year (same as US residents)
- Report all West Virginia rental income and expenses on **Schedule E (Supplemental Income and Loss)**
- Claim deductions for mortgage interest, property tax, insurance, repairs, utilities, property management fees
- **Do not claim personal exemptions**; non-residents receive no standard deduction for unrelated US income

Your taxable US rental income is subject to **US federal tax at graduated rates** (10% to 37% brackets for 2024/2025), **plus**:

- **3.8% Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)** on rental income if your modified adjusted gross income exceeds USD $125,000 (married filing separately) or USD $200,000 (single/other)
- **Self-employment tax does not apply** to rental income (rental real estate is passive)

### Section 871(d) Election: Avoid 30% Withholding

By default, US payers are required to withhold 30% on gross rental income paid to non-residents. However, you can file **Form 8288-B (Statement of Tax Liability Against U.S. Real Property Interests)** with the IRS to make an election under **Section 871(d)**.

This election allows you to:

- **Opt into the normal tax system** instead of the flat 30% withholding
- File Form 1040-NR and pay tax only on **net income** (after deductions), which is typically far less than 30% of gross
- Reduce or eliminate withholding through proper estimated tax payments

**Timing is critical**: File Form 8288-B **before the property pays you rent**. Ideally, file it in conjunction with your first 1040-NR or as soon as you acquire the property.

Without this election, 30% is withheld on every rental payment, and you'd recover the excess only when filing your annual return—creating significant cash flow delays.

### Schedule E: Documenting Deductions

On Schedule E, itemize:

- **Mortgage interest** (not principal)
- **Real estate taxes** (West Virginia property tax; see below)
- **Insurance** (landlord/hazard insurance)
- **Repairs and maintenance** (capital improvements go on Form 4562 for depreciation)
- **Utilities and HOA fees** (if you pay them)
- **Property management** fees and accounting/tax preparation fees related to the US property
- **Advertising** (if you list the property yourself)

**Do not claim:**
- Depreciation (CCA) on Form 1040-NR unless you file an election; most cross-border owners avoid this due to recapture tax complications
- Mortgage principal repayment
- Capital improvements (these are depreciated over time)

## West Virginia State Tax Obligations

### State Income Tax Return

West Virginia requires non-residents to file a state income tax return if they have West Virginia-source rental income, **even if they have no federal tax liability**. The state uses a **6.5% flat income tax rate** on net rental income.

File **Form IT-140 (West Virginia Individual Income Tax Return)** by **April 15** (same deadline as federal/Canada). Report:

- **Gross rental income** from your WV property
- **Deductions**: same as federal (mortgage interest, property tax, insurance, repairs, etc.)
- **Net income** multiplied by **6.5%** = WV state tax owed

**Key point**: You will not owe West Virginia state tax if your property expenses exceed your gross rents (a loss year).

### West Virginia Property Tax

West Virginia's effective property tax rate averages **0.59% of assessed value** statewide, though rates vary by county. For example:

- A USD $300,000 property in a median county: approximately USD $1,770/year
- Convert to CAD at 1.36: approximately CAD $2,409/year

Property taxes are due annually (usually by September 1) and are fully deductible on both your IRS Schedule E and your CRA Form T776.
