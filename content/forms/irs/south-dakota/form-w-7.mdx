# Form W-7: ITIN Application Guide for Canadian Landlords in South Dakota

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is an IRS application that assigns a unique nine-digit Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) to non-US citizens who cannot obtain a Social Security Number but have US tax filing requirements.

An ITIN functions identically to a Social Security Number (SSN) for federal tax purposes—it appears on your US tax return and is required for reporting income to the IRS. For Canadian landlords, the ITIN becomes your permanent US tax identifier and remains valid as long as you file a US tax return at least once every three years.

The ITIN format is 9XX-7X-XXXX (always beginning with 9).

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords with South Dakota Rental Property

### Why You Need an ITIN

As a Canadian resident owning rental property in South Dakota, you are a non-resident alien for US tax purposes. The US taxes worldwide rental income on US real estate at ordinary tax rates (currently 10% to 37% federal brackets) plus a 3.8% net investment income tax on certain high-income filers.

You have two primary triggers requiring an ITIN:

**1. Tax Filing Requirement**  
You must file Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) to report:
- Gross rental income from your South Dakota property
- Deductible rental expenses (mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, repairs, property management fees, depreciation)
- Capital gains or losses if you eventually sell the property

**2. FIRPTA Withholding Requirement**  
Under the Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA), when you eventually sell your South Dakota property, the buyer's closing agent must withhold 15% of the gross sales price and remit it to the IRS as estimated tax. To claim a refund of excess withholding or to file a return reporting the actual gain, you need an ITIN.

### South Dakota's Tax Advantage

South Dakota imposes **no state income tax**, which is a significant advantage compared to neighboring states. This means:
- All your rental income tax liability is federal only
- Your total effective tax burden on South Dakota rental income is determined entirely by federal brackets and deductions
- You have no state-level compliance burden in South Dakota (though you may have obligations in your home province in Canada)

However, South Dakota property taxes average **1.22% of assessed value annually**—a moderate rate that ranks in the middle nationally. These property taxes are fully deductible on your Form 1040-NR.

## Who Files Form W-7

You file Form W-7 if you:
- Do not have and cannot obtain a US Social Security Number
- Have a US tax filing requirement (such as rental income from South Dakota property)
- Are a non-resident alien (not a US citizen or permanent resident)
- Need the ITIN for US federal tax purposes

**You do NOT need Form W-7 if:**
- You already have an ITIN (you cannot apply for a new one; reuse your existing number)
- You have a valid Social Security Number

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete and File Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing W-7, assemble:

- **Valid passport or national ID** (color copy, front and back)
  - Canada: Valid Canadian passport is acceptable
  - Document must not be expired; expiration date must be current or within 6 months of application
  
- **Proof of US tax filing requirement**
  - For rental property owners: Form 1040-NR (if filing simultaneously) or correspondence from IRS showing you owe US tax
  - A copy of your lease agreement or property deed showing you own South Dakota property works as supporting evidence
  
- **Proof of foreign residency** (if applicable)
  - Canadian T1 General Return (most recent year)
  - Canadian driver's license (copy)
  - Residency certificate from your province of residence

- **Completed Form W-7** (signed and dated; electronic signatures accepted)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7

**Part I – Personal Information**
- Line 1a: Enter your full name exactly as it appears on your passport
- Line 1b: Enter your date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY format)
- Line 1c: Check "Individual" (not business)
- Line 1d: Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)

**Part II – Mailing Address**
- Enter your current residential address (use your Canadian address)
- Do NOT use a PO box; IRS requires a physical street address

**Part III – US Residency**
- Check "No" (you are not a US resident)
- Line 3: Check "Nonresident alien" or "Foreign student," depending on your status

**Part IV – Reason for Applying**
- Check the box that applies: typically "Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return" (this applies to you as a rental property owner)
- If filing to satisfy FIRPTA withholding, you may also check "Required to file a return" or specify FIRPTA-related purpose in writing on the form

**Part V – Declaration**
- Sign and date the form (must be notarized or signed under penalty of perjury with an "X" mark if you cannot sign)
- Print your name below the signature line

### Step 3: Attach Supporting Documents

Attach color copies (front and back) of:
1. Your passport (primary ID)
2. Proof of tax filing requirement (Form 1040-NR draft or property deed)
3. Proof of Canadian residency (recent T1 or provincial ID)

Do NOT include original documents—the IRS requires copies only.

### Step 4: Submit Your Application

**Mail to the IRS:**

For individuals with foreign addresses:
```
Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operations
P.O. Box 149342
Austin, TX 78714-9342
USA
```

**OR file electronically:**
- If you are filing Form 1040-NR simultaneously, you can attach Form W-7 to your 1040-NR return filed through an authorized e-file provider
- This is the fastest and preferred method

Allow 4–8 weeks for processing if mailing. Electronic submission with a 1040-NR typically processes within 4 weeks.

## South Dakota-Specific Considerations

### No State Income Tax Filing
Unlike states such as Minnesota, Montana, or Wyoming, South Dakota does **not** require state income tax returns. This simplifies your compliance—you file **federal Form 1040-NR only**, not a state return.

However, verify whether your county or municipality (such as Sioux Falls or Rapid City) imposes any local property tax or rental license requirements. Most do not, but some jurisdictions require a business license for property management. Check with your South Dakota county assessor.

### Property Tax Deduction
South Dakota's average effective property tax rate of 1.22% means on a $500,000 property, you'll deduct approximately $6,100 annually in property taxes on Form 1040-NR (Schedule A for non-residents, or directly on the 1040-NR rental income section).

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Benefit
Under Article XXII of the Canada-US Income Tax Treaty, as a Canadian resident, you may be entitled to:
- Deductions and credits allowed to US residents (rental expense deductions, depreciation, mortgage interest)
- Possible reduction of withholding taxes on rental income under Article X

You can claim a foreign tax credit on your Canadian T1 return for US federal income taxes paid on your South Dakota rental income, reducing double taxation.

### FIRPTA and the 15% Withholding
When you sell your South Dakota property, the buyer's closing agent must withhold 15% of the gross sales price (not the net gain). Your ITIN is critical for:
- Receiving a withholding certificate from the IRS if you can prove tax liability is less than 15%
- Filing a US tax return after sale to claim a refund of excess withholding

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Submitting an expired or invalid ID**
   - Ensure your passport is current or expiring within 6 months. Expired documents are rejected outright.

   - Type the form when possible. Illegible entries delay processing by 2–4 weeks.
