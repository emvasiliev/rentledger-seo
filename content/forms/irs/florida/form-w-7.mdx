# Form W-7: ITIN Guide for Canadian Landlords with Florida Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the official IRS application to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit identification number issued by the IRS to individuals who are required to have a US taxpayer identification number but are not eligible for a Social Security Number (SSN).

For Canadian landlords, the ITIN serves as your official US tax identification number. It allows you to file US income tax returns, comply with FIRPTA withholding obligations, and meet other IRS requirements related to US-source income.

## Why Canadian Landlords Need an ITIN

As a Canadian resident earning rental income from Florida property, you are classified as a **non-resident alien** for US federal tax purposes. The IRS requires you to:

1. **File Form 1040-NR** (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) to report US-source rental income
2. **Comply with FIRPTA** (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding rules if you sell the property
3. **Report income and expenses** from your Florida rental to the IRS annually
4. **Potentially file Florida documentary stamp tax** returns on property acquisitions

Without an ITIN, you cannot file these required forms or ensure withholding agents (like title companies in a property sale) know where to report your tax information.

## How ITIN Applies Specifically to Florida Landlords

### Florida's Tax-Friendly Environment

Florida stands out as one of the most popular US states for Canadian real estate investors, particularly those from Ontario and Quebec. This preference is driven by several factors:

- **No Florida State Income Tax**: Unlike most US states, Florida does not impose a state income tax. This means your rental income is subject only to federal US taxation—not additional state-level taxation.
- **Property Tax Rate**: Florida's average effective property tax rate is 0.89%, which is relatively modest compared to other US states. Property taxes are calculated on the assessed value of your property and vary slightly by county.
- **Proximity and Demand**: Florida's geographic proximity to Canada, combined with strong tourist and residential demand, makes it an attractive rental market.

### FIRPTA Implications in Florida

When you eventually sell your Florida rental property, FIRPTA requires the buyer's title company or attorney to withhold 15% of the gross sale price and remit it to the IRS on your behalf. However, if you have an ITIN and file appropriate certifications, you may be able to reduce this withholding or claim a refund on your US tax return. Without an ITIN on file with the IRS, the withholding process becomes complicated and delays can occur.

### Integration with Canadian Tax Reporting

As a Canadian resident, you must also report your US rental income on your Canadian T1 return. The foreign income is subject to Canadian tax, but you can claim a **foreign tax credit** for US federal taxes paid (and potentially Florida property taxes). An ITIN ensures your US tax filings are properly linked to your identity, making the foreign tax credit process smoother when filed with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

- A non-resident alien (not a US citizen, national, or permanent resident)
- Required to file a US tax return (Form 1040-NR) to report rental income
- Subject to FIRPTA withholding on a property sale
- Filing certain forms that require a US taxpayer identification number (such as Schedule E for rental income)

**Canadian landlords with Florida rental property fall clearly into this category.**

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before starting Form W-7, assemble:

- **Passport or national identity document** (your Canadian passport)
- **Proof of foreign residency**: A Canadian driver's license, provincial health card, or CRA Notice of Assessment
- **Documentation of US tax requirement**: A copy of your Form 1040-NR (if filing) or FIRPTA withholding documentation from the property sale
- **Rental property documentation**: Deed, title, or purchase agreement for the Florida property

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Itself

The form has five main sections:

**Part I: Personal Information**
- Enter your full legal name as it appears on your passport
- Provide your date of birth
- Indicate your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Enter your current mailing address (your Canadian home address is standard)

**Part II: Mailing Address and Country Code**
- Provide both your Canadian home address and, if different, a US contact address (optional, but helpful if you manage the property remotely)
- Use the correct three-letter country code for Canada (CAN)

**Part III: Reason for Applying**
- Check the box: **"Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return"** (this applies to most Canadian landlords filing 1040-NR)
- Alternatively or additionally, check: **"Other (specify)"** and write "FIRPTA withholding purposes"

**Part IV: Country of Residence for Tax Purposes**
- Enter Canada
- Provide any applicable FTIN (Foreign Tax Identification Number) or Canadian tax identification. This is often your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN), though you may prefer not to include it. Some practitioners recommend omitting the SIN to avoid additional IRS-CRA information sharing, but this is optional.

**Part V: Signature**
- Sign and date the form
- Have your signature notarized by a notary public

### Step 3: Certification of Document

Form W-7 requires certified copies of your identity and residency documents. You have three options:

1. **US Embassy or Consulate Certification**: Have a Canadian consulate official certify your documents (available in major Canadian cities)
2. **IRS Office Certification**: Submit documents in person at an IRS office in the US
3. **Certified Public Accountant (CPA) or Tax Professional Certification**: A US-licensed CPA, enrolled agent, or tax attorney can certify your documents

Most Canadian landlords use option 3 for convenience, working with a cross-border tax professional.

## Florida-Specific Considerations

### Documentary Stamp Tax and Form W-7

When you purchase Florida rental property, you'll encounter **documentary stamp tax**—a tax on the deed transfer (typically ~0.60% of purchase price). This is not directly related to Form W-7, but it's part of Florida real estate costs you should budget for alongside your federal and Canadian tax obligations.

### No State Income Tax Filing Requirement

Unlike many US states, Florida does not require non-resident aliens to file a state income tax return. Your only US filing obligation is the federal Form 1040-NR. This simplifies your annual reporting compared to owning property in states like New York, California, or Texas.

### County Property Tax Records

Florida is a public records state, and property taxes are assessed at the county level. Once you hold title to Florida property, your name will appear in county property records. Ensure your ITIN is properly registered with the IRS before finalizing your purchase, as the IRS may cross-reference property records.

### Timing Relative to Property Acquisition

Ideally, apply for your ITIN **before** closing on your Florida property. This ensures your ITIN is issued and on file with the IRS by the time you receive your first property tax bill or rental income. However, applications can take 4–8 weeks, so plan accordingly.

## Common Mistakes Canadian Landlords Make

### Mistake 1: Filing Without an ITIN First
Many Canadian landlords attempt to file Form 1040-NR before receiving their ITIN. The IRS will reject returns filed without a valid identification number. **Always obtain your ITIN first.**

### Mistake 2: Incomplete Document Certification
Submitting photocopies of your passport without proper notarization or professional certification is a common reason for ITIN application rejection. Invest in proper certification upfront.

### Mistake 3: Forgetting FIRPTA Obligations on Sale
Assuming an ITIN is only for annual rental income reporting is a mistake. Many Canadian landlords are surprised when selling to learn they needed an ITIN on file months or years earlier for FIRPTA withholding purposes. File W-7 proactively, even if you don't plan to sell soon.

### Mistake 4: Not Reporting the ITIN to the CRA
Once you receive your ITIN, provide it to the Canada Revenue Agency on your T1 return. This ensures proper integration of your foreign income reporting and prevents future CRA inquiries.
