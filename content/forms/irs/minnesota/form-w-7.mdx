# Form W-7: ITIN Application for Canadian Landlords with Minnesota Rental Property

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7, *Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number*, is the official IRS form used to request an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number issued by the Internal Revenue Service to individuals who are not eligible for a U.S. Social Security Number (SSN) but have a U.S. tax filing requirement.

For Canadian landlords, the ITIN serves a dual critical purpose: it allows you to file U.S. federal income tax returns (Form 1040-NR for non-residents), and it is mandatory for FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) compliance—specifically when rental properties are sold or when withholding agents need to identify you.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Minnesota Rental Property Owners

### Federal Requirements

As a Canadian non-resident owning rental property in Minnesota, you must obtain an ITIN to:

1. **File Form 1040-NR (U.S. Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return)** to report rental income, depreciation deductions, and mortgage interest paid on the Minnesota property.
2. **Comply with FIRPTA withholding** (Internal Revenue Code Section 897). When you eventually sell the Minnesota property, the buyer's closing attorney must withhold 15% of the net sale proceeds unless you obtain a certificate of non-foreign status or provide your ITIN to establish non-foreign status eligibility review.
3. **Meet IRS reporting requirements** for foreign persons engaged in a U.S. trade or business (rental activity typically qualifies as a trade or business).

### Minnesota State Tax Implications

Minnesota requires non-resident individuals to file a Minnesota state income tax return (Form MN 1040) if they have Minnesota-source income, including rental income. Minnesota's top marginal tax rate is **9.85%**, applied to net rental income after deductions.

While Minnesota state tax filing does not strictly require an ITIN (you can use a Canadian passport number or other identification on the state return), obtaining an ITIN prevents complications:
- **Correspondence from both IRS and Minnesota Department of Revenue** will reference your ITIN.
- **Consistency across filings** simplifies record-keeping and reduces audit risk.
- **Property management companies and mortgage servicers** increasingly request ITINs for non-resident owners.

### Property Tax Context

Minnesota's average effective property tax rate is **1.12%** of assessed value (varies by county and municipality). While property tax is deductible against rental income on your federal return, it is not directly affected by ITIN status. However, obtaining an ITIN facilitates accurate expense documentation for U.S. tax purposes.

### Canada-U.S. Tax Treaty Considerations

Under the Canada-U.S. Tax Treaty (Article XXII), you are entitled to file as a non-resident for U.S. tax purposes while remaining a Canadian resident. The treaty provides specific rules on real property income:

- U.S. rental income is generally taxable in the U.S. (Article VI).
- You may claim a foreign tax credit on your Canadian T1 return for Minnesota state and federal taxes paid.
- The treaty's limitation-on-benefits provision does not typically restrict individual landlords' access to treaty relief.

Your ITIN is essential for establishing treaty-protected status and claiming any applicable treaty-based position disclosure (Form 8833) if relevant.

## Who Must File Form W-7

File Form W-7 if you meet **all** of the following criteria:

1. You are a non-U.S. citizen without an SSN.
2. You have a U.S. tax filing requirement (rental income from Minnesota property creates this).
3. You are not eligible for an SSN (Canadian citizens generally are not, unless they have lawful permanent resident status).

**Timeline:** You should apply for an ITIN **before** filing your first Form 1040-NR. Many Canadian landlords apply concurrently with their first year's return, but advance application prevents delays.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documents

Before completing Form W-7, assemble:

- **Original or certified copy of your passport** (Canadian passport required; you will need to provide this for IRS verification).
- **Original or certified copy of your birth certificate** (if not embedded in passport; notarized or certified copy acceptable).
- **Proof of U.S. tax filing requirement** (copy of your draft or filed Form 1040-NR showing Minnesota rental income).
- **ITIN Application Authorization Form (Form 8233)** if applying through an authorized representative (optional but recommended for cross-border filers).

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Sections

**Part I – Personal Information:**
- Line 1: Enter your full legal name as it appears on your Canadian passport.
- Line 2: Enter your date of birth.
- Line 3: Enter your country of citizenship: **Canada**.
- Line 4: Enter your mailing address (use a U.S. address if you have property management representation in Minnesota; otherwise use your Canadian address).
- Line 5: Select the reason for ITIN application. For rental property owners, select **"Other (specify)"** and write **"Non-resident rental property owner; FIRPTA compliance"** or **"Form 1040-NR filing requirement."**

**Part II – Type of U.S. Visa:**
- Select **"Other"** and specify non-immigrant status or non-resident alien status. Canadian citizens without U.S. visas should select this category.

**Part III – Application Type:**
- Select **"Initial ITIN application"** (unless you are reapplying after expiration, addressed in deadline section below).

**Part IV – Authorizing IRS Documents:**
- Attach a copy of your Form 1040-NR (pages 1 and 2) demonstrating tax filing requirement.
- If you have not yet completed your 1040-NR, attach a summary statement explaining: "Applicant owns rental property located in [Minnesota county], generating annual rental income subject to U.S. non-resident alien taxation."

### Step 3: Attach Supporting Documentation

Include:
1. Certified copy of your Canadian passport (photo page and signature page).
2. Notarized or certified copy of your birth certificate (if applicable).
3. Form 1040-NR (or draft return with property details).

### Step 4: Choose Your Filing Method

**Option A: Concurrent with Form 1040-NR**
- Attach Form W-7 to your Form 1040-NR when you file.
- Mail to: **IRS, ITIN Operation, P.O. Box 149342, Austin, TX 78714-9342** (for Form 1040-NR + W-7 combined).
- Processing time: 6–8 weeks (longer if documents require clarification).

**Option B: Advance ITIN Application**
- Submit Form W-7 with documents separately before filing your return.
- Mail to the same Austin address.
- Once approved, you receive your ITIN by mail; then file Form 1040-NR with the ITIN in subsequent steps.

**Option C: Authorized Representative**
- If using a U.S. tax professional or accounting firm, they may submit Form W-7 on your behalf using Form 8821 or Form 2848 (power of attorney).
- Recommended for cross-border filers to reduce IRS correspondence delays.

## Minnesota-Specific Considerations

### 1. Minnesota Department of Revenue Coordination

Once you receive your ITIN, file **Minnesota Form MN 1040 (Minnesota Resident/Non-Resident Income Tax Return)** using your ITIN. Minnesota does not require a separate state ITIN; however, the ITIN satisfies federal identification requirements and simplifies state correspondence.

### 2. Minnesota Rental Income Reporting

Minnesota requires reporting of gross rental income, and you may deduct:
- Mortgage interest on the Minnesota property.
- Property taxes (Minnesota's ~1.12% effective rate).
- Maintenance and repairs.
- Property management fees.
- Depreciation (claimed consistently with federal Form 1040-NR).

Your Minnesota return should align with federal deductions claimed on Form 1040-NR to avoid audit flags.

### 3. FIRPTA Withholding in Minnesota

If you sell Minnesota rental property, the buyer or closing attorney must withhold **15% of the net sale proceeds** unless:
- You obtain an IRS certificate of non-foreign status (available for some treaty-protected persons), or
- You provide your ITIN to the closing attorney for withholding agent review.
