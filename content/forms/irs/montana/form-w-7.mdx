# Form W-7 (ITIN Application) for Canadian Landlords with Montana Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN), is the official IRS form used to request an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number. An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted like a Social Security Number (SSN) but assigned exclusively to non-US citizens who have US tax filing obligations but cannot obtain an SSN.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves as your US tax identifier. It appears on your Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return), Schedule E (rental income reporting), and FIRPTA withholding documents when you sell the property. Without an ITIN, you cannot file US federal tax returns or comply with Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) requirements.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Montana

### Federal-Level Application

As a Canadian citizen earning rental income from Montana real estate, you are classified as a **non-resident alien** for US tax purposes. The US-Canada Income Tax Treaty (Article IV) confirms your non-resident status if you maintain primary residence in Canada.

You must obtain an ITIN to:
- File Form 1040-NR reporting your Montana rental income
- Claim deductions for mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, and depreciation
- Report capital gains if you sell the property
- Comply with FIRPTA withholding obligations (8% of sale price) when disposing of the property

### Montana State-Level Application

Montana imposes a **6.75% state income tax** on non-resident individuals' Montana-source income, including rental receipts. You must file Montana Form 2: **Non-Resident and Part-Year Resident Individual Income Tax Return**.

Montana does not issue its own ITINs; it accepts the federal ITIN for state tax identification purposes. Once you receive your federal ITIN from the IRS, use that same number on your Montana state return.

**Montana property tax consideration:** While not directly related to the W-7, understand that Montana's average effective property tax rate of **0.84%** will factor into your overall deduction portfolio on both the federal 1040-NR and the Montana Form 2.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

- A Canadian citizen or resident without a US Social Security Number
- Earning rental income, royalties, or other US-source income from Montana property
- Subject to FIRPTA withholding rules (planning to sell the property)
- Required to file Form 1040-NR or state return for Montana

You do **not** need to file W-7 if:
- You already hold a valid Social Security Number
- You are a US citizen or green card holder
- You have no US tax filing obligation

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documents

Before completing W-7, assemble:
- Valid Canadian passport (or equivalent government-issued ID)
- Proof of Canadian tax residency (CRA Notice of Assessment or Canadian tax return)
- US rental property documentation (deed, property tax statement)
- Documentation of US tax filing requirement (rental lease, property management agreement)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Itself

**Part I – Personal Information:**
- Line 1a: Enter your full legal name (as it appears on your passport)
- Line 1b: Enter your date of birth
- Line 1c: Select your country of citizenship: **Canada**
- Lines 2–4: Provide your current Canadian mailing address

**Part II – Type of Applicant:**
- Check box **c**: "Other Individual" (the standard category for foreign rental property owners)
- Line 6: Reason for applying – select **"Rental income from US real property"** or **"Other (specify)"** and write "Non-resident alien reporting rental income"

**Part III – Identification:**
- Provide your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN) in the line requesting a foreign tax ID number
- Do **not** leave this blank; the IRS uses it to cross-reference your Canadian tax filings

**Part IV – US Residency:**
- Confirm you are a **non-resident alien**
- Provide your Montana property address
- Enter the month/year you first earned Montana rental income

**Part V – Declaration:**
- Sign and date the form
- Have your signature notarized by a Canadian notary public (required for IRS submission)

### Step 3: Prepare Supporting Documents

Attach to Form W-7:
- A certified copy of your Canadian passport (photo page only)
- A certified copy of a recent Canadian tax return or CRA Notice of Assessment
- A copy of your US rental property deed or property tax assessment

**Certification requirement:** All documents must be certified copies, meaning they are photocopies stamped and signed by an authorized person (notary, accountant, or government official) confirming they are true and accurate reproductions of the original.

### Step 4: Determine Submission Method

**Option A – Apply with Your First Form 1040-NR:**
- Attach the W-7 and supporting documents to your Form 1040-NR
- File jointly with your tax return using the IRS mailing address provided in the 1040-NR instructions
- Processing time: 4–6 weeks after filing

**Option B – Submit Directly to the IRS ITIN Unit:**
- Mail the completed W-7 package to the IRS Austin Submission Processing Center (address provided in W-7 instructions)
- Processing time: 6–8 weeks
- You will receive an ITIN notice (Form CP-565) by mail

**Best practice for Canadian landlords:** Submit W-7 with your first Form 1040-NR and Montana Form 2 (filed together). The IRS will process all three documents simultaneously.

### Step 5: Receive Your ITIN

The IRS will mail you Form CP-565, Notice of ITIN Assignment, which displays your newly issued ITIN (formatted as 9XX-XX-XXXX). Use this ITIN on all future US tax filings and FIRPTA documents.

## Montana-Specific Considerations

### Montana Form 2 Filing

Once you have your ITIN, file **Montana Form 2** with the Montana Department of Revenue within the same tax year. Montana does not automatically recognize federal ITIN holders; you must affirmatively file the state return.

On Montana Form 2:
- Report your Montana rental income (gross rents received)
- Claim Montana mortgage interest, property tax, and depreciation deductions (same as federal)
- Apply Montana's 6.75% tax rate to taxable rental income
- Claim a foreign tax credit if you pay Canadian income tax on the same rental income (Article XXIV, US-Canada Tax Treaty)

### Foreign Tax Credit Coordination

As a Canadian resident, you may owe Canadian income tax on the same Montana rental income. The US-Canada Tax Treaty allows you to claim a **foreign tax credit** on Form 1040-NR (line 48) to avoid double taxation.

On your Canadian T1 return:
- Report the Montana rental income in Canadian dollars (converted at the exchange rate on the date received)
- Pay Canadian federal and provincial tax
- On your US 1040-NR, claim Form 1118 (Foreign Tax Credit) to offset the US tax owed

**Example:** You earn $50,000 CAD in Montana rental income. You pay $11,250 USD in US federal tax (21% rate) and $3,375 CAD in Canadian tax (~27% combined federal-provincial). You can claim up to $3,375 CAD (converted to USD) as a foreign tax credit on Form 1040-NR.

### Property Tax Treatment

Montana property taxes (averaging 0.84% of assessed value) are deductible on both your US 1040-NR (Schedule E, Line 8) and your Montana Form 2. Do not double-deduct. Use the same property tax amount on both returns.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: Submitting Uncertified Documents
Many Canadian landlords mail photocopies of their passport or tax return to the IRS without certification. The IRS will reject the application and return it. All documents must be certified by a notary or comparable authority.

### Mistake #2: Using Your SIN as Your ITIN
Your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN) is not the same as a US ITIN. The W-7 asks for a foreign tax ID number for cross-reference only. Do not attempt to use your SIN as a US identifier on tax forms.
