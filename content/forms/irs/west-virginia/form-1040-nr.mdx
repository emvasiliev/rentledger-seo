# Form 1040-NR for Canadian Landlords with West Virginia Rental Property

## What is Form 1040-NR?

Form 1040-NR (U.S. Income Tax Return for Nonresident Alien Individuals) is the primary federal income tax return filed by non-resident aliens—including Canadian citizens—who have effectively connected income (ECI) from U.S. sources. For rental property owners, this form becomes necessary when you elect to treat your U.S. rental income as effectively connected with a U.S. trade or business under Section 871(d) of the Internal Revenue Code.

Without this election, your rental income would typically be subject to a flat 30% withholding tax with minimal deduction allowances. By filing Form 1040-NR and making the Section 871(d) election, you can deduct legitimate rental expenses—mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, management fees, and depreciation—just like U.S. landlords do on Schedule E of Form 1040.

## How Form 1040-NR Applies to West Virginia Rental Property

West Virginia presents a specific tax environment for non-resident alien landlords. Understanding both the federal and state implications is critical for compliance and tax planning.

### Federal Level

When you own rental property in West Virginia, your rental income is U.S.-source income. The IRS treats it as effectively connected income once you make the Section 871(d) election. This means:

- You must file Form 1040-NR federally
- You can deduct all ordinary and necessary rental expenses
- You can claim depreciation on the building (but not land)
- You become subject to federal income tax at graduated rates (currently 10%–37% depending on income level)
- West Virginia rental income is fully subject to federal tax

### West Virginia State Level

West Virginia imposes its own income tax on non-resident aliens with rental property located in the state. Key details:

- **Tax rate**: West Virginia's personal income tax is progressive, ranging from 3% to 6.5% on ordinary income. Rental income is taxed at the top marginal rate of **6.5%** for most landlords
- **Non-resident return requirement**: You must file West Virginia Form IT-40NR (West Virginia Income Tax Return for Nonresidents), not the resident form
- **Apportionment**: Only income attributable to West Virginia property is subject to state tax
- **Property tax**: West Virginia's effective property tax rate averages 0.59% (among the lowest in the nation), but individual counties vary; this is deductible on your federal return

The good news: West Virginia has relatively low property taxes compared to other states, reducing your overall state tax burden.

## Who Must File Form 1040-NR for West Virginia Rentals

You must file Form 1040-NR if:

1. You are a non-resident alien (Canadian citizen) for U.S. tax purposes
2. You own rental property generating income in West Virginia
3. You have made (or intend to make) the Section 871(d) election to treat rental income as effectively connected income
4. Your gross rental income meets IRS filing thresholds (generally $12,550 or more for 2024, adjusted annually)

**Important**: Even if your income is below the threshold, filing may be strategically advantageous to claim refundable credits or to establish a compliance record, particularly if you have U.S. withholding.

## Step-by-Step How to Complete Form 1040-NR

### Part I: Filing Information

- **Box a**: Enter your name exactly as it appears on your Social Security Number (SSN) or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)
- **Box b**: Your SSN/ITIN is required. Canadians without U.S. employment typically need an ITIN (apply via Form W-7)
- **Residency status**: You will indicate your visa type or status. As a Canadian with U.S. rental property, you are a "non-resident alien individual" unless you meet the "substantial presence test" (which most Canadian landlords do not)
- **Address**: Provide your Canadian home address

### Part II: Income

Schedule E (Supplemental Income and Loss) attaches to Form 1040-NR. Here you report:

- **Property address**: Your West Virginia rental address
- **Rental income**: Gross rents received during the tax year
- **Expenses**: 
  - Mortgage interest (not principal)
  - Property taxes (West Virginia real estate taxes)
  - Insurance
  - Utilities (if you cover them)
  - Repairs and maintenance
  - Property management fees
  - HOA fees (if applicable)
  - Depreciation (building only; calculated via Form 4562)
- **Net rental loss or income**: The bottom line of Schedule E flows to Form 1040-NR

### Part III: Tax and Credits

- Your taxable income from rental property is taxed at U.S. graduated rates
- Apply any applicable credits (unlikely for rental income alone, but possible if you have other U.S. tax credits)
- Calculate tax owed or refund due

### Part IV: Signature and Verification

- Sign and date the return
- Include your ITIN or SSN
- Indicate whether you are filing jointly (you likely are not, unless you are married to a U.S. citizen/resident)

## West Virginia-Specific Considerations

### State Return Coordination

Filing Form 1040-NR federally triggers a **mandatory West Virginia Form IT-40NR state return**. These two filings must align:

- Report the same gross income on both
- State deductions follow federal deductions (West Virginia allows federal itemized deductions or a standard deduction)
- The state return is due the same day as the federal return: **April 15** (or June 15 if eligible for extension)

Contact the **West Virginia Department of Revenue** if you are unsure about your state filing obligation. Their website provides the current-year forms and instructions.

### Property Tax Deduction

West Virginia property taxes are deductible against rental income on both the federal and state returns. Itemize these on Schedule E. If your property is valued at $200,000 with a 0.59% effective tax rate, you would deduct approximately $1,180 annually—a meaningful reduction in taxable income.

### No State Withholding Requirement

Unlike some states, West Virginia does not impose withholding on non-resident rental income. However, you must file and pay estimated quarterly taxes if you expect to owe $1,000 or more. Payments are made using **Form IT-40ES**.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not making the Section 871(d) election**: Filing Form 1040-NR alone does not trigger the election. You must also file **Form 8288-B** (Certificate of Withholding—Section 1445 Withholding) or attach a statement to Form 1040-NR explicitly electing Section 871(d) treatment. Verify this with a cross-border tax specialist.

2. **Deducting depreciation incorrectly**: Depreciation must be calculated on Form 4562 and is based on the building's cost basis, not land. Canadians sometimes overlook this, inflating deductions.

3. **Mixing land and building costs**: Only the building depreciates. Land is not depreciable. Allocate your purchase price proportionally based on a professional appraisal.

4. **Overlooking the Canadian T1 return**: As a Canadian resident, you must also file a Canadian T1 General Income Tax and Benefit Return. U.S. rental income is reportable on line 10400 (Other Employment Income) or line 10600 (Investment Income). You will also claim a **Foreign Tax Credit (FTC)** on line 40500 for U.S. federal and state taxes paid. This prevents double taxation under the Canada-U.S. Tax Treaty.

5. **Missing West Virginia state return**: Many Canadian landlords file Form 1040-NR but forget the West Virginia IT-40NR. This creates a compliance gap and can trigger state notices.

6. **Incorrect ITIN application**: If you don't have a U.S. SSN, apply for an ITIN via Form W-7 early. Processing takes several weeks.
