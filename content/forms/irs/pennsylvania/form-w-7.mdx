# Form W-7 Guide for Canadian Landlords with Pennsylvania Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, **Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)**, is the IRS form you must file if you're a Canadian resident without a US Social Security Number (SSN) but need to comply with US federal tax obligations. An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted as XXX-XX-XXXX, with the first three digits typically in the 900-series range.

For Canadian landlords owning Pennsylvania rental property, obtaining an ITIN is not optional—it's a prerequisite for:

- Filing Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return)
- Meeting FIRPTA withholding obligations if you sell the property
- Claiming foreign tax credits on your Canadian T1 return for US taxes paid

The ITIN itself does not authorize you to work in the US, but it establishes your US tax identity for income tax purposes.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Pennsylvania Rental Property Owners

As a Canadian landlord with Pennsylvania rental property, you face a multi-jurisdictional tax reality:

**Federal Level:** You must report all worldwide income (including Pennsylvania rental income) on Form 1040-NR and pay federal income tax at graduated rates (up to 37% on ordinary income).

**Pennsylvania State Level:** Pennsylvania imposes a flat 3.07% state income tax on rental income earned within the state. Unlike many states, Pennsylvania taxes non-residents on source-state income only. This means your Pennsylvania rental income is subject to Pennsylvania's 3.07% tax rate. You'll file **Form PA-40-NR** (Pennsylvania Nonresident Income Tax Return) to report this income. Pennsylvania has no reciprocal tax treaty relief for Canadian residents, so this tax applies regardless of Canada-US Tax Treaty protections.

**Property Tax:** Pennsylvania's average effective property tax rate is 1.58%, applied at the local (county/municipal) level. This is separate from state income tax but affects your overall return on investment.

**FIRPTA Considerations:** If you eventually sell the Pennsylvania property, FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) requires a 15% withholding on the gross sales price (reduced to 10% for properties under $300,000). An ITIN is essential for filing the required Form 8288 and claiming withholding credits.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if:

1. You are a **non-resident alien** for US tax purposes (determined by the substantial presence test or residency start/end date tests)
2. You have **no US Social Security Number** and cannot obtain one
3. You have a **US tax filing requirement** or need to claim FIRPTA withholding

For Canadian landlords, "non-resident alien" status is typically clear-cut: if you maintain Canadian residency and have not been granted US permanent residence (green card), you qualify as a non-resident alien for the IRS's purposes. The Canada-US Tax Treaty generally does not change your non-resident classification for these purposes; it primarily addresses tax residency to avoid double taxation.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete and File Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Documentation

Before filing Form W-7, prepare:

- Valid passport (Canadian passport)
- Birth certificate or equivalent vital record (certified copy)
- US tax return requiring the ITIN (Form 1040-NR)
- Proof of residency in Canada (utility bill, lease, property tax notice—dated within 12 months)
- US rental property deed or mortgage statement (proving US source income requirement)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Correctly

**Lines 1a–1c:** Enter your full legal name (exactly as it appears on your passport).

**Lines 2–5:** Provide date of birth, country of citizenship (Canada), and passport number. For "Country of residence for tax purposes," enter Canada. For "Passport number," include your Canadian passport number.

**Lines 6–8:** Enter your current mailing address in Canada and your US property address (under "Address associated with line 1c application").

**Part II – Reason for Applying:** Select box **d (Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return)**. This is the applicable reason for most landlords.

**Part III – Additional Information:** If you've previously applied for or been denied an ITIN, disclose that information. Most first-time applicants will leave this section blank.

**Part IV – Declaration:** Sign and date the form. If applying by mail, your signature must be original ink (not scanned).

### Step 3: Attach Required Documents

Enclose a copy of:
- Your Canadian passport (photo page)
- Your US tax return (Form 1040-NR) for the tax year the ITIN will be used
- Proof of Canadian residency (utility bill or property tax notice)

Do **not** send originals; photocopies suffice, but they must be clear and legible.

### Step 4: Submit to the IRS

**Processing Time:** 7–14 business days if filed with your 1040-NR during tax season; longer during off-season.

**In-Person Option:** If visiting the US, you can apply at an IRS office, but this is impractical for most Canadian residents.

### Step 5: Receive Your ITIN

The IRS will mail your ITIN notice (Form W-7 with assigned number) to your Canadian mailing address. Once received, use this ITIN on all subsequent US tax filings.

## Pennsylvania-Specific Considerations

### State Tax Filing and ITIN Use

Once you obtain your ITIN, file **Form PA-40-NR** with the Pennsylvania Department of Revenue to report Pennsylvania rental income at the 3.07% flat rate. Use your ITIN in the taxpayer identification number field on Schedule PA-40-NR, line 1.

Pennsylvania allows a credit for tax paid to another state or country (Schedule PA-40-NR, Line 10), which can include federal taxes and Canadian provincial taxes paid on the same income. This credit partially offsets Pennsylvania's 3.07% tax but does not eliminate it entirely.

### Rental Income Reporting

Report your Pennsylvania rental income on **Schedule E (Form 1040-NR)** and **Schedule PA-40-NR**. Include:

- Gross rental income
- Mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, maintenance, and capital cost allowance (CCA equivalent to US depreciation)
- Any management fees paid to a US agent

### Coordination with Canadian T1 Return

On your Canadian T1 return, report the same Pennsylvania rental income in Canadian dollars (converted at the applicable year-end exchange rate). Claim a **foreign tax credit** for:

- US federal income tax paid
- Pennsylvania state income tax paid (3.07%)
- US property taxes at 1.58% (if eligible under Canada-US Tax Treaty Article XXVI)

The Canada-US Tax Treaty generally prevents double taxation, but timing and rate differences between jurisdictions mean you may still owe net tax to one country.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Incomplete Documentation:** Ensure your passport photocopy is clear. The IRS will reject incomplete applications, delaying your ITIN by weeks.

2. **Mismatching Names:** Your Form W-7 name must match your passport exactly. Middle initials, hyphens, and accents must align. Discrepancies cause processing delays.

3. **Filing W-7 Too Late:** Apply for your ITIN **before** the April 15 filing deadline if you're filing a 1040-NR for that year. ITINs cannot be used retroactively.

4. **Mixing SSN and ITIN:** Never provide an SSN and ITIN for the same person on IRS forms. This triggers IRS notices.

5. **Failing to Update Pennsylvania Forms:** Even after obtaining a federal ITIN, always cross-reference it on your Pennsylvania Form PA-40-NR to prevent state processing errors.
