# Form W-7 (ITIN) Application for Canadian Landlords with Indiana Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, officially titled "Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)," is the IRS form used to request a unique nine-digit taxpayer identification number for individuals who are not U.S. citizens and do not have a Social Security Number (SSN). For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves as your primary U.S. tax identification number and is essential for filing U.S. federal tax returns and complying with FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) obligations.

The ITIN is typically formatted as 9XX-XX-XXXX and functions identically to an SSN for tax filing purposes, though it does not grant work authorization or social benefits.

## How an ITIN Applies to Canadian Landlords with Indiana Rental Property

As a Canadian resident owning rental property in Indiana, you are classified as a **non-resident alien** for U.S. tax purposes. This classification triggers several critical filing obligations:

**Federal Obligations:**
- File Form 1040-NR (U.S. Income Tax Return for Nonresident Aliens) annually
- Report rental income, depreciation deductions, and mortgage interest
- Comply with FIRPTA withholding rules if you eventually sell the property

**Indiana State Obligations:**
- File an Indiana income tax return (Form IT-40, Nonresident or Part-Year Resident)
- Report rental income subject to Indiana's flat 3.05% state income tax
- Claim deductions for mortgage interest, property taxes, and repairs

**FIRPTA Withholding:**
- When you eventually sell your Indiana rental property, the buyer's attorney must withhold 15% of the net sale proceeds (or 10% of gross sales price) under FIRPTA, unless you qualify for an exemption
- An ITIN is required to file the necessary FIRPTA exemption form or to ensure proper tax credit on your U.S. returns

Without an ITIN, you cannot properly file these returns, and the IRS may impose penalties for non-compliance.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

- A Canadian resident (non-U.S. citizen)
- Owning U.S. real property (directly or through a partnership/LLC)
- Required to file a U.S. income tax return (Form 1040-NR)
- Needing a FIRPTA exemption number or filing a FIRPTA-related return
- Unable to obtain a Social Security Number

You should apply for your ITIN **before or with your first U.S. tax return filing**. If you plan to sell the property, apply before the sale closes to avoid delays in FIRPTA processing.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing W-7, assemble:

- Your valid Canadian passport or similar government-issued photo ID
- A certified copy of your birth certificate (in English) or passport photo page
- Proof of Canadian residency (driver's license, property tax bill, utility bill)
- Any existing U.S. tax documents (lease agreements, property deeds, mortgage statements)
- Your Canadian Social Insurance Number (for reference, though not required)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 (Current Tax Year Version)

**Part A: Personal Information**
- Line 1a: Enter your full legal name as it appears on your passport
- Line 1b: Enter your date of birth (month, day, year)
- Line 1c: Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Line 1d: Enter your country of residence (Canada)

**Part B: Mailing Address**
- Provide your Canadian mailing address where the IRS can reach you
- You may also list a U.S. address (e.g., your property manager's address) if preferred for correspondence

**Part C: ITIN Reason and Prior U.S. Tax Filing**
- Check the box indicating your reason for needing an ITIN (typically "Resident alien filing U.S. tax return" or "Nonresident filing U.S. tax return")
- Indicate whether you have filed a prior U.S. tax return
- If filing a 1040-NR for rental income, this is your primary reason

**Part D: Dependent Information**
- Leave blank unless claiming U.S. dependents (unlikely for a Canadian landlord)

**Part E: Identification Document Information**
- Document Type: Select "Passport"
- Document Number: Your Canadian passport number
- Country of Issue: Canada
- Expiration Date: Your passport expiration date

### Step 3: Prepare Supporting Documents

Obtain certified English translations of any non-English documents from a professional translator. The IRS requires originals or certified copies; photocopies are not acceptable unless certified by a notary or official translator.

### Step 4: Choose Your Filing Method

**Option A: By Mail with Certified Documents**
- Submit completed Form W-7 with original or certified copies of identification
- Mail to the IRS ITIN Unit address (check current instructions on IRS.gov)
- Processing time: 4–6 weeks
- **Recommended for Canadian applicants** as it avoids travel to the U.S.

**Option B: In Person at an IRS Office**
- Schedule an appointment at a U.S. IRS office (requires travel)
- Bring original documents and completed W-7
- Instant verification and faster processing
- Only advisable if visiting the U.S.

**Option C: Through Acceptance Agent Program**
- Work with an authorized acceptance agent (tax professional or financial institution) in the U.S.
- Less common for individual landlords but available

### Step 5: File With Your First Tax Return

Attach your completed W-7 application to your Form 1040-NR when you file. Do **not** mail them separately—the IRS coordinates ITIN issuance with your first return filing.

## Indiana-Specific Considerations

### State Income Tax Filing Requirements

Indiana imposes a flat 3.05% income tax on all taxable income, including rental property income. Even though you do not have federal filing requirements until your rental income exceeds standard deduction thresholds, Indiana requires non-resident aliens to file a return if they earn **any** Indiana-source income.

File **Form IT-40, Part 3 (Nonresident or Part-Year Resident)** with your federal 1040-NR. Include:
- Gross rental income from your Indiana property
- Deductions allocable to the property (mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, depreciation)
- Net Indiana-source income × 3.05% = State income tax due

### Property Tax and FIRPTA Implications

Indiana's average effective property tax rate is 0.85% of assessed value. This creates a significant deduction opportunity:
- Annual property tax payments reduce your federal taxable income on Form 1040-NR
- Property taxes also reduce your Indiana taxable income
- The Canada-U.S. Tax Treaty (Article XXII) allows a foreign tax credit for Indiana taxes paid, which you claim on your Canadian T1 return

**FIRPTA and Indiana Property Sales:**
When you sell your Indiana property, the buyer or buyer's attorney must withhold tax under FIRPTA. Indiana does not impose an additional FIRPTA-style withholding—only federal withholding applies. Your ITIN ensures the IRS correctly processes your sale and applies any withholding to your account.

### Foreign Tax Credit Coordination

On your Canadian T1 return (Schedule 1), claim a foreign tax credit for both federal and Indiana taxes paid. The credit is calculated as:
- (Canadian tax on worldwide income) × (U.S.-source income ÷ worldwide income)
- Then credit U.S. taxes paid (up to the calculated limit)

An ITIN ensures your U.S. filings are correctly linked to your Canadian tax account and foreign tax credit claims.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Failing to Apply Early**
- Apply for your ITIN as soon as you acquire the property or before the first rental season
- Delays in ITIN issuance can push back your 1040-NR filing and create penalties

**2. Submitting Invalid Documents**
- Do not submit photocopies without certification
- Non-English documents must include certified English translations
- The IRS rejects weak identification documents and returns the entire application

**3. Listing a Different Name on W-7 vs. Other Documents**
- Ensure your name is identical on your passport, W-7, and all subsequent tax returns
- Middle initials and spelling must match exactly

**4. Forgetting to File State Returns**
- Many Canadian landlords focus only on federal 1040-NR and neglect Indiana Form IT-40
