# Form W-7 (ITIN) Guide for Canadian Landlords with Arkansas Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, **Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)**, is your pathway to obtaining a unique nine-digit tax identification number from the US Internal Revenue Service (IRS). Unlike a Social Security Number (SSN), which is issued by the Social Security Administration to US citizens and authorized residents, an ITIN is specifically designed for non-resident aliens who must file US federal tax returns but lack an SSN.

For Canadian landlords, the ITIN serves a dual critical function:

1. **Tax compliance**: Filing Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) to report rental income, deductions, and calculate US federal tax liability
2. **FIRPTA compliance**: Enabling proper withholding under the Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act when you eventually sell the property

The ITIN is valid for tax purposes only and does not confer any immigration status or right to work in the United States.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Arkansas

### Federal Tax Obligations

As a Canadian non-resident landlord with rental property in Arkansas, you must file **Form 1040-NR** with the IRS annually. This form requires a taxpayer identification number—either an SSN or ITIN. Since you don't have an SSN, you'll need an ITIN.

Your Arkansas rental income is subject to US federal tax at graduated rates (ranging from 10% to 37% depending on income level). The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article XXIII) addresses taxation of real property income, but it does **not** eliminate your obligation to file and pay US federal tax. Instead, it provides relief mechanisms through foreign tax credits.

### Arkansas State Tax Obligations

Arkansas imposes state income tax on non-resident rental income at a flat **4.4%** rate. You must file **Form AR2(NR)** (Arkansas Nonresident Individual Income Tax Return) annually if your Arkansas-source income exceeds the filing threshold (currently $2,500 for single filers).

However, Arkansas state filing does **not** require an ITIN—the state accepts the IRS notice of the ITIN application or your US federal identification. That said, obtaining the ITIN first streamlines the process considerably.

### Property Tax Considerations

Arkansas's average effective property tax rate is **0.62%**, among the lowest in the US. However, individual county assessments vary. Pulaski County (Little Rock area), for example, assesses closer to 0.55%, while some rural counties may be higher. Property taxes are deductible against your gross rental income on both your US Form 1040-NR (Schedule E) and your Canadian T1 return.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you meet **all** of these criteria:

- You are a non-resident alien for US tax purposes
- You do not have an SSN
- You have US-source income (rental income from Arkansas property qualifies)
- You are required to file a US federal tax return (Form 1040-NR)

A Canadian citizen with permanent residency status in Canada is presumed to be a non-resident alien for US federal tax purposes, even if you maintain US investment property. The **Substantial Presence Test** and **Green Card Test** under US tax law determine residency status; most Canadian landlords meet neither test.

Filing Form W-7 **does not** affect your Canadian residency or tax status—the Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article IV) specifically preserves each country's right to determine residency independently.

## Step-by-Step Instructions for Completing Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing W-7, prepare:

- Passport (Canadian passport accepted; must be valid)
- Birth certificate or equivalent vital record
- Proof of Canadian residency (e.g., provincial driver's license, property deed, utility bill)
- IRS Form 7526-J (Residency Declaration) if applicable

### Step 2: Complete Part A—Applicant Information

**Line 1**: Enter your full legal name as shown on your Canadian passport.

**Line 2**: Enter your date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY format).

**Line 3**: Select your country of citizenship: **Canada**.

**Line 4**: Enter your Canadian residential address. This is critical—use your primary Canadian address, not the Arkansas property address.

**Line 5**: Mailing address: You may use a US address (e.g., a property management company in Arkansas) if you prefer correspondence there.

### Step 3: Complete Part B—Visa Information

**Lines 6–8**: Enter your passport number, country of issuance (Canada), and expiration date. The passport must be valid.

**Line 9**: Enter your Canadian province of residence.

### Step 4: Complete Part C—Reason for Applying

**Line 10**: Check the appropriate box. For most Canadian landlords, select:
- **Box a** if you are filing a US tax return (1040-NR)
- **Box c** if you need the ITIN specifically for FIRPTA purposes (property sale withholding)

If you are both filing a return and expect a future property sale, you may check both a and c.

### Step 5: Complete Part D—Applicant's Mailing Address for IRS Correspondence

Provide a mailing address where the IRS should send your ITIN notice. This is typically your Canadian address or a US agent's address (e.g., your accountant or property manager in Arkansas).

### Step 6: Certification and Signature

Sign and date the form. The signature must match your passport signature. Do not use initials only.

## Submitting Form W-7

### Method 1: Filing with Your 1040-NR (Recommended for First-Time Filers)

Attach the completed W-7 with originals (not photocopies) of your supporting documents to your first Form 1040-NR when you file. The IRS will process both simultaneously.

**Filing address for Canadian residents:**
```
Internal Revenue Service
Philadelphia Service Center
2970 Market Street
Philadelphia, PA 19104
USA
```

Include a cover letter stating:
```
ITIN Application Enclosed - Non-Resident Alien
Taxpayer Name and Canadian Address
Tax Year [current year]
```

### Method 2: Filing W-7 Independently

You may file W-7 separately from your 1040-NR if you need the ITIN in advance (e.g., for FIRPTA escrow requirements). Send the completed form with original supporting documents to the same Philadelphia address.

**Processing time**: 4–6 weeks under normal circumstances; up to 120 days during peak filing season.

### Method 3: ITIN Application Agent (IRS-Certified)

Canadian tax preparers or CPAs with IRS-Certified Acceptance Agent status can file W-7 on your behalf, reducing documentation requirements (certified copies are acceptable). This often accelerates processing.

## Arkansas-Specific Considerations

### Coordination with Form AR2(NR)

Once you receive your ITIN, you must report it on your Arkansas Form AR2(NR). Arkansas does not require a separate application for state tax ID; it uses your federal ITIN. File the state return with the same documentation as federal (Schedule E for rental income, depreciation, and deductible expenses).

### Property Management Agent Requirement

Many Arkansas property management companies require proof of US tax ID before processing rental income. Having your ITIN in hand before the first rental season streamlines this process.

### Withholding Considerations

Arkansas does not impose withholding on rental income payments to non-residents (unlike some states). However, any entity paying you must report to the IRS using your ITIN.

### Depreciation and Cost Segregation

Arkansas real property is depreciable over 27.5 years under US tax rules. Canadian landlords often overlook the benefit of cost segregation studies, which can accelerate depreciation on the building structure and land improvements. Request a professional cost segregation analysis when you obtain your ITIN to maximize deductions.

### Foreign Tax Credit Coordination

Your Arkansas state taxes (4.4% on rental income) are creditable against your Canadian federal tax. Report the ITIN and US tax details on your Canadian T1 return (line 40100) to claim the foreign tax credit. Keep copies of your US 1040-NR and Arkansas AR2(NR) for CRA verification.
