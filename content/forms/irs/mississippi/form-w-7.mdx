# Form W-7 for Canadian Landlords: The Mississippi ITIN Guide

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS application to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number that functions similarly to a US Social Security Number for tax filing purposes, but is issued to non-US citizens who do not qualify for an SSN.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN is essential to:
- File US federal income tax returns (Form 1040-NR) on rental income
- Comply with FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding obligations
- Meet state tax reporting requirements in Mississippi
- Claim deductions and establish a tax filing history with the IRS

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Mississippi

As a Canadian resident owning rental real estate in Mississippi, you occupy a specific cross-border tax position. The US-Canada Tax Treaty (Article XV) addresses taxation of income from real property. While the treaty provides some relief mechanisms, it does **not** exempt you from filing US tax returns on Mississippi rental income.

### Federal Requirements
You must file Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) annually if your Mississippi rental property generates net rental income. To file this form, the IRS requires a valid ITIN.

### Mississippi State Requirements
Mississippi imposes a **5% state income tax on rental income** for non-residents. Mississippi does not have a reciprocal agreement with Canada that eliminates this obligation. You will need to file Form MS 1040-NR (Mississippi Non-Resident Income Tax Return) with the Mississippi Department of Revenue. While Form MS 1040-NR can be filed with your federal ITIN, the ITIN must first be obtained through Form W-7.

### FIRPTA Considerations
If you sell your Mississippi rental property, FIRPTA requires a withholding of 15% of the sale proceeds. To establish a withholding exemption or to properly report the sale, you need an ITIN. Additionally, your US buyer or their agent must verify your ITIN before closing.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if:

- You are a Canadian citizen or permanent resident (non-US citizen)
- You have no US Social Security Number and are ineligible to obtain one
- You have US-source income (rental income from Mississippi property)
- You are required to file a US federal tax return (Form 1040-NR)
- You need an ITIN for FIRPTA withholding purposes or property sale reporting

**You do not need an ITIN** if your only US-source income is subject to 30% withholding tax and you are not filing a return to claim a refund (rare for rental income situations).

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, you must provide original or certified copies of identity and residency documentation:

**Identity Documents** (one required):
- Valid Canadian passport
- Canadian permanent resident card (with photograph)
- Canadian driver's license (with photograph)

**Residency Documents** (one required):
- Birth certificate (long-form, certified)
- Government-issued residence permit
- Canadian tax assessment notice or Notice of Assessment from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7

**Part I: Personal Information**
- Enter your full name exactly as it appears on your identity document
- Provide your Canadian address (your principal residence)
- Provide your date of birth

**Part II: Type of Applicant**
- Select box "b" (Nonresident alien individual)
- This classification is critical for Canadian landlords

**Part III: Reason for Applying**
- Select "Engaged in US trade or business" (box "a")
- For rental property owners, this applies because rental income is considered US trade or business income under IRC §875

**Part IV: Residency Information**
- Enter Canada as your country of citizenship
- Confirm your residency status as a Canadian resident

**Part V: IRS Use Only**
- Leave blank

### Step 3: Obtain an Acceptance Agent (ITIN Application Method)

You have three methods to file Form W-7:

**Method 1: IRS Acceptance Agent (Recommended for Canadian Landlords)**
The IRS has designated acceptance agents, including many accounting firms, legal firms, and tax preparation offices with US presence. You can find an acceptance agent through the IRS website or by contacting a cross-border tax specialist in the US. This method allows remote filing and is fastest (4-6 weeks).

**Method 2: IRS Office Walk-In**
You would need to visit an IRS office in person with original documentation. This is impractical for most Canadian landlords.

**Method 3: Mail to IRS**
You can mail Form W-7 with certified copies of supporting documents directly to the IRS (address on the form). Processing time is 6-8 weeks.

### Step 4: Submit and Track

Once your acceptance agent or the IRS processes Form W-7, you will receive:
- Notice of ITIN assignment (on a letter from the IRS)
- Your nine-digit ITIN in format XXX-XX-XXXX

The IRS typically issues ITINs within 4-6 weeks of submission via acceptance agent.

## Mississippi-Specific Considerations

### State Tax Filing Coordinate with ITIN Issuance
Mississippi requires the ITIN on all state tax forms. Do **not** file Form MS 1040-NR until you receive your ITIN. Filing without an ITIN will result in processing delays and potential penalties.

### Property Tax vs. Income Tax
Mississippi's average effective property tax rate is **0.65%**, assessed by county (rates vary by county). This is separate from the 5% state income tax and does not depend on an ITIN. However, your ITIN may be required for property tax administration purposes. Check with the county assessor's office in the county where your property is located.

### Rental Income Calculation
On Form 1040-NR and Form MS 1040-NR, calculate Mississippi rental income as:

**Gross Rental Income (rents received)**
- Less: Mortgage interest (if loan-financed)
- Less: Property taxes
- Less: Insurance
- Less: Repairs and maintenance
- Less: Depreciation (if applicable)
- **= Net Rental Income**

The 5% Mississippi state tax applies to this net income.

### Canadian Tax Credit Coordination
Canadian landlords must also file a Canadian T1 return (Form T776 for rental income). The Canada-US Tax Treaty allows you to claim a **foreign tax credit** on your Canadian return for US federal and Mississippi state taxes paid. This prevents double taxation. Maintain documentation of:
- Form 1040-NR (US federal return filed)
- Form MS 1040-NR (Mississippi state return filed)
- Proof of payment for both returns

Report these foreign taxes paid when filing your Canadian T1 on Schedule 1 or through TurboTax Canada/Wealthsimple, or with your accountant.

## Common Mistakes Canadian Landlords Make

**1. Filing a US Return Before Receiving an ITIN**
Do not submit Form 1040-NR before your ITIN is issued. The return will be rejected. Wait for your ITIN assignment letter from the IRS.

**2. Confusing ITIN with SSN**
An ITIN is **not** transferable to an SSN. If you later become a US resident and obtain an SSN, you must notify the IRS to deactivate the ITIN. Use the ITIN consistently across all US tax filings while you are non-resident.

**3. Failing to File Mississippi State Returns**
Some Canadian landlords assume that filing Form 1040-NR is sufficient. Mississippi imposes a separate 5% state tax on non-resident rental income. Failure to file Form MS 1040-NR results in penalties and interest.

**4. Incorrectly Reporting Property on Form W-7**
Do not reference your Mississippi property address on Form W-7. The form asks for your **principal residence** (Canadian address). Reference the Mississippi property only when filing Forms 1040-NR and MS 1040-NR, where Schedule E (Supplemental Income/Loss) lists the property details.

**5. Submitting Illegible or Uncertified Documents**
Ensure all supporting documents are original certified copies or notarized. The IRS rejects blurry scans or photocopies without certification.
