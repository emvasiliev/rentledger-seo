# Form W-7: ITIN Guide for Canadian Landlords with Iowa Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS application used by non-US citizens and non-resident aliens to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted like a Social Security Number (SSN) but used exclusively for US federal tax filing and reporting purposes when you do not qualify for an SSN.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN is essential because:

- The IRS requires a taxpayer identification number to file Form 1040-NR (Non-resident Alien Income Tax Return)
- Iowa requires an ITIN or SSN for state income tax reporting on rental income
- FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding agents must report your ITIN when you sell US real property
- Mortgage lenders and property managers may request it for tax documentation

## Why Canadian Landlords in Iowa Need Form W-7

### Federal Tax Filing

As a Canadian resident earning rental income from Iowa property, you are classified as a non-resident alien for US income tax purposes. Under US tax law, you must file Form 1040-NR reporting worldwide income, including Iowa rental revenue. The IRS will not process your return without a valid ITIN or SSN.

### Iowa State Income Tax

Iowa imposes a 6% state income tax on net rental income earned within the state by non-residents. Non-resident individuals must file **Iowa Form IA 1040-N** (Non-resident and Part-Year Resident Individual Income Tax Return) with the Iowa Department of Revenue. Iowa will not process your return without a federal ITIN.

### FIRPTA Compliance

When you eventually sell your Iowa rental property, the buyer's closing attorney or title company must withhold 15% of the sale price under FIRPTA and remit it to the IRS. This withholding is credited against your US capital gains tax liability. To claim the withholding credit and report the sale properly on your Form 1040-NR, you must have an ITIN.

### Treaty Considerations

The Canada-US Tax Treaty provides relief from double taxation. To claim treaty benefits (such as reduced withholding rates on investment income or treaty-based positions), you may need to file Form W-8BEN or Form 8833 with the IRS. Both forms require an ITIN.

## Iowa-Specific Tax Context

### State Income Tax Rate and Obligation

Iowa imposes tax on net rental income at a 6% flat rate for non-residents. This is calculated as:

**Net Iowa Rental Income = Gross Rents − Deductible Expenses × 6%**

Deductible expenses include mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, maintenance, utilities, property management fees, and depreciation (if claimed on your federal return).

### Property Tax Implications

Iowa's average effective property tax rate is **1.57%** of assessed value. This property tax is fully deductible against your rental income for both federal and Iowa purposes. Property tax bills are typically mailed in December for the following year's taxes.

### Filing Status and Credits

As a non-resident, you cannot claim the standard deduction or personal exemptions on your Iowa return. However, you may claim itemized deductions related to your rental activity and any tax treaties credits. Iowa does not offer a foreign tax credit, but you can claim US federal foreign tax credits on your Form 1040-NR for Iowa state taxes paid, which flows through to your Canadian T1 return.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you:

- Are a Canadian citizen or resident
- Own rental property in Iowa or other US states
- Do not have a US Social Security Number (SSN)
- Plan to file Form 1040-NR with the IRS
- Need to comply with FIRPTA withholding rules
- Want to open a US bank account or obtain an Employer Identification Number (EIN) for a US property management entity

You do **not** need to file W-7 if you already have a valid SSN (e.g., from previous US employment) or if you are filing only as a beneficiary of a US estate or trust (in which case a different process applies).

## How to Complete Form W-7: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Gather Required Documents

Before completing W-7, collect the following:

- **Passport**: A valid Canadian passport is required as primary government-issued ID
- **Proof of Foreign Status**: A letter from the Canadian government confirming your residency status, or a copy of your Notice of Assessment (NOA) from Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) showing your Canadian address
- **Reason for ITIN**: Documentation supporting your reason (e.g., executed lease agreement for Iowa property, property deed showing your ownership, or mortgage statement)

The IRS requires certified copies or notarized copies of foreign documents. Have your documents notarized by a Canadian notary public before mailing to the IRS.

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7

**Section 1: Personal Information**

- Enter your full legal name as it appears on your Canadian passport
- Enter your date of birth
- Enter your foreign address (your Canadian residential address)
- Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)

**Section 2: Type of Applicant**

Check the box for "Individual requesting ITIN for US federal tax purposes" (typically option c: "Nonresident alien filing a US tax return").

**Section 3: Reason for Applying**

Select "US Federal tax return filing" or "FIRPTA withholding purposes" (or both, if applicable).

**Section 4: Country of Citizenship/Residence**

Enter "Canada."

**Section 5: Signature**

Sign and date the form. If filing by mail, the signature must be notarized or certified by a tax professional.

### Step 3: Attach Supporting Documentation

Attach:

- Notarized copy of your Canadian passport (photo page)
- Proof of foreign residency (NOA from CRA, utility bill, or residency letter)
- Copy of your Iowa property deed, lease agreement, or mortgage statement
- A cover letter explaining your ITIN purpose (e.g., "I own rental property in Iowa and must file Form 1040-NR and Iowa Form IA 1040-N")

### Step 4: Choose Your Filing Method

**Option A: Mail to IRS (Slowest)**

Send Form W-7 with original documents to:

Internal Revenue Service  
ITIN Operations  
Austin, TX 73301  
USA

Processing time: 6–12 weeks after receipt.

**Option B: Apply Through an Authorized IRS Agent (Faster)**

The IRS maintains a list of Authorized IRS Agents (banks, accountancies, and tax professionals) who can certify your documents and accelerate processing. Search the IRS Agent List at **irs.gov/individuals/itin/authorized-agents**.

Many cross-border tax firms in Canada (such as those specializing in US expat and non-resident taxes) are authorized agents. This reduces processing time to 2–4 weeks.

**Option C: In-Person Appointment (Fastest)**

If you travel to the US, you may apply in person at an IRS office in Iowa or another state. Bring original documents and Form W-7. Processing time: same day or next business day.

### Step 5: Track Your Application

After mailing, the IRS will send you a confirmation letter with a tracking number. Use this to check your application status online at **irs.gov** or call the ITIN Help Line at **267-941-1000** (from Canada, this is a long-distance call; many cross-border accountants offer updates at no charge).

## Iowa-Specific Considerations

### Coordinating with Iowa Form IA 1040-N

Your ITIN must match exactly on both your federal Form 1040-NR and your Iowa Form IA 1040-N. Mismatches may delay processing or trigger audit. After receiving your ITIN from the IRS, wait 2–3 weeks before filing your Iowa return to ensure the IRS database has updated.

### Rental Income Reporting on Iowa Return

Iowa requires non-residents to report:

- Gross rental income (all rents received, including deposits forfeited for damage)
- All deductible expenses (itemized, not standard deduction)
- Net rental income (taxed at 6%)

Iowa will not recognize federal depreciation deductions for Iowa purposes if you depreciate the property on your Form 1040-NR. This creates a timing difference; you may claim depreciation federally but not on your Iowa return in the same year.

### Property Tax Deduction

Iowa property taxes are 100% deductible against your rental income. Obtain a property tax statement from the Iowa County Assessor's office
