# Form W-7: ITIN Application Guide for Canadian Landlords with Washington Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, **Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)**, is the official IRS form used to request a unique nine-digit taxpayer identification number for individuals who are not eligible for a US Social Security Number (SSN) but have US tax filing obligations.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves as your US tax identification number and is required to:
- File US non-resident alien tax returns (Form 1040-NR)
- Comply with Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) withholding rules
- Report income from rental properties in the United States
- Claim foreign tax credits on your Canadian return

Without an ITIN, you cannot legally file US tax returns or meet FIRPTA obligations, and you expose yourself to penalties and compliance issues.

## Why Washington State Is Attractive to Canadian Landlords

Washington state has no state income tax, making it particularly appealing for Canadian investors. Unlike other states that impose state-level income taxes on rental income, Washington landlords avoid this additional tax burden. However, this does **not** exempt you from federal US tax obligations or the need for an ITIN.

Key Washington tax facts for landlords:
- **State income tax rate**: 0% (no state income tax)
- **Average effective property tax rate**: 1.03% (based on state property valuations)
- **Federal capital gains tax**: Still applies to US-situs real property
- **FIRPTA withholding**: Still applies to property sales

The absence of state income tax simplifies your Washington filing requirements but does not eliminate federal reporting obligations.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

**A non-resident alien (for US tax purposes) with:**
- Rental income from Washington real property
- Capital gains from the sale of Washington real estate
- Mortgage interest and property tax deductions to claim
- Withholding tax credits from FIRPTA withholding on property sales
- Any other US-source income requiring tax filing

**Canadian residents are automatically considered non-resident aliens** for US federal tax purposes unless you hold US citizenship or a permanent resident visa (green card).

The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article I) defines your tax residency status and may provide relief from double taxation, but it does not eliminate the need for an ITIN if you have US tax filing obligations.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Part 1: Personal Information

**Line 1a–1c**: Enter your legal name exactly as it appears on your passport or government identification. Use your Canadian legal name; the IRS does not require it to match a US state name format.

**Line 2**: Enter your date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY format).

**Line 3**: Select your country of citizenship (Canada).

**Line 4**: Enter your current mailing address. You may use a Canadian address if you reside in Canada.

### Part 2: Reason for Applying

**Line 6**: Check the box that applies. For rental property income, select:
- **Box b**: "Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return"

This is the primary reason Canadian landlords file W-7. Do not select other boxes unless they also apply.

### Part 3: Identification and Residency

**Line 8**: Enter your country of residence (Canada).

**Line 9**: If you have a Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN), you may enter it here, though it is not required for ITIN issuance.

**Line 10**: Specify your immigration status. Select "Other" and write "Canadian resident" if no box matches your situation. You are not claiming any US immigration status.

### Part 4: Certifications and Signature

You must sign and date the form. If filing on behalf of a spouse, you must both sign if either of you has US rental income.

## Required Documentation

The IRS requires **one primary document** and, in most cases, **one secondary document** to prove identity and foreign residency:

### Primary Documents (Choose One):
- Valid Canadian passport
- Canadian provincial driver's license
- Canadian national ID card

### Secondary Documents (Choose One):
- Original or certified copy of your birth certificate
- Canadian provincial health insurance card
- Mortgage statement or property tax bill on your Washington property
- Utility bill (electricity, water, gas) showing your Canadian address

**Critical**: All documents must be **original, certified copies, or notarized**. Photocopies alone are not accepted. Work with a tax professional or your accountant to ensure documents meet IRS standards.

## Washington-Specific Considerations

### No State Income Tax Benefit Does Not Reduce Federal Obligations

Washington's lack of state income tax simplifies your overall US tax burden, but you still owe federal tax on rental income. The absence of state tax means:
- No Washington Form 1040 equivalent to file
- No state-level foreign tax credit to claim
- Simpler overall filing compared to states like California or New York

However, you must still file Form 1040-NR with the IRS.

### Property Tax Deductions

Washington's effective property tax rate of 1.03% is relatively low compared to other states. Property taxes are deductible on your Form 1040-NR Schedule A (if you itemize). When calculating your ITIN application timeline, ensure you have property tax bills available to support deduction claims.

### FIRPTA Withholding Coordination

If you sell Washington rental property, FIRPTA rules require the buyer's closing agent to withhold 15% of the sale price (as of 2024) unless you obtain a FIRPTA withholding certificate from the IRS. You must have an ITIN **before the sale closes** to obtain this certificate. Without an ITIN, the full 15% withholding applies regardless of your actual tax liability.

File Form W-7 at least **4–6 weeks before any anticipated property sale** to ensure ITIN issuance in time.

### Cross-Border Implications

Under the Canada-US Tax Treaty, you may be eligible for:
- **Article XXII (Mutual Agreement Procedures)**: Relief from double taxation on the same income
- **Article XXIV (Elimination of Double Taxation)**: Foreign tax credits for Canadian tax paid

Your Canadian accountant should file Form 1040-NR and claim the foreign tax credit on your US return. On your Canadian T1 return, you will report worldwide income, including US rental income, and claim a credit for US federal and state taxes paid (though Washington has no state tax).

## Common Mistakes When Filing Form W-7

1. **Using a Temporary Address**: Do not use a hotel address or temporary US address on Form W-7. Use your permanent Canadian residence address.

2. **Submitting Copies Instead of Certified Documents**: The IRS will reject photocopies. Obtain certified copies from Canadian government agencies or have documents notarized.

3. **Misidentifying Your Reason for Filing**: Do not select "visa-required" boxes. Select "nonresident alien required to file a US tax return."

4. **Timing the Application Too Late**: Filing W-7 immediately before a property sale closes risks ITIN non-issuance. Apply 6–12 months before any anticipated transaction.

5. **Forgetting to Report Your SIN**: While not required, providing your Canadian SIN helps the IRS cross-reference your identity.

6. **Not Coordinating with a Tax Professional**: Cross-border filing is complex. Errors on W-7 delay ITIN issuance and can trigger audit flags.

## Key Deadlines and Processing Times

- **W-7 Processing Time**: 4–6 weeks under normal circumstances; up to 12 weeks during peak tax season (January–April)
- **First 1040-NR Filing Deadline**: April 30 (following the tax year) for non-residents; US residents have April 15
- **FIRPTA Withholding Certificate Request**: Must be filed with IRS before property closing (timeline varies; coordinate with your real estate attorney)
- **ITIN Validity**: Valid indefinitely unless you fail to file a US tax return for three consecutive years
