# Form W-7: ITIN Application Guide for Canadian Landlords with Massachusetts Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS application used to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted as XXX-XX-XXXX, issued by the IRS to individuals who are required to comply with US tax law but do not have (and are not eligible for) a US Social Security Number.

For Canadian landlords, the ITIN serves two critical functions:

1. **Tax filing**: Filing a Form 1040-NR (Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) to report US rental income
2. **FIRPTA compliance**: Satisfying Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act withholding requirements when selling US real property

Without an ITIN, Canadian rental property owners cannot properly file US taxes or manage FIRPTA obligations, both of which carry significant penalties if neglected.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Massachusetts

Massachusetts presents a unique tax landscape for non-resident foreign landlords. Unlike many US states, Massachusetts requires non-residents to file state income tax returns on rental income sourced within the state.

### Federal and State Tax Obligations

When you own rental property in Massachusetts, you must file:

- **Federal Form 1040-NR**: Report US-source rental income to the IRS
- **Massachusetts Form 1-NR/PY**: Report Massachusetts rental income to the Department of Revenue (5% state tax rate applies to net rental income)

Both filings require a tax identification number. The ITIN satisfies this requirement at the federal level and is typically accepted by Massachusetts for state filing purposes as well.

### Massachusetts Tax Rate Context

Massachusetts imposes a **5% flat tax** on rental income for non-residents. This applies to:

- Gross rent received
- Less allowable deductions (mortgage interest, property taxes, repairs, depreciation, insurance, utilities paid by owner)

Additionally, Massachusetts has an average effective property tax rate of **1.2%**, which represents a significant deductible expense when calculating net Massachusetts rental income.

Example: If you collect $24,000 annually in rent with $8,000 in property taxes and $4,000 in other allowable deductions, your Massachusetts taxable rental income is $12,000, subject to $600 in state tax (5%).

### FIRPTA Withholding Requirements

If you plan to sell Massachusetts rental property in the future, the FIRPTA rules require a buyer's agent (or the buyer if unrepresented) to withhold **15% of the sale price** as estimated federal tax (this withholding goes to the IRS, not the state). To properly manage FIRPTA withholding and eventual settlement, you must have an ITIN.

The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article XXII) allows for treaty benefits that may reduce this withholding in certain circumstances, but treaty benefits require an ITIN to claim.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

- A Canadian citizen or permanent resident
- Non-resident of the United States for tax purposes
- Required to file a US tax return (1040-NR) because you have US-source income (such as Massachusetts rental income)
- Without a US Social Security Number
- Required to satisfy FIRPTA obligations on a future property sale

You should apply for an ITIN **proactively** when you acquire Massachusetts rental property. Do not wait until you sell the property or miss a filing deadline.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete and File Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, assemble the following:

- Valid passport (Canadian passport is acceptable identification)
- Proof of tax residency in Canada (Canadian tax return, provincial health card, or CRA notice of assessment)
- Proof of Massachusetts rental property ownership (deed, purchase agreement, property tax statement, or mortgage statement)
- Proof of US tax filing requirement (draft 1040-NR showing rental income, or lease agreement)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7

**Section 1: Personal Information**
- Enter your full legal name as it appears on your passport
- Enter your date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY format)
- Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)

**Section 2: US Address**
- Provide your Massachusetts rental property address or the address of your property manager
- If you do not have a US mailing address, use c/o a property management company or real estate professional managing your property

**Section 3: Permanent Foreign Address**
- Enter your Canadian home address
- This is your primary residence in Canada

**Section 4: Reason for Application**
- Check the box: "Nonresident alien required to get a US taxpayer identification number"
- Additionally check: "To comply with US tax laws (including FIRPTA if applicable)"

**Section 5: Declaration**
- Sign and date the form
- Enter the current date

### Step 3: Certify Your Passport

You must provide certified copies of your identification. The IRS requires:

- **Primary ID**: Copy of your valid passport (biographical page)
- **Secondary verification**: This can be your Canadian driver's license, provincial health card, or a notarized declaration

Certification options:
- Have a US-based notary public certify the copies
- Use a Canadian notary (acceptable but ensure copies are in English)
- Use a US consulate or embassy in Canada (most reliable)

### Step 4: Prepare Your Attachments

Attach the following to your Form W-7:

1. Certified copy of your passport
2. Evidence of US tax filing requirement (typically a completed 1040-NR draft showing rental income line-by-line)
3. Evidence of Massachusetts property ownership (property tax bill, deed, or lease agreement)
4. Copy of your Canadian tax return (to establish Canadian residency)

### Step 5: Submit Form W-7

**Mailing Address for Form W-7:**

```
Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operations
Austin, TX 73301
USA
```

**Processing from Canada:**

Mail your complete Form W-7 package to the Austin address via registered mail or courier with tracking. Allow 6–8 weeks for processing. The IRS will mail your ITIN to the Canadian address you provide on the form.

**Alternative: In-Person Filing**

If you travel to the US, you may apply in person at an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center in Massachusetts (Boston area). Bring your original passport and all documentation. This option is faster (2–3 weeks).

## Massachusetts-Specific Considerations for ITIN Holders

### Filing Your Massachusetts Form 1-NR/PY

Once you receive your ITIN, you must file Massachusetts Form 1-NR/PY annually if you have Massachusetts rental income. Key points:

- **Filing deadline**: Same as federal deadline (April 15th, or June 15th if you file a US extension)
- **Your ITIN**: Use your newly issued ITIN as your taxpayer identification number
- **Massachusetts rental income only**: Do not report Canadian rental income on this return; Canadian income is reported on your Canadian T1 General form

### Foreign Tax Credit Coordination

As a Canadian resident, you will likely pay both US federal income tax and Massachusetts state income tax on your rental income. To avoid double taxation:

1. **US Foreign Tax Credit**: File Form 1116 with your 1040-NR to claim a credit for Massachusetts state taxes paid
2. **Canadian Foreign Tax Credit**: Claim a credit on your Canadian T1 General (Schedule 1, lines 40427 and 40428) for US federal and state taxes paid

The Canada-US Tax Treaty allows you to claim credits for taxes paid to both countries.

### Massachusetts Property Tax Deduction

Remember that Massachusetts property taxes are deductible at the federal level when calculating taxable income on your 1040-NR. Do not double-deduct these taxes on both US and Canadian returns.

### Estimated Tax Payments

Once you have an ITIN and are filing a 1040-NR, the IRS expects quarterly estimated tax payments if you anticipate owing more than $1,000 in annual US tax. Form 1040-ES (NR) is used to calculate these payments. Use your ITIN when remitting payments electronically (IRS Direct Pay) or by check.

## Common Mistakes When Filing Form W-7

1. **Incomplete or uncertified documentation**: The IRS rejects applications with photocopies that are not certified. Always have your passport certified by a notary or consulate.

2. **Submitting Form W-7 without a 1040-NR**: The IRS typically requires evidence that you have a tax filing requirement. Always attach a completed (or draft) 1040-NR showing rental income.
