# Form W-7: ITIN Application for Canadian Landlords with West Virginia Rental Property

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS document used to apply for an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit taxpayer identification number formatted as XXX-XX-XXXX, issued by the IRS to individuals who are required to file US tax returns but do not have a Social Security Number (SSN).

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves a critical dual purpose: it allows you to file US non-resident tax returns (Form 1040-NR) for rental income and enables compliance with FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding obligations when you eventually sell your property.

## How Form W-7 Applies in West Virginia

West Virginia presents specific tax considerations for Canadian property owners that make ITIN acquisition essential:

**State-Level Income Tax Obligations**
West Virginia imposes a state income tax of 6.5% on rental income generated from WV property. As a non-resident, you are required to file Form WV/IT-140 (West Virginia Non-Resident and Part-Year Resident Return) annually. The IRS will not process your federal Form 1040-NR without an ITIN, and West Virginia's Department of Tax and Revenue will cross-reference federal filing status. Without an ITIN, you cannot properly file either federal or state returns.

**FIRPTA Compliance**
When you sell your West Virginia rental property, FIRPTA requires that the buyer (or closing agent) withhold 15% of the gross sale proceeds and remit these funds to the IRS. An ITIN is mandatory to receive FIRPTA withholding certificates and to file the required Form 8288-B to claim a reduced withholding rate or refund of over-withheld amounts.

**Property Tax Considerations**
West Virginia's effective property tax rate averages 0.59% of assessed value. While this is relatively low, counties still require proper identification for assessments and communications. An ITIN establishes your legal US taxpayer identity for all property-related filings.

## Who Must File Form W-7

Canadian landlords must file Form W-7 if they:

- Own rental property in West Virginia and receive rental income
- Do not have a US Social Security Number
- Are required to file Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return)
- Will be subject to FIRPTA withholding on future property sales
- Are otherwise required to file federal income tax returns on US-source income

You are considered a non-resident alien for US tax purposes if you fail the "substantial presence test" (generally, fewer than 183 days in the US during the relevant year and prior two years, weighted appropriately). Most Canadian citizens with permanent residence in Canada satisfy this test and file as non-residents.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, assemble:

- Valid passport (Canadian) or government-issued photo ID
- Proof of foreign status (Canadian citizenship document, valid foreign passport, or residence card)
- Proof of US rental property ownership (deed, mortgage statement, property tax bill, or lease agreement)
- Documentation of business purpose (evidence of rental activity: lease agreements, bank statements showing rental deposits, property insurance)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately

**Part 1: Personal Information**
- Enter your full legal name as it appears on your Canadian passport
- Enter your date of birth (DDMMYYYY format)
- Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Provide your Canadian address of residence

**Part 2: IRS Application**
- Select reason for applying: "Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return"
- Provide your country of birth and citizenship status dates
- Confirm that you are NOT eligible for a Social Security Number

**Part 3: Mailing Address**
- Provide a valid US address or a Canadian address where the IRS will send correspondence. Many Canadian landlords provide their property manager's address in West Virginia or a US contact address.

**Part 4: Preparer Information (if applicable)**
- If a tax professional completes the return, they must complete this section with their PTIN, signature, and credentials

### Step 3: Attach Required Documentation

The IRS requires certified copies or original documentation. Do NOT submit:

- Passport originals (submit certified copies)
- Multiple documents of the same type

Submit photocopies or certified copies of:

1. Your Canadian passport (front and back)
2. Proof of US property ownership (property deed, property tax assessment, or mortgage statement)
3. An IRS Form 4029 or evidence of business purpose (rental property documentation, bank statements, lease)

### Step 4: File Form W-7

**Method 1: File with Your Initial Form 1040-NR**
Attach Form W-7 to your completed Form 1040-NR, sign both returns, and mail to the IRS address on the 1040-NR instructions.

**Method 2: File Standalone (Advance ITIN)**
If you want an ITIN before filing your first return (advisable for FIRPTA purposes), submit Form W-7 with documentation directly to:

IRS ITIN Operations
P.O. Box 447161
Baltimore, MD 21244-7161

**Method 3: Use an Authorized Representative**
If using a Canadian or US tax professional (CPA, Enrolled Agent), they can file on your behalf with proper authorization (Form 2848 or 8821).

### Step 5: Provide Passport Certification

The IRS may request that you have your passport certified by a notary, US embassy official, or state official. Canadian passport holders can obtain certification from:

- A US notary public
- Any US state government office
- The US embassy in Canada or a US consulate

## West Virginia-Specific Considerations

### Coordinating Federal and State Filing

Once you receive your ITIN from the IRS, use this number on:

1. **Federal Form 1040-NR** (filed with IRS)
2. **West Virginia Form WV/IT-140** (filed with WV Department of Tax and Revenue)
3. **West Virginia Schedule SE-NR** (if self-employed, reporting rental income)

West Virginia does not recognize Canadian residents as exempt from state income tax. Even if you claim foreign tax credits on your federal return, you must file WV/IT-140 and pay WV state income tax on gross West Virginia rental income.

### Foreign Tax Credit Coordination

As a Canadian resident paying Canadian income tax on worldwide income, you may claim a foreign tax credit on Form 1040-NR (Form 1116 for non-resident aliens). However:

- Canadian federal and provincial taxes paid on WV rental income can offset US federal tax liability
- West Virginia state tax is NOT creditable against Canadian federal tax
- You must track WV taxes separately

### Rental Property Deductions

West Virginia does not provide special deductions for non-resident landlords. You may deduct:

- Mortgage interest
- Property taxes (WV average: 0.59%)
- Insurance, maintenance, and management fees
- Depreciation (if property is held as investment real estate)

These deductions reduce your West Virginia taxable rental income dollar-for-dollar.

### FIRPTA and Sale of Property

When you sell your West Virginia property:

1. The buyer's closing agent must withhold 15% of sale proceeds (FIRPTA withholding)
2. You must file Form 8288-B with the IRS to report the sale and claim your ITIN
3. If you have an ITIN and properly file Form 8288-B, over-withheld amounts are refunded
4. West Virginia does not impose an additional property transfer tax, simplifying the sale process

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Submitting Original Passport**: The IRS accepts certified copies only. Submit originals only if requested.

2. **Incomplete Property Documentation**: Do not submit a property tax bill alone. Include multiple documents showing ownership (deed + mortgage statement, or deed + property tax bill).

3. **Using a Canadian Address for Mailing**: While your residence address is Canadian, provide a US contact address for faster IRS correspondence.

4. **Failing to Coordinate with Form 1040-NR**: Do not file Form W-7 separately and then file 1040-NR with a different identification. Attach both together or file W-7 in advance with clear cross-reference.

5. **Missing the FIRPTA Link**: Many landlords assume ITIN is only for annual tax filing. Ensure your ITIN is active and on file well before selling the property.
