# Form W-7 (ITIN) Guide for Canadian Landlords with Nevada Rental Property

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7, *Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number*, is the IRS application to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted as XXX-XX-XXXX that functions like a Social Security Number (SSN) for US tax purposes, but is issued to individuals who are not eligible for an SSN.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves two critical purposes:
1. **Tax filing requirement**: To file Form 1040-NR (U.S. Income Tax Return for Nonresident Alien Individuals) and report US-source rental income
2. **FIRPTA compliance**: To satisfy Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act withholding obligations when selling the property

Without an ITIN, the IRS cannot process your returns, and your US renters' withholding agents cannot fulfill their legal obligations under FIRPTA (26 U.S.C. § 1445), which requires 15% withholding on the sale proceeds of real property by foreign persons.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Nevada

### The Nevada Tax Advantage

Nevada has **no state personal income tax**, making it an exceptionally attractive jurisdiction for Canadian real estate investors. However, this federal tax benefit does not exempt you from US federal income tax requirements.

As a Canadian non-resident alien owning Nevada rental property, you must:
- Report rental income on Form 1040-NR (US federal level)
- File Nevada's annual property tax returns (based on assessed value, not income)
- Comply with FIRPTA when selling

The average effective Nevada property tax rate is **0.59%** on assessed valuation, one of the lowest in the US. While this reduces your state-level tax burden, you remain fully subject to federal income tax at graduated rates (10% to 37% for 2024) on net rental income.

### FIRPTA and the ITIN Connection

If you sell your Nevada rental property, the buyer's closing agent must withhold 15% of the gross sale price and remit it to the IRS under FIRPTA rules. The IRS uses your **ITIN to track this withholding** and apply it against your US federal tax liability.

Without an ITIN on file, closing delays or withholding complications frequently occur, as title companies cannot complete escrow instructions.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:
- A non-resident alien (including Canadian citizens)
- Owning US real property generating rental income
- Required to file a US tax return (Form 1040-NR)
- Not eligible for a Social Security Number
- Opening a US bank account to receive rental deposits (some lenders require an ITIN)

You do **not** need an ITIN if you have a valid US visa and SSN, or if your Nevada property generates no income (though this is rare for landlords).

## Step-by-Step How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documents

Before completing W-7, assemble:
- Valid Canadian passport (photocopy)
- Proof of residency (Canadian driver's license, utility bill, or provincial health card)
- Lease agreement or property deed showing Nevada address
- Proof of tax filing requirement (draft Form 1040-NR or FIRPTA documentation)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately

**Section 1: Personal Information**
- Enter your full name exactly as it appears on your Canadian passport
- Provide your Canadian address (not the Nevada property address—you're a non-resident)
- Enter your date of birth

**Section 2: Country of Citizenship and Residence**
- Select "Canada" as country of citizenship
- Enter Canada as your country of residence

**Section 3: Type of Applicant**
- Select box **c** ("Other individual") if you don't fit the previous categories
- In the blank, write: "Non-resident alien; US real property rental income"

**Section 4: Reason for Applying**
- Check **a** ("Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return")
- Also check **g** ("Other") and write: "FIRPTA withholding compliance"

**Section 5: US Taxpayer Identification Number Previously Assigned**
- Leave blank—this is your first ITIN application

**Section 6: Declaration**
- Sign and date the form
- Your signature must match your passport

### Step 3: Compile Supporting Documentation

Prepare a certified copy of your Canadian passport (notarized by a US notary if possible). The IRS prefers that US notaries certify foreign documents, but Canadian notarization is acceptable.

You may also include:
- A certified copy of your Canadian driver's license
- Your Nevada property deed or lease
- A draft Form 1040-NR showing Nevada rental income

### Step 4: Submit the Application

**Option A: Mail with Tax Return (Recommended for Canadian Residents)**

Attach Form W-7 to your first Form 1040-NR and mail both to:
```
Department of the Treasury
Internal Revenue Service
Philadelphia Service Center
ITIN Unit
2970 Market Street, Room 611
Philadelphia, PA 19104
```

**Option B: Send Directly to ITIN Unit**

If you're applying outside the tax-filing cycle (e.g., before rental season begins), mail W-7 with all supporting documents to the Philadelphia address above.

**Option C: ITIN Application Assistant Program (IAP)**

If you have contacts in the US (tenant, property manager, CPA), they may apply on your behalf by visiting an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center with your W-7 and original documents. This accelerates approval to 2–4 weeks.

### Step 5: Allow Processing Time

- **By mail**: 4–6 weeks during tax season (January–April); 2–3 weeks off-season
- **By IAP appointment**: 10–14 business days
- **Online via IRS account (if eligible)**: Varies

The IRS will mail your ITIN approval notice (CP565 or CP565-A) to your Canadian address.

## Nevada-Specific Considerations

### Property Tax Filing Deadlines

While Nevada imposes no income tax, you must file Nevada real property tax returns annually. The deadline is typically **May 15th**. Failure to file can trigger assessed penalties.

You may file online through the Nevada Department of Taxation or by mail. The 0.59% effective rate applies to your assessed value, which the county assessor sets independently of your rental income.

### FIRPTA Withholding and Nevada Title Companies

Nevada title and escrow companies are highly familiar with FIRPTA rules due to the prevalence of foreign ownership. When selling, ensure your ITIN is provided **in writing** to the title company at least 30 days before closing. If they cannot verify your ITIN with the IRS, they may place sale proceeds in escrow pending verification.

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Considerations

The Canada-US Income Tax Treaty (Article XIII) addresses real property income. As a Canadian resident, you remain taxable in Canada on worldwide income, including Nevada rental income. However:
- The US taxes you under IRC § 871(d) on net rental income at regular graduated rates
- You claim a foreign tax credit on your Canadian T1 return for US federal tax paid
- No foreign tax credit is available for Nevada property tax (it's not an income tax), but it may be deductible as a business expense on your 1040-NR

### Currency Reporting

Report all USD amounts on Form W-7 and Form 1040-NR in US dollars. If you receive CAD rent payments, convert to USD at the Bank of Canada exchange rate on the payment date (not an average rate).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Incomplete Documentation**: The single largest cause of Form W-7 rejection is missing or unverified supporting documents. Always include a **certified** copy of your passport, not a photocopy.

2. **Wrong Mailing Address**: Sending W-7 to a regional IRS office instead of the Philadelphia ITIN Unit delays processing by 6–8 weeks. Use only the Philadelphia address above.

3. **Timing the Filing**: Applying for an ITIN *after* your rental season begins risks missing your 1040-NR filing deadline. Apply by **December 1st** of the year before you have US rental income.

4. **Confusing ITIN with SSN**: An ITIN does not allow you to work in the US. It is exclusively for tax and FIRPTA purposes. Do not represent it as an SSN to US employers or lenders.

5. **Forgetting FIRPTA Notification**: When selling the property
