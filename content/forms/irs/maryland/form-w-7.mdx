# Form W-7: ITIN Guide for Canadian Landlords in Maryland

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, the **Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)**, is the official IRS form used to request a unique nine-digit tax identification number for individuals who are not eligible for a Social Security Number (SSN) but must file US tax returns or comply with US tax withholding requirements.

An ITIN functions identically to an SSN for federal tax purposes and appears in the format: 9XX-XX-XXXX. For Canadian landlords, obtaining an ITIN is essential—not optional—when you own rental property in the United States and generate taxable income subject to US reporting requirements.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Maryland

As a Canadian resident owning rental property in Maryland, you occupy the status of a **non-resident alien (NRA)** under US tax law. This status triggers two critical obligations:

**1. Federal Tax Filing Requirement (Form 1040-NR)**

You must file a US non-resident alien tax return (Form 1040-NR) to report rental income, deductions, and calculate federal income tax liability. The IRS will not process your return without an ITIN. Maryland rental income is fully taxable at federal rates (10% to 37% depending on total income).

**2. FIRPTA Withholding Compliance**

The Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) requires that when you eventually sell your Maryland property, a 15% withholding be calculated on the gain. An ITIN is mandatory for FIRPTA reporting (Form 8288-B) and to establish your taxpayer identity with the IRS.

Under the **Canada-US Tax Treaty** (Article XXII), you may be able to reduce US tax on rental income if you qualify for treaty benefits, but claiming these benefits still requires an ITIN and proper filing.

## Maryland-Specific Tax Context

Maryland imposes a **5.75% state income tax** on net rental income for non-residents. A Maryland non-resident return (Form 502 NR) must be filed separately from your federal return. The combination of federal (approximately 24–37% depending on tax bracket), Maryland state (5.75%), and property tax (average effective rate: 1.09%) creates significant cumulative tax exposure, making accurate identification and documentation via ITIN essential for compliance and foreign tax credit planning.

## Who Must File Form W-7

Form W-7 applies specifically to you if:

- You are a non-resident alien (not a US citizen or green card holder)
- You own US real property generating rental income
- You must file a US federal income tax return (Form 1040-NR)
- You do not have and are not eligible to obtain an SSN
- You require an ITIN for FIRPTA purposes related to property sales

Canadian citizens are presumed non-resident aliens for this purpose unless they hold US green cards.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, assemble:

- Valid passport (Canadian passport)
- Birth certificate (US-certified copy is preferred but not always required)
- Proof of Canadian residence (utility bill, lease, property deed in Canada, or provincial ID)
- Rental property deed or title for your Maryland property
- Your anticipated 2024 US tax return (Form 1040-NR) or FIRPTA documentation

### Step 2: Complete Part I – Personal Information

- **Line 1a–1d**: Enter your legal name exactly as it appears on your Canadian passport
- **Line 2**: Enter your date of birth
- **Line 3**: Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- **Line 4**: Enter your country of residence (typically Canada, unless you reside elsewhere)
- **Line 5**: Enter your Canadian mailing address

Do not leave any field blank. Inconsistencies between your passport and Form W-7 can delay processing.

### Step 3: Complete Part II – IRS Use Only

You do not complete this section. The IRS completes it upon receipt and will assign your ITIN.

### Step 4: Complete Part III – Reason for Applying

- **Line 6**: Select the box that applies. For rental property owners, typically box "c" (Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return) or box "f" (Other) with clarification written in (e.g., "FIRPTA withholding").

### Step 5: Certify and Sign

- Sign and date the form under penalty of perjury
- Have your signature notarized by a US-based notary public or have it certified by a Canadian notary with an apostille
- Do not use initials; your full legal signature is required

## Maryland-Specific Considerations

### 1. Coordination with Maryland Non-Resident Returns

Once you receive your ITIN, you will use it on your **Form 502 NR (Maryland Non-Resident Individual Income Tax Return)**. Maryland's Department of Revenue requires the same ITIN that appears on your federal return. Mismatches trigger correspondence and delays.

### 2. Property Tax Assessment and ITIN

Maryland counties do not require an ITIN for property tax bills, but your ITIN should align with the ownership records you file with the county auditor. Inconsistencies may create complications if Maryland audits your residency status or the legitimacy of your tax claims.

### 3. Foreign Tax Credit Coordination

On your Canadian T1 General return (filed with the Canada Revenue Agency), you will report your US rental income and claim a **foreign tax credit** for US federal and Maryland state taxes paid. Your ITIN must match the IRS records to ensure proper credit allocation. Ensure all US returns filed with your ITIN are exact copies of what you report to the CRA.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Incomplete Documentation**

The most common error is submitting Form W-7 without certified copies of required documents. The IRS will reject incomplete applications. Use original documents with official seals, not photocopies.

**2. Signature Issues**

Signing Form W-7 without US-based notarization causes rejections. Canadian notarization alone is insufficient unless accompanied by an apostille. Budget an additional 2–4 weeks for apostille processing.

**3. Misaligned Information**

If your name on Form W-7 differs from your passport or from your rental deed, processing delays extend 4–8 weeks. Use identical legal names across all documents.

**4. Incorrect "Reason for Applying"**

Selecting the wrong box can result in an ITIN that is restricted for certain purposes. Rental property owners should explicitly indicate "Non-resident alien filing Form 1040-NR for rental income" to ensure full eligibility.

**5. Failing to Submit with First Return**

Do not attempt to file Form 1040-NR without an ITIN. The IRS will reject it. Submit Form W-7 first, wait for ITIN assignment (4–6 weeks), then file your return.

## Key Deadlines

- **ITIN Processing**: 4–6 weeks under normal circumstances; extend to 8–10 weeks during peak tax season (January–April)
- **Tax Return Filing Deadline**: June 15, 2024 (automatic extension for non-residents filing US returns from outside the US)
- **Maryland Return Deadline**: Same as federal (typically April 15 with extension to June 15 for residents filing from outside Maryland)
- **FIRPTA Withholding**: Must be reported within 10 days of property sale; ITIN must be active before sale closing

## Key Takeaways for Maryland Landlords

- **You cannot file Form 1040-NR without an ITIN**: Begin Form W-7 processing immediately upon acquiring Maryland rental property; do not wait until tax return filing season to apply.

- **Notarization and certified documents are non-negotiable**: Invest in US-based notarization and apostille certification of your passport and birth certificate to avoid rejection and 4–8 week processing delays.

- **Your ITIN must align across federal, state, and Canadian filings**: Ensure your ITIN on Form 1040-NR, Maryland Form 502 NR, and your Canadian T1 General return are identical to prevent IRS correspondence, state audit complications, and CRA foreign tax credit disputes.
