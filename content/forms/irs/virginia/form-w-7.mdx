# Form W-7 (ITIN Application) for Canadian Landlords with Virginia Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN), is the official IRS document you must file to obtain a US taxpayer identification number if you don't have a Social Security Number (SSN). An ITIN is a nine-digit identification number formatted like an SSN but used exclusively for tax filing and reporting purposes.

For Canadian landlords, the ITIN serves two critical functions:
1. **Tax filing**: It allows you to file Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) to report rental income from Virginia properties
2. **FIRPTA compliance**: It enables proper withholding on the sale of US real property under the Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA)

Without an ITIN, you cannot file a US federal return, file state returns in Virginia, or comply with FIRPTA rules—which can expose you to significant penalties and jeopardize the sale of your property.

## How W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Virginia

### Federal Tax Obligation

As a Canadian resident earning rental income from Virginia property, you are a **non-resident alien** for US tax purposes. Under US tax law, non-residents are taxed on US-source income at graduated rates. Rental income (rent, security deposits applied to repairs, parking fees) is classified as US-source income.

You must file **Form 1040-NR** annually to report this income. The 1040-NR requires an ITIN—the IRS will not accept a Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN).

### Virginia State Tax Obligation

Virginia imposes a state income tax of **5.75%** on rental income earned by non-residents. You must file **Form 540NR** (or the equivalent Virginia non-resident return) to comply with state requirements. Virginia also requires an ITIN or equivalent identification number on state returns.

Additionally, Virginia's property tax rate averages **0.82%** of assessed value, though rates vary by locality. Property taxes are deductible on your federal return (Schedule E) but not on Virginia's state return.

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Considerations

The Canada-US Income Tax Treaty (Article XXIII) addresses taxation of real property income. As a Canadian resident, you may benefit from treaty provisions, but you must still file US returns and obtain an ITIN to claim treaty benefits or file protective elections.

On your Canadian **T1 General** return, you'll report worldwide income, including Virginia rental income in Canadian dollars. You can claim a **foreign tax credit** for US federal and Virginia state taxes paid, reducing your Canadian tax liability. However, you cannot claim the foreign tax credit without proof of filing (IRS transcript, Notice of Assessment).

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if:
- You are a non-resident alien (for tax purposes) without an SSN
- You earn US-source income (rental income from Virginia property)
- You are required to file a US tax return (Form 1040-NR)
- You need an ITIN to comply with FIRPTA withholding on property sales

**You do not need an ITIN if:**
- You have an existing SSN (e.g., from prior US employment)
- You have applied for an SSN and it is pending

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete and File Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing W-7, collect:
- **Proof of foreign status**: Your Canadian passport (valid or expired within the past 5 years)
- **Proof of residence**: A Canadian utility bill, bank statement, or property tax bill (dated within the past 12 months)
- **US tax return**: A copy of Form 1040-NR (or whichever return requires the ITIN)
- **IRS documents**: Any correspondence from the IRS relevant to your account

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately

**Part I – Personal Information**
- Enter your full name exactly as it appears on your passport
- Provide your date of birth
- Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Provide a Canadian mailing address

**Part II – Type of Applicant**
- Select the box that applies; for rental income filers, typically **"c) Nonresident alien individual required to file a US income tax return"**
- Provide your visa type if applicable (most Canadian landlords select "other")

**Part III – ITIN Status**
- Check the appropriate box confirming you don't have an SSN
- If you've previously been assigned an ITIN, provide that number

**Part IV – Certification**
- Sign and date the form
- Include your Canadian phone number and email

### Step 3: Attach Required Documents

The IRS requires certified copies or originals:
- A **notarized copy** of your Canadian passport (photo page and one other identifying page)
- An acceptable **proof of residence** (utility bill or bank statement)
- Your **Form 1040-NR** (copy)

Do **not** mail original documents. Use certified copies from a Canadian notary public.

### Step 4: Submit to the IRS

You have two filing options:

**Option A: File by Mail**
- Send Form W-7, attachments, and a cover letter to the IRS ITIN Unit:
  - Internal Revenue Service
  - ITIN Operation
  - Austin, TX 73301 USA
- Include a copy of your 1040-NR with the W-7
- Processing time: 60–90 days (may extend during peak periods)

**Option B: File Through an Acceptance Agent (Recommended)**
- The IRS has designated acceptance agents in Canada (primarily in major cities like Toronto, Vancouver, Montreal)
- Acceptance agents verify documents in person, reducing rejection risk
- Processing time: 2–4 weeks after submission
- Find an agent at: www.irs.gov (search "acceptance agents")

## Virginia-Specific Considerations

### Virginia's Non-Resident Return Requirements

Once you obtain your ITIN, you must file Virginia's non-resident income tax return. Virginia does not have a separate ITIN form; you'll use your IRS-issued ITIN on the Virginia return.

Key points:
- **Form 540NR**: File this form if you earned Virginia-source income
- **Virginia tax rate**: 5.75% (flat rate, no brackets)
- **Due date**: Same as federal return (April 15 or June 15 if extended)
- **Payment method**: Virginia accepts electronic payment via its tax platform; Canadian landlords may face delays with checks mailed from Canada

### Virginia Property Tax Deduction

Virginia property taxes are **not deductible** on the Virginia state return itself (Virginia does not allow a deduction for property taxes paid). However, they **are deductible** on your federal Form 1040-NR, Schedule E (rental real estate). This creates a tax benefit: you pay Virginia tax on gross rental income but deduct property taxes on the federal return.

### Local Jurisdiction Considerations

Virginia's property tax is assessed by locality. For example:
- Fairfax County: ~0.83% of assessed value
- Arlington County: ~0.78%
- Richmond City: ~0.55%

The assessed value may differ from your purchase price. Obtain the assessment from your local tax assessor's website.

### FIRPTA and ITIN

If you sell your Virginia rental property, the IRS requires a **FIRPTA withholding** equal to 15% of the gross sale price (some states require additional withholding). Your ITIN is required to:
- File Form 8288-B (Statement of US Real Property Disposition by Foreign Person)
- Claim FIRPTA exemption or reduced withholding
- Receive credit for withheld amounts on your 1040-NR

Without an ITIN on file with the IRS, you cannot claim the withholding as a credit.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Mismatched names**: Ensure your name on W-7 matches your passport exactly. Nicknames or shortened names cause rejections.

2. **Expired or invalid passport**: Your passport must be valid or expired less than 5 years. A very old or expired passport (5+ years expired) is not accepted.

3. **Incomplete documentation**: Photocopied documents (not certified) are rejected. Always use a notarized copy.

4. **Filing W-7 without a 1040-NR**: The IRS will not process W-7 without an attached tax return. File both together.
