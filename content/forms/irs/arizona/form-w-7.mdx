# Form W-7 Guide for Canadian Landlords with Arizona Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS application used by non-US citizens and non-residents to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted similarly to a Social Security Number (SSN) but exclusively for tax filing and compliance purposes.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves as your US tax identifier when you don't have—and aren't eligible for—a US Social Security Number. The ITIN allows you to:

- File non-resident US income tax returns (Form 1040-NR)
- Comply with FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding obligations
- Report US-source rental income to the IRS
- Claim treaty benefits under the Canada-US Tax Treaty

## How Form W-7 Applies to Arizona Landlords

### Arizona Tax Environment

Arizona imposes state income tax on rental income at a flat rate of **2.5%** on net rental income for non-residents. This is lower than most US states, making Arizona an attractive jurisdiction for Canadian real estate investors. However, this also means you must file an Arizona Nonresident Tax Return (Form 140-NR) in addition to your federal 1040-NR.

While Arizona does **not** impose state withholding on non-resident rental income (unlike California, which withholds 7.5%), you remain responsible for filing and paying tax on net rental income. Property tax in Arizona averages **0.62% of assessed value annually**—among the lowest in the US—making Arizona particularly popular with Canadian investors from Alberta and British Columbia.

### FIRPTA and Withholding

When you sell Arizona rental property, FIRPTA requires a **15% federal withholding** on the gross sale price (with limited exceptions). To claim an ITIN and potentially qualify for FIRPTA withholding reductions under the Canada-US Tax Treaty, you must obtain an ITIN before or at the time of sale.

Under Article XIII of the Canada-US Tax Treaty, Canadian residents may be eligible to reduce or eliminate FIRPTA withholding if they claim treaty benefits. However, claiming these benefits requires an ITIN and proper documentation of Canadian residency.

## Who Files Form W-7?

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

1. **A Canadian resident** (as determined by Canadian residency rules, not immigration status)
2. **A non-resident alien** for US tax purposes (regardless of Canadian citizenship)
3. **Acquiring or holding rental property** in Arizona and requiring a US tax identifier
4. **Required to file a 1040-NR** for rental income or anticipated property sales

You do **not** need an ITIN if you hold US property solely for personal use and have no US tax filing requirement. However, if you generate rental income or will eventually sell the property, an ITIN is essential.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before submitting Form W-7, prepare:

- **Valid passport** (Canadian passport certified copy, notarized)
- **Letter explaining purpose** (state that you own Arizona rental property and require an ITIN for tax filing and FIRPTA purposes)
- **Proof of filing requirement** (copy of draft 1040-NR or rental income documentation)
- **IRS Form 8233** (if claiming treaty benefits for withholding reduction—optional at this stage but useful)

The IRS requires certified copies of your passport. In Canada, you can have a notary public, lawyer, or Canadian bank branch officer certify the copy.

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7

**Line 1a–1c**: Enter your full legal name, date of birth, and country of citizenship (Canada).

**Line 2**: Enter your current Canadian address (your mailing address in Canada).

**Line 3**: Select the appropriate reason you need an ITIN. For Arizona landlords, choose:
- "Tax return filing" (if reporting rental income), OR
- "Other" and specify FIRPTA withholding / non-resident alien tax compliance

**Line 4–6**: Leave blank if you've never applied for an ITIN. If you previously applied, provide details.

**Line 7**: Answer whether you are a US citizen or national. Answer **NO**.

**Line 8**: Confirm your immigration status. As a Canadian resident, select **"Other"** and write "Canadian resident."

**Line 9**: Select "Yes" to indicate you are requesting an ITIN to satisfy a US tax filing requirement.

### Step 3: Attach Required Documents

**Attachment 1: Proof of Identity**
- Certified copy of your Canadian passport (front and back pages)

**Attachment 2: Proof of US Tax Filing Requirement**
- A signed statement describing your Arizona rental property (address, acquisition date, annual rental income)
- Reference to the year you intend to file a 1040-NR
- Statement that you require an ITIN for federal and Arizona state tax compliance

**Attachment 3: Declaration of Residency** (optional but helpful)
- Statement confirming you are a resident of Canada for tax purposes
- If applicable, a copy of your Notice of Assessment from the Canada Revenue Agency showing your Canadian address

### Step 4: Sign and Certify

You must sign Form W-7 in front of a **notary public** or authorized US representative (such as a US embassy or consulate official, or IRS official). You **cannot** sign the form yourself and send it unsigned.

**Canadian notarization is acceptable** if the notary certifies that you appeared before them and signed the form in their presence. The certification must state: "I certify that I have personally examined the signatory and I am satisfied that the signatory is the individual making this application."

**Processing time**: 4–6 weeks. ITINs are valid for three years if you don't file a required federal return during that period. If you file a 1040-NR, your ITIN remains valid.

**Alternative: In-person filing** at a US embassy or consulate in Canada (if you have an appointment). This may expedite processing.

## Arizona-Specific Considerations

### Arizona Nonresident Return Filing

Once you receive your ITIN, you must also file **Arizona Form 140-NR** (Nonresident Income Tax Return) annually if you generate Arizona rental income. File this simultaneously with your federal 1040-NR.

Arizona allows a **credit for foreign taxes paid**, which you can use to offset the 2.5% Arizona tax by claiming the US federal tax you paid on the same income. This integration with the federal return prevents double taxation.

### No State Withholding

Unlike California, Arizona does **not** require withholding on non-resident rental income. However, you remain personally responsible for quarterly estimated tax payments to Arizona (Form 140-ES) if your annual Arizona tax liability exceeds $500.

### FIRPTA Sale Reporting

When you sell Arizona rental property:

1. Obtain an ITIN before closing (provide to the escrow agent)
2. File a federal 1040-NR in the year of sale to report the gain
3. Claim FIRPTA treaty relief (Form 8288-B) if eligible, which may reduce withholding from 15% to 0–5% depending on treaty qualification
4. File Arizona Form 140-NR to report Arizona-source gain

### Property Tax Appraisal

Arizona property tax assessments are relatively straightforward and transparent. The effective tax rate of **0.62%** applies uniformly. No additional state-level income withholding applies to rent or sale proceeds, simplifying your compliance obligations relative to other US states.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Submitting an unsigned Form W-7**: The form must be notarized and signed. Unsigned applications are rejected.

2. **Providing a non-certified passport copy**: The IRS requires certified copies. A photocopy alone is insufficient.

3. **Not explaining your US tax filing requirement**: Blank or vague explanations on Line 3 cause delays. Clearly state "I own rental property at [Arizona address] and file Form 1040-NR."

4. **Confusing ITIN with an SSN**: An ITIN does not grant you a Social Security Number or any work authorization. It is strictly a tax identifier.
