# Form W-7: ITIN Application Guide for Canadian Landlords with Kansas Rental Property

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the application form you submit to the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number that functions similarly to a US Social Security Number (SSN) but is specifically issued to non-US citizens and residents who have US tax filing obligations.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves as your primary US tax identifier. It appears on all US federal tax forms you file, including Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) and is essential for complying with Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) withholding requirements.

## How the ITIN Applies Specifically in Kansas

As a Canadian landlord owning rental property in Kansas, you face a unique intersection of federal and state tax obligations:

**Federal Level:**
The IRS requires you to file Form 1040-NR if you have rental income from Kansas property. Rental income is considered "effectively connected income" (ECI) and is subject to federal tax at ordinary rates (currently 10–37% depending on your tax bracket). You cannot file a US return—federal or state—without a valid ITIN.

**Kansas State Level:**
Kansas imposes state income tax at a flat rate of 5.7% on all taxable income, including rental income from property located within the state. Non-residents who own Kansas rental property must file Form K-40 (Kansas Individual Income Tax Return) or Form K-40N (for nonresidents) to report this income to the Kansas Department of Revenue. You will need your ITIN on the Kansas return as well.

**Property Taxes:**
Kansas has an average effective property tax rate of 1.41% of property value (combined state and local). While property tax is not a Kansas income tax issue, understanding this adds to your overall cost basis and rental yield calculations.

**FIRPTA Compliance:**
If you sell your Kansas rental property, FIRPTA rules require a withholding of 15% of the gross sale price (or 21% in some circumstances). Any buyer's agent or title company will request your ITIN before closing. Without an ITIN, the transaction may be delayed or the full 21% withholding rate may apply.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

- A Canadian citizen or permanent resident without a US Social Security Number
- Required to file a US federal tax return (1040-NR) due to rental income from Kansas property
- Subject to FIRPTA withholding on the sale of Kansas real property
- Required to file a Kansas nonresident income tax return (Form K-40N)

You do **not** need to file W-7 if you already hold a valid ITIN or Social Security Number.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### **Step 1: Gather Required Documentation**

Before you begin, assemble:
- Your valid Canadian passport (or other government-issued ID)
- Proof of your non-US residency (e.g., Canadian driver's license, property tax bill from Canada)
- Proof of your US tax filing requirement (a copy of your 1040-NR draft or FIRPTA sale documentation)
- Your Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN)
- Your mailing address (both Canadian and, if applicable, a US address for correspondence)

### **Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Sections**

**Part 1: Personal Information**
- Enter your full legal name as it appears on your Canadian passport
- Enter your date of birth
- Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Leave the Social Security Number field blank

**Part 2: ITIN Reason Code**
- Select reason code **a** (you are required to file a US tax return)
- This is the primary reason Canadian landlords apply for an ITIN

**Part 3: Mailing Address**
- Provide your current Canadian address as your permanent address
- Provide a US address if you maintain one or use a property manager's address in Kansas

**Part 4: Country-Specific Information**
- Country of birth: Canada
- Country of citizenship: Canada
- Type of visa: N/A (leave blank, as you are not a US immigrant visa holder)

**Part 5: Declaration**
- Sign and date the form
- The signature must match your passport

### **Step 3: Attach Supporting Documentation**

You must submit original or certified copies of your passport and one additional identity document. Many Canadian landlords submit:
- Certified copy of Canadian passport
- Original or certified copy of Canadian driver's license or provincial photo ID

### **Step 4: Choose Your Filing Method**

**Option A: File with Your 1040-NR**
Attach completed Form W-7, signed passport copy, and supporting ID to your Form 1040-NR. Mail to the IRS address for nonresident aliens (varies by state; for Kansas renters, typically the Philadelphia ITIN Unit).

**Option B: File Before Your 1040-NR**
Submit Form W-7 standalone with all supporting documents to expedite ITIN issuance. Send to:

Internal Revenue Service  
ITIN Operation  
P.O. Box 447  
Bensalem, PA 19020  
USA

**Option C: Use a Certified Acceptance Agent (CAA)**
Many cross-border tax firms in Canada or Kansas-based CPAs serve as IRS Certified Acceptance Agents. They can certify your passport copies, reducing document uncertainty and speeding processing.

### **Step 5: Track Your Application**

The IRS processes Form W-7 applications within 2–6 weeks if all documentation is complete. You can track your application via the IRS website using a receipt number issued when your application is accepted. Do not expect your ITIN by mail; the IRS typically notifies you only if there are missing items.

## Kansas-Specific Considerations

**1. Nonresident Income Tax Filing**

Kansas Form K-40N (Kansas Nonresident/Part-Year Resident Income Tax Return) must be filed by April 15 (or your applicable filing extension deadline) to report your rental income and property taxes. You will enter your ITIN in the identification section. Kansas allows a credit for federal taxes paid on the same income, which reduces your Kansas liability.

**2. Rental Expense Deductions**

Kansas allows you to deduct legitimate rental expenses:
- Mortgage interest (not principal)
- Property management fees
- Utilities (if you cover them)
- Repairs and maintenance
- Property taxes (the 1.41% average cited)
- Insurance
- Depreciation

These deductions reduce your Kansas taxable income dollar-for-dollar.

**3. FIRPTA and Kansas Real Estate Sales**

If you sell your Kansas property, FIRPTA withholding (federal) applies regardless of your ITIN status, but having an ITIN ensures smooth closing processes. Kansas does not impose a separate state FIRPTA withholding; federal FIRPTA is sufficient.

**4. Canada-US Tax Treaty Relief**

As a Canadian resident, you may benefit from Article 6 of the Canada-US Income Tax Treaty. The treaty may reduce your US tax liability on rental income, particularly if Canada has already taxed the same income. File Form 8833 (Treaty-Based Return Position Disclosure) with your 1040-NR if you claim treaty benefits that reduce your reported US income.

**5. Canadian Tax Credits**

On your Canadian T1 return (Schedule 1), you can claim a foreign tax credit for US federal taxes paid on your Kansas rental income. This prevents double taxation on the same income. Keep copies of all IRS notices of assessment.

## Common Mistakes

- **Missing or expired passport copies:** The IRS will reject applications with photocopies of expired documents.
- **Mismatched signatures:** Your signature on Form W-7 must match your passport signature exactly.
- **Incomplete Part 2:** Failing to select the correct ITIN reason code delays processing.
- **Submitting without supporting ID:** A passport alone is insufficient; you must provide two forms of identification.
- **Filing Form W-7 after a FIRPTA sale closes:** Contact the title company immediately if you lack an ITIN; delays of even one day can trigger full 21% withholding.
- **Confusing ITIN with a Social Security Number:** An ITIN does not permit you to work in the US or confer immigration status.
