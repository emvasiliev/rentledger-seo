## Cross-Border Rental Property Taxation: A Guide for New Brunswick Owners of West Virginia Property

Owning rental property across the Canada–US border creates a unique tax environment. As a New Brunswick resident with a West Virginia rental property, you're subject to tax filing requirements in three jurisdictions: Canada, the United States (federal), and West Virginia State. This guide walks you through each requirement with specific forms, rates, and deadlines.

## Why This Combination Matters

West Virginia has a meaningful state income tax (6.5%) and a low property tax rate (0.59% average effective rate). New Brunswick has no provincial sales tax above the federal HST, but HST does not apply to real property. Most importantly, the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) and the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) both tax worldwide income, and they coordinate through information-sharing agreements.

The key risk: **If you don't file correctly in both countries, you'll face double taxation plus penalties.** The solution involves strategic use of tax elections, foreign tax credits, and proper documentation.

## CRA Obligations for Canadian Tax Residents

### File Form T776: Statement of Real Estate Rentals

You must report all rental income from your West Virginia property on **Form T776**, filed with your annual Canadian personal income tax return (Form T1 General). This applies whether you file as a Canadian resident or non-resident.

**What to report on T776:**
- Gross rental income (converted to CAD at the exchange rate on the receipt date, or use Bank of Canada annual average: 1 USD = 1.36 CAD)
- Eligible expenses: property taxes, insurance, mortgage interest, utilities, repairs, property management fees, and depreciation (capital cost allowance, or CCA)
- Foreign exchange gains or losses on USD income

### File Form T1135: Foreign Income Verification Statement

If you own real property outside Canada, you must file **Form T1135** with your tax return. This form reports the fair market value of the West Virginia property in Canadian dollars as of December 31.

**Filing deadline:** Same as your tax return (typically June 15 for individuals).

**Penalty for late filing:** $25 per day, up to $2,500, plus potential accuracy-related penalties if CRA challenges unreported income.

### Claim a Foreign Tax Credit

You'll pay tax to the IRS and West Virginia State. The CRA allows you to claim a **federal non-refundable foreign tax credit (FTC)** on **Schedule 1 of Form T1 General** to avoid double taxation.

**How it works:**
- Calculate your Canadian federal tax on worldwide income (including the WV rental income in CAD)
- Subtract the tax you paid to the IRS and West Virginia (converted to CAD)
- You cannot claim more credit than the Canadian federal tax on that foreign income
- Any unused credit may carry back one year or forward up to five years

**Example:** If you paid $3,000 USD in combined US federal and West Virginia state tax, convert it to CAD ($3,000 × 1.36 = $4,080 CAD) and claim it on your Schedule 1.

## US Federal Tax Obligations

### Obtain an ITIN

If you don't have a US Social Security Number (SSN), you must apply for an **Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)** from the IRS. Use **Form W-7** (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number).

**Where to send:** IRS ITIN Unit, P.O. Box 149342, Austin, TX 78714-9342.

**Processing time:** 6–7 weeks (longer if documents require authentication).

**Why it matters:** You need an ITIN to file Form 1040-NR and to avoid the default 30% withholding on rental income.

### File Form 1040-NR: US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return

As a Canadian resident (non-US citizen), you file **Form 1040-NR**, not Form 1040. This return is for nonresident aliens with US-source income.

**Filing deadline:** June 15 (same as Canadian deadline, though IRS typically allows an automatic extension to October 15).

**Where to send:** IRS International Section, P.O. Box 409101, Ogden, UT 84409.

### File Schedule E and Report Rental Income

Attach **Schedule E** (Supplemental Income and Loss) to your Form 1040-NR. Report:
- Gross rental income from the West Virginia property
- Property address and VIN (if applicable) or rental property identification
- Deductible expenses (mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, repairs, utilities, depreciation)

**Key point:** Schedule E on Form 1040-NR allows you to deduct expenses, unlike the default withholding approach (which would tax you on gross income).

### Make a Section 871(d) Election

**This is critical.** By default, the IRS withholds 30% of gross rental income from a nonresident alien. You can avoid this by electing under **Section 871(d)** of the Internal Revenue Code to be taxed on net (gross income minus deductions) instead of gross.

**How to elect:**
- File **Form 8288-B** (Certificate of Withholding—Section 1445 Transfers) is not what you need. Instead, attach a statement to your Form 1040-NR titled "Section 871(d) Election" or consult a cross-border tax professional.
- The election is effective automatically once you file Form 1040-NR reporting net rental income (with expenses deducted).
- You must file the return timely; the election cannot be made retroactively.

**Tax rate:** Once the election is in effect, you pay regular federal rates on net income (10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, or 37%, depending on income bracket).

### Example Impact:

**Without Section 871(d) election:**
- Gross rent: $10,000 USD
- Withholding: $3,000 USD (30%)
- Owed to IRS: $0 (withholding covers it)
- Result: You lose deductions and overpay

**With Section 871(d) election:**
- Gross rent: $10,000 USD
- Deductible expenses: $4,000 USD
- Net taxable income: $6,000 USD
- Tax at 22% bracket: $1,320 USD
- Result: You save $1,680 USD and claim all expenses

## West Virginia State Tax Obligations

### File Form IT-140: West Virginia Nonresident Income Tax Return

As a non-resident of West Virginia, you must file **Form IT-140** (West Virginia Individual Income Tax Return—Nonresident) with the West Virginia Department of Revenue.

**Filing deadline:** Same as your federal return (June 15, or October 15 with extension).

**Where to send:** West Virginia Department of Revenue, Tax Compliance Division, P.O. Box 3784, Charleston, WV 25337-3784.

### Understand West Virginia Tax Rate and Withholding

- **State income tax rate:** 6.5% (flat rate on taxable income from WV-source rental property)
- **Withholding requirement:** Some property managers or rental agents may withhold state tax. If not, you must pay estimated tax by Form IT-40-W (Estimated Tax Voucher).

**No NR6 withholding:** Unlike Canada's Part XIII withholding (25%), West Virginia does not impose withholding at the source on nonresident rental income. You're responsible for paying through estimated payments or when filing your return.

### Deduct West Virginia Property Taxes

West Virginia's average effective property tax rate is 0.59% of assessed value. These property taxes are deductible on your Form IT-140 and your US federal Form 1040-NR Schedule E.

## Canada's Part XIII Withholding on Rental Income

The CRA can impose **Part XIII withholding** of 25% on gross rental income paid to a non-resident. However, you can avoid this by:

1. **Filing Form NR6** (Undertaking—Tax Withholding) before the first rental payment is made. This form must be signed by you and the property manager/tenant and filed with CRA.
2. **Proving to CRA that you've filed Form 1040-NR with the IRS.** Once the IRS has your ITIN and you're on file, CRA may waive Part XIII.

**Strategic timing:** File your US returns (federal and WV state) and Form NR6 **before** you receive your first rental payment to avoid the default withholding.
