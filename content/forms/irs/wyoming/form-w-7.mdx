# Form W-7: ITIN Application for Canadian Landlords with Wyoming Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the official IRS application document used to request an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number issued by the Internal Revenue Service to individuals who are required to file US tax returns but do not have a Social Security Number (SSN).

For Canadian landlords, the ITIN serves as your US tax identifier for federal income tax filing, FIRPTA compliance, and state-level reporting requirements. While Wyoming has no state income tax, you will still need a federal ITIN to file Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) and to comply with Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) withholding rules.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Wyoming

As a Canadian citizen and non-resident alien with rental property in Wyoming, you are required to:

1. **File Form 1040-NR annually** to report US-source income from your Wyoming rental property
2. **Comply with FIRPTA withholding** when you sell the property (15% federal withholding on the sales price)
3. **Report rental income and deductions** on Schedule E (Form 1040-NR)
4. **Claim foreign tax credits** on your Canadian T1 return for US taxes paid

The ITIN is essential because it allows the IRS to track your US tax obligations and ties your 1040-NR filing to your tax account. Without an ITIN, you cannot file a valid federal tax return as a non-resident alien.

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Considerations

Under Article IV of the Canada-US Income Tax Treaty, Canadian residents are generally considered non-resident aliens for US tax purposes if they do not meet the US substantial presence test. This treaty relationship does not eliminate your requirement to obtain an ITIN or file Form 1040-NR; rather, it may provide relief from certain US tax provisions and can help you claim foreign tax credits on your Canadian return.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must apply for an ITIN if you are:

- A non-resident alien with US-source rental income
- Required to file a US federal income tax return (Form 1040-NR)
- Subject to FIRPTA withholding on US real property sales
- Unable to obtain a Social Security Number

**Canadian landlords do not qualify for an SSN** because SSNs are issued only to US citizens, lawful permanent residents, and certain work-authorized nonimmigrants. Therefore, an ITIN is your only option to obtain a US tax identification number.

You are not required to file Form W-7 if you have already been assigned an ITIN by the IRS, or if you are filing Form 1040-NR for the first time and applying for your ITIN simultaneously.

## Step-by-Step Instructions for Completing Form W-7

### Part A: Applicant Information

- **Line 1**: Enter your full legal name exactly as it appears on your passport
- **Line 2**: Enter your mailing address (can be a US or Canadian address)
- **Line 3**: City, state/province, and postal code
- **Line 4**: Country of citizenship: Canada
- **Line 5**: Date of birth (MMDDYYYY format)
- **Line 6**: Check the box for "Non-Resident Alien Individual"
- **Line 7**: Country of residence: Canada
- **Line 8**: US telephone number (optional; if you have one)

### Part B: Reason for Applying

- **Check the box**: "Nonresident alien engaged in a US trade or business" (this applies to rental property owners)
- **Provide the primary reason**: "US rental property income from Wyoming real estate"

### Part C: IRS Use Only

Leave this section blank; the IRS will complete it.

### Part D: Required Documentation

Attach certified copies (not originals) of:

1. **Proof of Identity**: Valid passport photo page
2. **Proof of Foreign Status**: Your passport information page or national identification document
3. **Supporting Documentation**: If applying with Form 1040-NR, include a copy of your tax return

All documents must be certified by a notary public or attorney. If submitting documents from Canada, have them certified by a Canadian notary or lawyer.

## Wyoming-Specific Considerations

### No State Income Tax

Wyoming has **zero state income tax**, which simplifies your tax filing requirements considerably. You will not be required to file a Wyoming state income tax return or obtain a Wyoming-specific tax identification number.

However, Wyoming does impose:

- **Property tax**: Average effective rate of **0.61%** on real property (among the lowest in the US)
- **Possible county or municipal taxes**: Depending on your property's location within Wyoming
- **Possible rental licensing requirements**: Some Wyoming municipalities require rental property registration

These state and local obligations do not require an ITIN but should be addressed separately with your local Wyoming county assessor or municipality.

### Federal Tax Filing Only

Since Wyoming has no state income tax, your ITIN is used exclusively for **federal tax purposes**. Your Form 1040-NR and Schedule E (rental income/deductions) are filed only with the IRS, not with any Wyoming state agency.

### FIRPTA Withholding in Wyoming

If you sell your Wyoming rental property, the buyer (or their agent) must withhold **15% of the sale price** as FIRPTA withholding. The withholding is remitted to the IRS under your ITIN. You can claim this withholding as a credit on your 1040-NR for the year of sale.

## Common Mistakes When Filing Form W-7

1. **Submitting unverified documents**: All supporting documents must be certified by a notary or attorney. Self-certified or stamped copies are typically rejected.

2. **Inconsistent name spelling**: Ensure your name matches exactly across Form W-7, your passport, and your 1040-NR. Discrepancies delay processing.

3. **Missing supporting documents**: The most common reason for rejection is submitting the application without certified passport copies or proof of foreign status.

4. **Using a US address when applying from Canada**: While you may provide a US mailing address, clearly indicate on the form that you are a Canadian resident if you are. This helps the IRS correctly classify you as a non-resident alien.

5. **Failing to indicate the reason for the ITIN**: Be specific. Write "US rental property income" or "FIRPTA reporting" rather than leaving it vague.

6. **Not applying early enough**: Do not wait until the tax filing deadline to apply for an ITIN. Processing typically takes 4–6 weeks.

## Key Deadlines and Timelines

- **ITIN Application**: File Form W-7 **at least 6–8 weeks before you need to file your 1040-NR** to allow for IRS processing
- **1040-NR Filing Deadline**: June 15 for non-residents (note: this is later than the standard April 15 deadline)
- **Estimated Tax Payments**: If you expect to owe $1,000 or more in federal taxes, you may be required to make quarterly estimated payments using Form 1040-ES-NR. Deadlines are April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15.
- **FIRPTA Reporting**: If you sell your property, FIRPTA withholding must be reported on Form 8288 within 20 days of closing

## Filing Your Form W-7

### How to Submit

You can file Form W-7 by mail to:

**Internal Revenue Service**  
ITIN Operations  
Austin, TX 73301  
USA

Include:
- Completed Form W-7
- Certified copies of required documents
- If applying with 1040-NR: a copy of your completed return

### Processing Time

Expect 4–6 weeks for processing if submitting by mail. The IRS will mail your ITIN assignment letter to the address provided on the form.

### Tracking Your Application

You cannot track Form W-7 applications online through the IRS website. Allow the full processing timeframe before contacting the IRS.
