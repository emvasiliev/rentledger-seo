# Form W-7: Getting Your ITIN as a Canadian Landlord in Hawaii

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the official IRS application to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number issued by the US Internal Revenue Service to individuals who are required to have a US tax identification number but are not eligible for a Social Security Number (SSN).

For Canadian landlords owning rental property in Hawaii, the ITIN serves a critical function: it allows you to file US tax returns, report rental income, claim deductions, and most importantly, comply with FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding obligations when you eventually sell the property.

Without an ITIN, you cannot legally file Form 1040-NR (Non-resident Alien Income Tax Return) or establish compliance with Hawaiian and federal tax authorities. The IRS will not process your rental income tax returns without it.

## Why Canadian Landlords in Hawaii Need an ITIN

### FIRPTA Requirements

When a non-resident alien (which includes Canadian citizens) sells US real property, the buyer or buyer's agent must withhold 15% of the sale price under FIRPTA rules. This withholding is held in trust and applied against your final US tax liability. To claim a refund of excess withholding or to establish that a reduced withholding rate applies, you **must have an ITIN**.

### Hawaii's Unique Tax Requirements

Hawaii imposes state-level taxation on non-residents who earn Hawaii-source income, including rental income from real property located in Hawaii. You will be required to file:

- **Form N-15 (Hawaii Non-resident Tax Return)** with an ITIN
- State income tax at rates up to 11% (depending on your brackets)
- **Hawaii General Excise Tax (GET)** at 4% on rental income—a unique Hawaii tax not found in most US states

The GET is applied to gross rental proceeds before deductions and is collected as part of Hawaii's business and income tax system. This makes Hawaii significantly more expensive for non-resident landlords than other US states.

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Benefit

The Canada-US Tax Treaty (Article XXII) allows Canadian residents to claim a foreign tax credit on their Canadian tax return for taxes paid to Hawaii and the US federal government. To claim this credit on your Canadian T1 General return, you will need to report your ITIN on your Canadian tax filing. The ITIN is recognized by the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) as a valid foreign tax identification number for this purpose.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you meet **all** of the following criteria:

- You are a non-resident alien for US tax purposes (Canadian citizen without US residency status)
- You own US real property generating rental income in Hawaii
- You do not have a US Social Security Number
- You are required to file a US income tax return (Form 1040-NR) or need the ITIN for FIRPTA withholding purposes
- You have a valid reason for needing a US tax identification number

You do **not** need Form W-7 if you have already been issued an SSN or if you are a US citizen or permanent resident (green card holder).

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, compile the following documents:

- **Valid passport or national identity document** (Canadian passport is ideal)
- **Proof of housing in Canada** (utility bill, property tax assessment, lease agreement dated within last 12 months)
- **Proof of Hawaii rental property ownership** (deed, property tax bill, mortgage statement)
- **Completed Form W-7** (available on IRS.gov)
- **Copy of your lease agreements or property management contract**
- **Bank statements showing rental income deposits**

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately

**Part I – Personal Information:**
- Enter your full legal name as it appears on your passport
- Provide your date of birth (MMDDYYYY format)
- Enter your current Canadian address
- Do **not** leave the SSN field blank; write "N/A" or cross it out

**Part II – Type of ITIN Applicant:**
Check the appropriate box. For a Canadian landlord, you will typically select:
- "Other individual" (if you have no US visa status)
- Or "Resident alien" (if you have applied for US residency)

**Part III – Reason for Applying:**
Select:
- "US tax return filing requirement" (this is your primary reason as a rental property owner)
- You may also check "FIRPTA withholding" if you anticipate selling the property

**Part IV – Residency Status:**
- Check "Nonresident alien"
- Enter Canada as your country of citizenship and residence

### Step 3: Attach Required Documentation

Prepare two **certified copies** (notarized by a Canadian notary public) of your passport. The IRS requires:

1. Certified copy of passport identification page
2. Certified copy of passport biographical page showing your date of birth

Hawaii property documentation should include a certified copy of the deed or property tax assessment.

### Step 4: Submit Form W-7

**Method 1: Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) Application Acceptance Agent**

The IRS recommends submitting Form W-7 **in person** through an ITIN Acceptance Agent. In Hawaii, you can submit through:

- Hawaiian tax preparers or CPA firms authorized as ITIN Acceptance Agents
- Some US embassies or consulates in Canada (though this process can be slow)

If submitting in person in Hawaii, you can:
1. Visit an IRS office in Honolulu
2. Work with a Hawaii-based tax preparer or CPA who acts as an authorized acceptance agent

**Method 2: Mail Submission**

You may mail Form W-7 with supporting documents to:

**Internal Revenue Service**  
**ITIN Operation**  
**P.O. Box 149342**  
**Austin, TX 78714-9342**

Mail submissions take significantly longer (60–120 days) than in-person submissions (typically 15–21 days).

**Method 3: Submit with Your First 1040-NR**

You can attach Form W-7 to your Form 1040-NR (Non-resident Alien Tax Return) when you file your first Hawaiian rental income return. However, this delays processing and may result in a rejection notice if the form is incomplete.

### Step 5: Track Your Application

The IRS will provide you with an acknowledgment number when you submit Form W-7. You can check the status of your application on IRS.gov using:
- Your acknowledgment number
- Your passport number
- Your date of birth

Processing typically takes 2–4 weeks for in-person submissions.

## Hawaii-Specific Considerations for ITIN Applicants

### Hawaii's General Excise Tax (GET)

Hawaii's 4% GET is levied on the gross rent received from your Hawaii property. Unlike federal income tax, which allows deductions, the GET is calculated on **gross receipts**. This means:

- Your $10,000 monthly rent is subject to $400 in GET (4% × $10,000)
- GET is owed whether or not you have a mortgage, property taxes, or maintenance expenses
- GET must be remitted separately to Hawaii Department of Revenue

Your ITIN will be required to register for a Hawaii **General Excise Tax License** (number starting with 3-) issued by the State of Hawaii Department of Revenue & Taxation.

### Hawaii State Income Tax Return (Form N-15)

As a non-resident with Hawaii-source rental income, you must file:

- **Form N-15** (Hawaii Non-resident Tax Return)
- Tax rates range from 1.4% to 11% depending on your income level
- Filing deadline: April 20 (Hawaii's extended deadline, 20 days after federal deadline)
- You must include your ITIN on this state return

The combination of federal 1040-NR filing, Hawaii GET registration, and state Form N-15 filing requires a coordinated tax strategy. Your ITIN must be used consistently across all three filings.

### Property Tax on Hawaii Rental Property

Hawaii's property tax rate averages **0.28%** of assessed value, significantly lower than many US states. However, non-resident owners may face different assessment or valuation rules. Obtain an ITIN before registering the property with Hawaii County (or Honolulu County) tax assessor, as they may require it for non-resident property ownership records.
