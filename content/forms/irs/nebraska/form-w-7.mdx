# Form W-7 Guide for Canadian Landlords with Nebraska Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS application used by non-resident aliens to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted like a Social Security Number (SSN) but designated exclusively for individuals who are not eligible for SSNs but need a US tax identification number for federal tax purposes.

For Canadian landlords, obtaining an ITIN is not optional—it is a mandatory requirement to file US non-resident tax returns, comply with federal withholding obligations, and satisfy Nebraska state tax filing requirements.

## Why Canadian Landlords in Nebraska Need an ITIN

### Federal Requirement

As a non-resident alien with US-source rental income, you must file **Form 1040-NR** (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) with the IRS. The IRS will not process any return without a valid taxpayer identification number. Since Canadian citizens do not qualify for a US Social Security Number based on permanent residency, an ITIN becomes your mandatory US tax identifier.

### Nebraska State Income Tax Requirement

Nebraska imposes a **5.84% state income tax** on rental income earned within the state. Non-resident landlords must file **Nebraska Form 1040N** (Nebraska Adjusted Gross Income Tax Return) and provide a US tax identification number on the return. Without an ITIN, you cannot legally file this return or satisfy your Nebraska tax obligations.

### FIRPTA Compliance

Under the Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA), when you eventually sell your Nebraska rental property, the buyer's closing agent is required to withhold **15% of the gross sale price** as federal tax. To establish FIRPTA withholding certificates and manage the withholding process, an ITIN is essential. Failure to provide an ITIN can result in the full 15% withholding being retained without credit against your actual liability.

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Consideration

The Canada-US Income Tax Treaty (Treaty) provides relief from double taxation and may reduce your US tax rate on rental income to **15%** under certain provisions. However, Treaty benefits require you to file Form 8833 (Treaty-Based Return Position Disclosure), which cannot be submitted without a valid ITIN on your US return. The Treaty does not eliminate the ITIN requirement; it only modifies the tax rate applied.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are:

- A Canadian citizen (or permanent resident) without a US Social Security Number
- Deriving US-source income (rental income from Nebraska property qualifies)
- Required to file a US federal tax return (1040-NR)
- Subject to Nebraska state income tax on that income
- Planning to sell the Nebraska property in the future and require FIRPTA compliance

You do not need to file Form W-7 if you hold a valid US Social Security Number, have a US work visa (H-1B, etc.) that grants SSN eligibility, or have no US tax filing requirements.

## Step-by-Step Guide to Completing Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, assemble the following:

- Your valid Canadian passport or travel document
- Your complete Canadian residential address (as shown on CRA tax returns)
- Your Social Insurance Number (SIN)—used for reference only
- Documentation of your Nebraska rental property (deed, property tax statement, or lease agreement)
- Proof of income source (rental statements, bank records showing rent deposits)

### Step 2: Determine Your Filing Method

The IRS offers three methods to file Form W-7:

**Method 1: ITIN Application Agent**
Use an IRS-certified ITIN application agent (typically found at tax preparation firms, banks, or accounting offices in your US state). The agent verifies your documents in person and submits Form W-7 directly to the IRS.

**Method 2: Mail with Certified Copy**
Submit Form W-7 with a certified copy of your passport and supporting documents by mail to the IRS office for your region. The IRS will verify documents and send your ITIN by mail (6–8 weeks processing).

**Method 3: IRS Online Account (Limited Availability)**
Some applicants may use IRS Online ITIN Application for Agents only, but this is not available for direct individual applications from Canada.

**For Canadian landlords, Method 2 (mail) is most practical.**

### Step 3: Complete Form W-7 Sections

**Part I: Personal Information**
- Enter your full legal name as it appears on your passport
- Enter your date of birth
- Enter your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Enter your Canadian province of residence

**Part II: Mailing Address**
- Enter your residential address in Canada
- This is your primary address for all IRS correspondence

**Part III: US Address (if applicable)**
- Leave blank if you have no US address; enter your property manager's address if you use one in Nebraska

**Part IV: Identification Document**
- Check the box for "Foreign passport"
- Enter passport number and country of issuance
- Enter passport expiration date

**Part V: Reason for Applying**
- Check the box: "Nonresident alien filing a US tax return" or "Other (specify)—rental income from US property"
- Provide the applicable tax year

**Part VI: Country Code**
- Enter CA (Canada)

### Step 4: Submit with Supporting Documents

Mail Form W-7 with the following attachments:

1. Certified copy of your Canadian passport (certified by a notary public or the Canadian embassy/consulate in the US)
2. Proof of Nebraska rental property ownership (property deed, property tax assessment, or utility statement)
3. Evidence of US-source income (rental receipts, bank statements showing deposits from US tenants)
4. Cover letter in English explaining your situation (optional but recommended)

**Important:** Do not mail originals; use certified copies only.

### Step 5: Address and Mailing Instructions

Mail to the IRS ITIN Unit for your region:

**For Nebraska residents:**

IRS ITIN Operations Unit
P.O. Box 447
Covington, KY 41011
USA

Process time: 6–8 weeks via regular mail; faster if using a certified ITIN application agent in Nebraska.

### Step 6: Receive Your ITIN

Once approved, the IRS mails your ITIN to your Canadian address. The notice includes your nine-digit ITIN. **Use this number on all future US tax filings**, including:

- Form 1040-NR (federal non-resident return)
- Nebraska Form 1040N (state return)
- FIRPTA withholding certificates
- Form 8833 (Treaty-based return position disclosure)

## Nebraska-Specific Considerations

### State Income Tax Filing Alignment

Nebraska's 5.84% income tax rate applies to all non-resident rental income. You must file Nebraska Form 1040N concurrently with your federal Form 1040-NR. Both require your ITIN. Nebraska does not recognize ITINs as substitute identification; you must provide the ITIN exactly as issued by the IRS.

### Property Tax Context

Nebraska's average effective property tax rate of **1.73%** is significant when calculating total US tax liability on your rental property. While not a state income tax, property taxes may be deductible on your federal return (Form 1040-NR, Schedule A) and may provide foreign tax credits on your Canadian T1 return when combined with state income tax.

### Coordination with Canadian Tax Filing

Report all Nebraska rental income on your **Canadian T1 General return** in the year earned, even before your US return is filed. Use Form T2209 (Federal Foreign Tax Credit) to claim credits for US federal and state taxes paid. The foreign tax credit prevents double taxation but requires both your US and Canadian tax identification numbers (ITIN and SIN) to align properly on both returns.

### FIRPTA Withholding for Future Sales

When you sell the Nebraska property, the IRS requires Form 8288-B (FIRPTA withholding certificate). Provide your ITIN to the closing agent to establish the proper withholding rate and certificate. Without an ITIN, the full 15% withholding applies with no recourse.
