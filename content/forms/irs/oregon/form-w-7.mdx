# Form W-7 (ITIN) for Canadian Landlords with Oregon Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN), is the IRS document that allows non-US citizens and non-resident aliens to obtain a US taxpayer identification number. An ITIN is a nine-digit number formatted like a Social Security Number (SSN) but used exclusively for tax purposes when you don't qualify for an SSN.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN is essential for filing US federal tax returns (Form 1040-NR) and complying with Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) withholding requirements. Without an ITIN, you cannot properly report rental income, claim deductions, or file Oregon state returns—and you expose yourself to penalties and withholding complications if your property is sold.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords with Oregon Rental Property

As a Canadian resident owning rental property in Oregon, you are classified as a non-resident alien for US tax purposes. This classification triggers several key obligations:

**Federal Tax Filing**
You must file Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) annually to report Oregon rental income. The IRS requires you to include a valid ITIN on this return. Without one, the IRS will reject your return or impose penalties.

**Oregon State Tax Filing**
Oregon taxes non-residents on income derived from Oregon sources—including rental income. Oregon's top marginal tax rate is 9.9%, applied to rental net income. You must file Oregon Form 40-N (Oregon Nonresident Income Tax Return) using your ITIN as identification.

**FIRPTA Compliance**
Under FIRPTA, when your Oregon property is sold, the buyer (or their agent) must withhold 15% of the gross sale price as a deposit on your US tax liability. This withholding is tied to your ITIN. If you don't have an ITIN by the time of sale, the withholding agent must withhold at a default rate of 21%—a significant increase that can create cash flow problems and complication at closing.

**Canada-US Tax Treaty Benefits**
The Canada-US Income and Gains Tax Treaty (Article VI) addresses taxation of real property income. Even as a non-resident, you can claim certain treaty benefits on your Form 1040-NR (using Schedule OI) to reduce double taxation. Your ITIN is required to claim these benefits.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if:

- You are a Canadian citizen or resident who owns rental property in Oregon
- You do not have a US Social Security Number
- You need a US tax identification number to file Form 1040-NR for federal purposes
- You are subject to FIRPTA withholding on a property sale
- You need to file Oregon Form 40-N for state income tax purposes

You do **not** need to file W-7 if you already have an ITIN (you'll use the existing number on all future returns).

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

**Step 1: Gather Required Documents**

Before completing W-7, assemble:
- Valid passport (Canadian) or travel document
- Proof of Oregon rental property ownership (deed, purchase agreement, or property tax statement)
- Proof of Canadian residency (driver's license, provincial health card, or utility bill)
- Completed IRS Form W-7 (current tax year version)
- If filing jointly with a spouse: their passport and documentation

**Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Sections**

*Part A (Personal Information):*
- Enter your full name exactly as it appears on your passport
- Enter date of birth
- Enter your Canadian address
- Select "Individual" as entity type

*Part B (Type of Applicant):*
Select the box corresponding to your situation. For most Canadian landlords, this is:
- Box c: "Nonresident alien individual" (most common for landlords)
- You may also check if applying to file a US tax return

*Part C (Exemption from Waiver of Visa):*
This section applies only in specific circumstances; typically, you'll leave this blank.

*Part D (Country of Citizenship/Residence):*
Enter Canada.

*Part E (Type of ITIN Needed):*
Select the reason for your ITIN application:
- If you're filing a return: "To file a US tax return"
- If related to a property sale: "As a beneficial owner of US real property" or note the FIRPTA requirement

*Part F (Taxpayer Declaration):*
Sign and date the form. Your signature must match your passport.

**Step 3: Attach Required Documentation**

You must submit:
- **Original or certified copy** of your valid Canadian passport (not a photocopy unless certified by a notary)
- **Proof of Oregon real property ownership**: a copy of the deed, property tax assessment letter from the Oregon Department of Revenue, or purchase agreement
- **Proof of residency**: Copy of Canadian driver's license or utility bill showing your Canadian address

**Step 4: Submit to IRS**

You have three submission options:

1. **Mail to IRS**: Send Form W-7 with attachments to:
   - IRS Philadelphia ITIN Operation  
     Attn: ITIN Unit  
     2970 Market Street  
     Philadelphia, PA 19104-3553  
   - Processing time: 15–30 days after receipt

2. **Appointment at IRS Office**: Visit an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center (TAC) with original documents. Portland, Oregon has a TAC at:
   - 620 SW Main Street, Room 400, Portland, OR 97205
   - Bring originals; they'll return them and process same-day

3. **Authorized Certifying Agent**: Work with a Certified Public Accountant (CPA) or tax professional authorized by the IRS to certify your documents. Many cross-border tax firms in Vancouver, BC, or Seattle, WA, offer this service.

## Oregon-Specific Considerations

**Oregon Form 40-N Requirement**
Once you receive your ITIN, you must file Oregon Form 40-N annually if your rental income exceeded Oregon's filing threshold (currently $3,750 for 2024). Oregon has no reciprocal agreement exempting Canadian residents; all non-residents with Oregon-source income must file.

**Oregon Property Tax Implications**
While obtaining an ITIN doesn't affect Oregon property tax assessment, it does establish your tax liability identification for state records. Oregon's effective property tax rate averages 0.97%, but this is separate from your income tax filing obligation.

**Timing for New Property Owners**
If you've recently purchased Oregon rental property, file Form W-7 immediately—ideally before your first rental income is received. Oregon requires non-residents to file returns within 4 months and 15 days of the tax year end (typically April 15 for calendar-year filers).

**FIRPTA Withholding Planning**
If you plan to sell your Oregon property within the next 12 months, prioritize obtaining your ITIN beforehand. Provide the buyer's agent with your ITIN at least 30 days before closing; otherwise, the 21% default FIRPTA withholding will apply, and you'll be responsible for resolving the overpayment with the IRS.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Submitting Unauthorized Copies**: The IRS requires certified or original documents for identity verification. Photocopies from your local notary aren't sufficient unless the notary certifies them as true copies.

2. **Delaying Application Until Property Sale**: Obtaining an ITIN typically takes 2–3 weeks. If you wait until a sale is imminent, you'll miss the deadline to provide it to the buyer's agent, triggering the 21% withholding rate.

3. **Incomplete Form W-7**: Ensure Part E (Type of ITIN Needed) is clearly marked. Leaving it blank delays processing or causes rejection.

4. **Mismatched Names**: Your name on Form W-7 must exactly match your passport. Middle names, initials, and spelling must be identical.

5. **Ignoring Oregon State Filings**: Federal ITIN compliance doesn't automatically satisfy Oregon state requirements. File Form 40-N even if your federal return shows no tax due; Oregon law requires it.

6. **Not Tracking Your ITIN**: Record your ITIN immediately upon receipt. Use it consistently on all subsequent Form 1040-NR, Oregon Form 40-N, and FIRPTA documents.
