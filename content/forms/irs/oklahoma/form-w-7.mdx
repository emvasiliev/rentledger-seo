# Form W-7 for Canadian Landlords: Your Oklahoma ITIN Guide

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7, Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN), is the official IRS document you use to obtain an ITIN—a nine-digit taxpayer identification number assigned to non-US citizens who have US tax obligations but cannot obtain a Social Security Number (SSN).

An ITIN serves as your US tax identifier for all federal tax purposes. For Canadian landlords, it's essential for filing Form 1040-NR (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) and meeting FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) withholding requirements when you sell or refinance Oklahoma rental property.

Think of it as your US tax "passport." Without it, you cannot legally file US tax returns or comply with federal withholding rules—even though you have a Canadian Social Insurance Number (SIN).

## Why Canadian Landlords Need an ITIN in Oklahoma

As a Canadian landlord with rental property in Oklahoma, you have two separate tax obligations:

**Federal US obligation:** You must report rental income, depreciation, and deductions on Form 1040-NR annually. This income is not exempt under the Canada-US Tax Treaty just because you're Canadian.

**Oklahoma state obligation:** Oklahoma imposes a 4.75% state income tax on all rental income earned within the state by non-residents. You must file Oklahoma Form 504-NR (Non-Resident Income Tax Return) annually and report net rental income, even if you operate at a loss (losses don't shield you from filing requirements in Oklahoma).

**FIRPTA compliance:** When you eventually sell your Oklahoma property, the buyer's closing agent is required under FIRPTA to withhold 15% of the gross sale price (or net proceeds, depending on the transaction) and remit it to the IRS. To use Form 8288-B (Statement of Withholding on Dispositions by Foreign Persons), you *must* have an ITIN on file. Without one, withholding calculations become problematic, and the transaction may be delayed.

An ITIN is the foundational document that enables compliance across all three obligations.

## Who Must File Form W-7 for Oklahoma Rental Income

You must apply for an ITIN if:

- You are a Canadian citizen or permanent resident
- You own rental property in Oklahoma (directly or through a pass-through entity)
- You have no US Social Security Number
- You have a US tax filing requirement (rental income, capital gains on sale, or FIRPTA withholding)

If you operate the property through a US LLC or S-Corp, the entity itself may need an EIN (Employer Identification Number), but *you personally* still need an ITIN for your individual 1040-NR return to report your share of net income or capital gains.

**Important:** If you are a Canadian permanent resident or citizen, you do NOT automatically qualify for an SSN. You must apply for an ITIN.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documents

Before completing Form W-7, assemble:

- Your valid Canadian passport or birth certificate (original or certified copy)
- Proof of your Canadian tax residence (CRA Notice of Assessment, recent T1 return, or provincial tax return)
- Proof of your US rental property ownership (deed, closing statement, or property tax bill from Oklahoma County or the applicable county assessor)
- Your Canadian mailing address

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Sections

**Part I: Personal Information**
- Line 1: Enter your full legal name as it appears on your Canadian passport
- Line 2: Enter your date of birth (MMDDYYYY format)
- Line 3: Select your country of citizenship (Canada)
- Line 4: Enter your Canadian mailing address

**Part II: IRS Use Only**
- Leave blank; the IRS completes this section

**Part III: Identification Document Information**
- Specify Canadian passport as your identification document
- Enter your passport number and expiration date

**Part IV: Mailing Address**
- If different from your Canadian home address, provide your Oklahoma property address or a US representative's address (optional but recommended)

**Part V: Reason for Application**
- Check box "Other (specify)" and write: "Non-resident landlord, Form 1040-NR filing, rental income from Oklahoma real estate"

**Part VI: Declaration**
- Sign and date; you may sign electronically or by hand

### Step 3: Gather Supporting Attestation

Form W-7 requires **certified attestation** of your identification document. You have three options:

1. **IRS e-Services:** If you create an IRS online account, you can upload copies of your passport directly; the IRS verifies electronically (fastest option for Canadians, typically 2–4 weeks).

2. **Certified copies:** Provide a photocopy of your Canadian passport certified by your municipality, Canadian notary, or the Canadian embassy/consulate in the United States.

3. **In-person verification:** Visit an IRS office in the US (if you're in Oklahoma for property management) and present your original passport; an IRS employee will verify and return it to you.

The e-Services option is most practical for remote Canadian landlords.

### Step 4: Submit Form W-7

Mail Form W-7 with supporting documents to:

**Internal Revenue Service**  
ITIN Operations  
P.O. Box 149342  
Austin, TX 73714-9342

Or, if using e-Services, upload documents through your IRS account at **IRS.gov**.

**Processing time:** 2–4 weeks via e-Services; 4–6 weeks via mail (as of 2024).

## Oklahoma-Specific Considerations

### Oklahoma Property Tax and ITIN Coordination

Oklahoma's average effective property tax rate is 0.9% of assessed value. While property taxes are deductible on your Form 1040-NR, the Oklahoma County Assessor's office may request a US tax ID (ITIN or EIN) for property record purposes. Once you obtain your ITIN, provide it to the assessor to ensure clean title records and avoid complications when selling.

### Oklahoma 4.75% State Income Tax

Oklahoma requires you to file Form 504-NR and remit 4.75% state income tax on net rental income. However, Oklahoma allows a credit for income taxes paid to Canada on the same income (under the Canada-US Tax Treaty Article XXVI). 

**Filing sequence:** File your federal Form 1040-NR *first* with your ITIN. Then file Oklahoma Form 504-NR using the same ITIN. Report your foreign tax credit (FTC) on federal Form 1116, which will reduce your US federal tax; Oklahoma will recognize this reduction, and you may claim a reciprocal credit on Form 504-NR.

### FIRPTA and Oklahoma Sales

When you sell Oklahoma rental property, the closing agent will issue Form 8288 (FIRPTA withholding certification) to the IRS. Your ITIN must be on file at the time of sale. The withholding rate is 15% of the gross sales price (or net proceeds after costs, depending on the transaction structure). File Form 8288-B with the IRS within 20 days of closing using your ITIN.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: Using your Canadian SIN on Form 1040-NR**  
You cannot use your SIN as a substitute for an ITIN. The IRS will reject your return. Always wait for your ITIN before filing.

**Mistake 2: Delaying ITIN application until after a property sale**  
Apply for your ITIN *before* or *immediately after* acquiring the property. If a sale occurs while your ITIN is pending, FIRPTA withholding becomes complicated. Apply proactively.

**Mistake 3: Not providing updated Canadian documentation**  
If your passport has expired, the IRS may reject your application. Ensure your passport is valid for at least one more year before submitting Form W-7.

**Mistake 4: Forgetting to file Oklahoma Form 504-NR**  
Even if your net rental income is zero or negative, Oklahoma requires you to file Form 504-NR annually to avoid penalties. Report your income using your federal ITIN.

**Mistake 5: Omitting proof of property ownership**  
The IRS may deny your application if you don't provide documentation linking you to the Oklahoma property (deed, tax bill, or closing statement). Include this with your W-7 submission.
