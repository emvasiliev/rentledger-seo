## How to Get an ITIN (Form W-7) as a Canadian Landlord

If you're a Canadian resident who owns rental property in the United States, obtaining an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) is one of the first administrative steps you'll need to complete. Without an ITIN, you cannot file US tax returns, claim treaty benefits, or properly manage your FIRPTA withholding obligations.

This guide walks you through the complete ITIN application process, from understanding why you need one to receiving your number and using it for ongoing US tax compliance.

## What Is an ITIN and Why Do Canadian Landlords Need One?

An Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) is a nine-digit tax processing number issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS). It follows the same format as a Social Security Number (XXX-XX-XXXX) but always begins with the number 9 and has a 7 or 8 in the fourth digit position (9XX-7X-XXXX or 9XX-8X-XXXX).

### ITINs Are Required For:

**Filing Form 1040-NR**: As a non-resident alien with US-source rental income, you must file Form 1040-NR (US Nonresident Alien Income Tax Return) annually. The IRS requires a taxpayer identification number on this return.

**Claiming Treaty Benefits**: The Canada-US Tax Treaty allows you to reduce or eliminate certain withholding taxes, but you need an ITIN to claim these benefits on Form W-8BEN or your tax return.

**FIRPTA Compliance**: When you eventually sell your US property, the buyer must withhold 15% of the gross sales price under FIRPTA (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act) unless you have an ITIN and obtain a withholding certificate.

**Receiving Refunds**: If FIRPTA withholding exceeds your actual tax liability, you need an ITIN to file for and receive your refund.

**Property Management**: Many US property managers and financial institutions require an ITIN to set up accounts and process payments.

## Who Is Eligible for an ITIN?

You're eligible for an ITIN if you:

1. Do not have and are not eligible for a US Social Security Number
2. Have a requirement to furnish a federal tax identification number or file a federal tax return
3. Fall into one of the IRS-approved categories

As a Canadian landlord with US rental income, you qualify under the category of "non-resident alien required to file a US tax return."

**Important**: If you're eligible for a Social Security Number (SSN)—for example, if you have US work authorization—you must apply for an SSN instead of an ITIN.

## Step-by-Step ITIN Application Process

### Step 1: Complete Form W-7

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is a two-page form requiring:

**Personal Information**:
- Full legal name (must match your passport exactly)
- Mailing address (Canadian address is acceptable)
- Foreign address (if different from mailing address)
- Date and place of birth
- Country of citizenship

**Reason for Applying**: Check box (a) "Nonresident alien required to file a US tax return" and enter "1040-NR" in the space provided.

**Documentation Information**: List the identification documents you're submitting.

Sign and date the form. If you're using a Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA), they will complete their section.

### Step 2: Gather Required Documentation

The IRS requires original documents or certified copies from the issuing agency. For Canadian landlords, the most straightforward option is:

**Primary Document (Required)**:
- Valid Canadian passport (must be current, not expired)

Your passport serves as standalone proof of both identity and foreign status.

**Alternative Documents**: If you cannot submit a passport, you need a combination of documents proving identity and foreign status. Acceptable documents include:
- National identification card (showing photo, name, address, date of birth, and expiration)
- Canadian driver's license
- Birth certificate
- Foreign voter's registration card

**Critical Note on Document Copies**: The IRS only accepts:
1. Original documents (returned within 60 days)
2. Copies certified by the issuing agency (for passports, this means the passport office, not a notary)
3. Copies certified by a Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA)

Notarized photocopies are **not accepted**.

### Step 3: Prepare Your Tax Return

For most Canadian landlords, you must attach a completed Form 1040-NR to your W-7 application. This is the tax return that establishes your need for an ITIN.

**Exception**: If you're applying mid-year and haven't yet reached a filing deadline, you may be able to apply based on an anticipated filing requirement. However, attaching a tax return generally strengthens your application and is required in most circumstances.

Your Form 1040-NR should include:
- Schedule E (Supplemental Income and Loss) for rental income
- Any applicable elections (such as the IRC Section 871(d) election to treat rental income as effectively connected income)

### Step 4: Choose Your Submission Method

You have three options for submitting your ITIN application:

**Option A: Mail Application with Original Documents**

Mail your completed W-7, supporting documents, and Form 1040-NR to:

Internal Revenue Service  
ITIN Operation  
P.O. Box 149342  
Austin, TX 78714-9342

Original documents will be returned within 60 days of receipt. Use a trackable courier service when sending original passports.

**Option B: Use a Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA)**

CAAs are authorized by the IRS to verify your identity documents and certify copies. This means you don't have to mail your original passport. CAAs include certain accountants, lawyers, and enrolled agents who have completed IRS certification.

To find a CAA in Canada, search the IRS Acceptance Agent Program list at irs.gov or contact a cross-border tax professional.

CAA services typically cost $100–$400 CAD in addition to tax preparation fees.

**Option C: Visit an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center**

You can apply in person at designated IRS offices in the United States. This isn't practical for most Canadian landlords but may be convenient if you're already traveling to the US.

### Step 5: Wait for Processing

Current IRS processing times for ITIN applications are **7 to 11 weeks** during regular periods and can extend to 14+ weeks during peak tax season (January through April).

The IRS will mail your ITIN assignment letter (CP565) to the address on your W-7. Ensure your mailing address is accurate and can receive US mail.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Expired Passport**: Your passport must be valid at the time of application. The IRS will reject applications with expired identification documents.

**Name Mismatches**: Your name on Form W-7 must exactly match your passport. Include middle names if they appear on your passport.

**Missing Tax Return**: Unless you qualify for a specific exception, failing to attach Form 1040-NR is the most common reason for rejection.

**Using Notarized Copies**: Regular notarized photocopies are not accepted. You need certification from the issuing agency or a CAA.

**Applying Too Early**: You cannot apply for an ITIN before you have a tax filing requirement. Speculative applications will be rejected.

## ITIN Renewal Requirements

ITINs expire if not used on a federal tax return for three consecutive years. Additionally, ITINs with middle digits 70–88 have already expired and require renewal before use.

To renew, submit a new Form W-7 with current identification documents. You can renew without attaching a tax return if your ITIN has expired due to non-use and you're renewing for current-year filing.

## Timeline for Canadian Landlords

**Best Practice Timeline**:

1. **December–January**: Gather documents and prepare Form 1040-NR for the prior tax year
2. **February–March**: Submit W-7 application
3. **April–June**: Receive ITIN
4. **October 15**: Extended deadline for Form 1040-NR if needed

The standard Form 1040-NR deadline for calendar-year filers is **April 15** if you had withholding, or **June 15** if you had no withholding (with automatic extension to **October 15** available via Form 4868).
