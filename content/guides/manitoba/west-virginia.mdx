## US Rental Property Taxation for Manitoba Residents: West Virginia Guide

If you're a Manitoba resident earning rental income from a West Virginia property, you're operating in a unique tax environment. You'll file returns in three jurisdictions: Canada (CRA), the United States (IRS), and West Virginia. Each has different rules, deadlines, and withholding requirements. Understanding this layered system helps you avoid penalties, minimize withholding surprises, and claim legitimate deductions.

This guide walks through your tax obligations step by step.

## Why This Combination Matters

Manitoba and West Virginia tax rental income differently. In Manitoba, you report worldwide income to the CRA and pay federal and provincial tax on net rental profit. The US taxes non-residents on gross rental income at a federal level, then West Virginia adds a state layer. Canada and the US don't fully offset each other's taxes automatically—you'll need to manually claim foreign tax credits to avoid double taxation.

West Virginia's 0.59% effective property tax rate and 6.5% state income tax create additional obligations that many Canadian landlords miss on their first return.

## Canadian Tax Obligations: CRA

### Filing Form T776

You must report all worldwide rental income on **Form T776 (Statement of Real Estate Rentals)**. This form requires:

- Gross rents received (converted to Canadian dollars)
- Operating expenses (property tax, insurance, utilities, repairs, mortgage interest, property management fees)
- Depreciation (capital cost allowance, or CCA) — *optional but usually claimed*
- Net rental income or loss

**Key point:** The CRA requires you to report rental income whether or not the US withheld tax.

### Currency Conversion

Convert all US income and expenses to CAD using the **Bank of Canada annual average exchange rate**. For 2025 transactions, use 1 USD = 1.36 CAD (or the rate applicable to your tax year). Keep records of the rate used; the CRA may accept monthly rates if you file consistently.

### Form T1135: Foreign Property Disclosure

If your West Virginia property's fair market value exceeds CAD 100,000 at any point during the tax year, you must file **Form T1135 (Foreign Income Verification Statement)**. This form asks for:

- Location of the property (West Virginia, USA)
- Cost basis
- Fair market value in Canadian dollars
- Any income generated

File Form T1135 with your tax return. Failure to file can result in a $2,500 minimum penalty per year.

### Foreign Tax Credit Claim

You'll pay tax to both the US and Canada on the same income. Claim a **foreign tax credit** on Schedule 1 to reduce your federal tax. The credit is limited to the lesser of:

1. Foreign tax actually paid
2. Canadian federal tax on the same foreign income

**Example:** If you earned $5,000 USD net rental income and paid $750 USD in combined federal and West Virginia tax, you'd convert that to CAD (roughly $1,020 CAD) and claim it as a credit against your Canadian federal tax.

Manitoba also allows a provincial foreign tax credit; apply it on Form MB428.

## US Tax Obligations: IRS

### Obtaining an ITIN

As a non-US resident, you cannot use a Social Insurance Number (SIN) to file US returns. You must obtain an **Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN)** from the IRS. 

Apply using **Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number)** with certified copies of your passport and proof of Canadian residency. Mail to the IRS Philadelphia office (address on Form W-7). Processing takes 4–6 weeks. Many cross-border accountants handle this; it typically costs CAD 150–300 for professional assistance.

Once issued, your ITIN remains valid for five years of non-filing. If you file every year, it doesn't expire.

### Filing Form 1040-NR

Non-residents file the **Form 1040-NR (U.S. Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return)**, not the standard 1040. Key differences:

- You report gross rental income on **Schedule E (Profit or Loss from Business, Rental Real Estate, Royalties, Partnerships, S Corporations, Trusts, etc.)**
- You claim deductions (mortgage interest, property tax, insurance, repairs, depreciation)
- Report net rental income on the 1040-NR

If your net rental income is negative (loss), you generally cannot carry forward losses to offset other US income—a significant disadvantage for non-residents.

### Section 871(d) Election

Normally, the IRS withholds **30% of gross rent** from non-residents. This is punitive if your actual tax rate is lower than 30%, because the withholding is on *gross* income, not net profit.

File **Form 8288-B (Statement of Tax Withholding on Dispositions by Foreign Persons)** or include a statement with your first 1040-NR stating you **elect under Section 871(d)** to be taxed on net rental income instead. This reduces withholding to approximately your effective tax rate (usually 10–20% of net income).

Instruct your property manager or tenant to withhold only the amount shown on Form 1098-T, which you'll estimate based on your net income.

### US Tax Deadlines

- **Form 1040-NR filing deadline:** June 15, 2025 (for 2024 tax year); automatic 2-month extension to June 15, 2026
- **Quarterly estimated tax (Form 1040-ES):** April 15, June 17, September 15, 2025; January 15, 2026
- **Form W-7 (ITIN application):** No annual deadline, but renew every 5 years if you stop filing

## West Virginia State Tax Obligations

### State Income Tax Return

West Virginia imposes a **6.5% state income tax** on non-resident rental income. You must file **West Virginia Form IT-140 (Individual Income Tax Return)** as a non-resident.

Key rules:
- Report total US income (federal returns provide the baseline)
- Claim your deductions on Schedule C (if self-employed/rental) or carry forward federal net income
- West Virginia has no reciprocal credit for Canadian income tax, but you can claim it indirectly by using the federal foreign tax credit calculation

**Filing deadline:** April 15, 2025 (same as federal)

### Property Tax

West Virginia's effective property tax rate averages **0.59%** statewide, though rates vary by county. This is typically lower than Canadian property taxes but still significant.

Ensure your property manager remits property tax on time; unpaid property tax can result in tax sale proceedings. In some West Virginia counties, non-resident owners may face additional compliance or documentation requirements.

## Selling the Property: FIRPTA Overview

If you sell your West Virginia rental property, the US imposes the **Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA)** withholding. The buyer must withhold **15% of the sale price** and remit it to the IRS on your behalf.

File **Form 8288 (U.S. Withholding Tax Return for Disposition by Foreign Persons)** within 10 days of sale. You'll report the gain, calculate actual tax, and claim back any excess withholding.

FIRPTA is complex; consult a cross-border accountant before selling.
