# Form W-7: ITIN Application Guide for Canadian Landlords with Utah Rental Property

## What is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS application used by non-US citizens and non-resident aliens to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number formatted similarly to a Social Security Number (SSN) but exclusively for tax administration purposes.

For Canadian landlords, an ITIN serves two critical functions:

1. **Tax filing identification** – Required to file Form 1040-NR (U.S. Tax Return for Nonresident Alien Individuals) to report rental income from Utah property
2. **FIRPTA compliance** – Necessary for Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act (FIRPTA) withholding obligations when selling US real estate

Unlike an SSN, an ITIN does not authorize work or confer immigration status. It exists solely for federal tax compliance.

## How Form W-7 Applies to Canadian Landlords in Utah

### Utah's Tax Environment for Non-Resident Landlords

Utah imposes a **4.65% state income tax** on rental income earned within the state, even by non-residents. As a Canadian landlord with Utah rental property, you must:

- File **Form 40-N** (Utah Nonresident Return) annually if your net Utah-source income exceeds the filing threshold
- Report all rental income from Utah property (gross rents, less allowable deductions)
- Pay Utah state income tax independently from your federal obligations
- Include Utah property tax (averaging 0.63% of property value) as a deductible expense on both federal and state returns

Your ITIN connects all these filings. Without it, you cannot properly file 1040-NR with the IRS or Form 40-N with the Utah State Tax Commission.

### FIRPTA Withholding Context

If you sell Utah rental property, FIRPTA requires the buyer's closing agent or title company to withhold **15% of the gross sale price** and remit it to the IRS. To claim exemption from this withholding or to report the transaction correctly, you need an ITIN. The ITIN also allows you to file Form 8288-B (Statement of Withholding on Dispositions by Foreign Persons) to request a reduced withholding amount or a refund if excess tax was withheld.

### Canada-US Tax Treaty Implications

Under the Canada-US Income and Taxes Treaty (Article XXII), Canadian residents are generally entitled to credits for foreign taxes paid. When you file your Canadian T1 General return (Schedule 11), you can claim a foreign tax credit for US federal and Utah state income taxes paid on your rental income. However, you must have properly filed and paid US taxes first—which requires an ITIN.

## Who Files Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you are a Canadian resident (tax resident in Canada) who:

- Owns rental property in Utah and has no US Social Security Number
- Must file Form 1040-NR to report rental income to the IRS
- Requires an ITIN for FIRPTA transactions or withholding
- Cannot qualify for an SSN (e.g., you are not authorized to work in the US)

You do **not** need Form W-7 if you already hold an SSN or if you are a US citizen or permanent resident (green card holder).

## Step-by-Step Instructions for Completing Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing Form W-7, prepare:

- **Original or certified copy of your Canadian passport** or other national identity document
- **Birth certificate** (certified copy, if not contained in passport)
- **Proof of Canadian tax residency**: Most recent Notice of Assessment from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)
- **Proof of US rental property ownership**: Deed, property tax bill, or mortgage statement showing your name and Utah address
- **Documentation of your US tax filing requirement**: A copy of the incomplete 1040-NR you intend to file, or correspondence from a tax professional explaining why you need the ITIN

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Line-by-Line

**Part A: Personal Information**

- **Line 1a**: Enter your full legal name as it appears on your Canadian passport
- **Line 1b**: List any aliases or previous names
- **Line 2**: Enter your date of birth (MM/DD/YYYY format)
- **Line 3**: Indicate your country of citizenship (Canada)
- **Line 4**: Provide your Canadian residential address

**Part B: Mailing Address**

Enter your US mailing address (where the IRS can send your ITIN). This can be your Utah property address, your property manager's address, or a US accountant's office address.

**Part C: US Visa Information**

- Indicate your visa type (or "None" if you do not hold a US visa)
- Provide your passport number and expiration date

**Part D: Reason for Form W-7**

- Select **Box b**: "Nonresident alien filing a US tax return" (for rental income reporting), **or**
- Select **Box c**: "Other" and specify FIRPTA withholding or real property transaction purposes

**Part E: Preparer Information**

If a tax professional is assisting, provide their details. Many Canadian-American tax specialists will complete this section for you.

### Step 3: Gather Certified Copies

The IRS requires certified copies of identity documents. You can:

- Obtain certified copies from Service Canada or your provincial vital statistics office
- Have your Canadian accountant certify the documents
- In some cases, the IRS will accept documents certified by a notary public in the US

**Do not send original documents**—always submit certified copies only.

### Step 4: Submit to the IRS

**Mailing Address:**

```
Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operation
P.O. Box 447
Bensalem, PA 19020
USA
```

**Processing Time:**

- Standard processing: 4–6 weeks (by mail)
- Expedited processing: Available through an IRS Acceptance Agent (in-person verification reduces processing time to 1–2 weeks)

**In-Person Verification Option:**

You can visit an IRS Acceptance Agent in Utah (typically located in IRS offices in Salt Lake City) with your documents to accelerate processing. Many Canadian accountants use this method for clients.

## Utah-Specific Considerations

### Filing Deadlines Coordination

- **Federal Form 1040-NR deadline**: April 15 (same as US residents); extensions available until October 15
- **Utah Form 40-N deadline**: April 15 (same as federal deadline)
- **ITIN Application Timing**: Apply **before** these deadlines to avoid late-filing penalties

### Utah Property Tax Integration

Utah's property tax (averaging 0.63% statewide, but varies by county and municipality) is deductible as a rental expense on both your 1040-NR and Form 40-N. Ensure you:

- Maintain copies of all Utah property tax bills
- Claim depreciation on the building (not land) on Form 4562 (Depreciation)
- Deduct mortgage interest, repairs, utilities, and property management fees

### State-Level Withholding

If you hire a US property manager or real estate professional in Utah, they may be subject to **Utah backup withholding** if you do not provide your ITIN. Furnishing your ITIN to the property manager and payor entities ensures correct reporting on Form 1098-S (Mortgage Interest Statement) and Form 1099-MISC (Miscellaneous Income).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Submitting original documents instead of certified copies**: The IRS will reject your application and return original documents.
2. **Incomplete or inconsistent name spelling**: Your W-7 name must match exactly with your passport, T1 return, and all property ownership documents.
3. **Providing a Canadian mailing address without a US contact address**: The IRS requires a US address to mail your ITIN assignment letter.
4. **Failing to indicate the correct reason for needing an ITIN**: Using the wrong box can cause delays; clearly state rental income filing or FIRPTA purposes.
5. **Not submitting the W-7 early enough**: If you wait until April to apply and need the ITIN for that year's 1040-NR filing, delays may result in penalties.
6. **Omitting proof of US tax filing requirement**: Include documentation (such as a property deed or incomplete 1040-NR draft) demonstrating why you need the ITIN.
