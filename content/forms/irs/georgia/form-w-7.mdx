# Form W-7: ITIN Guide for Canadian Landlords with Georgia Rental Property

## What Is Form W-7?

Form W-7 (Application for IRS Individual Taxpayer Identification Number) is the IRS application that allows non-US citizens without a Social Security Number (SSN) to obtain an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). An ITIN is a nine-digit tax identification number that functions like an SSN for federal tax purposes.

For Canadian landlords, the ITIN is essential for filing US federal tax returns and complying with US tax withholding obligations on rental income and property dispositions.

## Why Canadian Landlords in Georgia Need an ITIN

As a Canadian resident owning rental property in Georgia, you are classified as a **non-resident alien** for US tax purposes. The IRS requires all individuals filing a US tax return to have either a Social Security Number or an ITIN.

Your primary obligations requiring an ITIN include:

- **Filing Form 1040-NR** (US Non-Resident Alien Income Tax Return) to report rental income, mortgage interest deductions, and property expenses
- **Complying with FIRPTA withholding** when you eventually sell the Georgia property (Foreign Investment in Real Property Tax Act)
- **Filing Georgia Form IT-NR** (Georgia Non-Resident Income Tax Return) on rental income subject to Georgia's 5.75% state income tax rate
- **Obtaining an ITIN before filing**, as the IRS will not process a 1040-NR without one

## Georgia-Specific Tax Context

Understanding Georgia's tax environment is critical for your compliance strategy:

**Georgia State Income Tax**: Georgia taxes rental income at a flat 5.75% rate for non-residents. You must file Georgia Form IT-NR annually if you have Georgia-source rental income exceeding the filing threshold.

**Georgia Property Taxes**: Georgia's average effective property tax rate is 0.92% of home value. This is deductible on your US Form 1040-NR Schedule A (if you itemize), and also deductible on your Canadian T1 return as foreign taxes paid (subject to foreign tax credit limitations).

**FIRPTA Withholding**: When you sell the Georgia property, the buyer's closing agent must withhold 15% of the sale price under FIRPTA rules. Having your ITIN in place before the sale prevents delays in the withholding process and ensures proper credit application.

## Who Must File Form W-7

You must file Form W-7 if you:

- Are a Canadian resident (non-US citizen)
- Own rental property in Georgia generating US-source income
- Do not have a US Social Security Number
- Plan to file a Form 1040-NR for US tax years you have Georgia rental income
- Need to provide an ITIN to a property buyer or manager for FIRPTA withholding purposes

You do **not** need an ITIN if your only US connection is investment income (dividends, interest) that is adequately withheld at source, though rental real estate income requires direct filing.

## Step-by-Step: How to Complete Form W-7

### Step 1: Gather Required Documentation

Before completing W-7, assemble:

- Valid Canadian passport or equivalent government-issued identification
- Proof of Canadian residence (utility bill, lease, official correspondence)
- Documentation of your US tax requirement (e.g., copy of your draft Form 1040-NR for the year in question)
- Proof of the Georgia rental property ownership (deed, closing documents, property tax bill)

### Step 2: Complete Form W-7 Accurately

**Part I – Applicant Information**:
- Line 1: Full name as it appears on your Canadian passport
- Line 2: Country of citizenship (Canada)
- Line 3: Country of residence (Canada)
- Line 4: US visa status: Select "Other" and write "Non-Resident Alien – Rental Real Estate"
- Line 5: Passport number (Canadian passport)
- Line 6: Date of birth
- Line 7: Gender

**Part II – Mailing Address**:
- Use your Canadian mailing address
- The IRS will mail your ITIN approval notice (Notice of ITIN Assignment) to Canada

**Part III – Reason for Application**:
- Check the box for "US tax return filing requirement"
- In the space provided, specify: "Non-resident alien filing Form 1040-NR for rental property income in Georgia"

**Part IV – Declaration**:
Sign and date the form. Obtain a signature from a third-party certifying official (see next section) rather than self-certifying where possible.

### Step 3: Arrange Third-Party Certification

The IRS prefers Form W-7 be accompanied by a **Form 8288-B certification** from:

- A US tax professional (CPA or enrolled agent) licensed in the US
- A Canadian cross-border tax specialist with IRS authority
- A US bank officer who has verified your identity

For Canadian applicants, having a cross-border CPA review and certify the application significantly reduces processing delays.

### Step 4: Photocopy Identity Documents

Prepare certified photocopies of:

- Pages of your Canadian passport showing your photograph, name, and passport number
- A secondary document confirming Canadian residency

**Critical**: Do NOT send original documents. The IRS will not return originals sent with W-7 applications.

### Step 5: File the Application

**Mailing Address**:
```
Internal Revenue Service
ITIN Operation
Austin, TX 73301
USA
```

**Processing Time**: Allow 4–6 weeks for ITIN assignment from date of receipt. The IRS will mail your Notice of ITIN Assignment (Form W-7 approval) to your Canadian address.

**Alternative**: If you are physically present in the US, you may apply in person at an IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center, which can accelerate processing to same-day approval.

## Georgia-Specific Considerations

### Timing With Georgia IT-NR Filing

The Georgia Department of Revenue requires an ITIN or SSN on Form IT-NR. You must obtain your ITIN **before** filing your Georgia state return. Since federal Form 1040-NR and Georgia Form IT-NR are typically filed simultaneously (both due April 15), apply for your ITIN **at least 8–10 weeks before** your intended filing date.

### Coordination With Canadian T1 Return

You must report the same Georgia rental income on both:

- **US Form 1040-NR** (Schedule E for rental income and expenses)
- **Canadian Form T1** (Line 10410 for foreign employment/rental income)

The ITIN ensures consistent reporting across both jurisdictions. In Canada, you claim a **foreign tax credit** (Form T2209) for US federal and state taxes paid on this income.

### FIRPTA Withholding Setup

Once you receive your ITIN, provide it to your Georgia property manager or tenant-paying entity **in writing**. If you later sell the property, notify the closing attorney of your ITIN. The buyer's agent must withhold 15% of the gross sale price under FIRPTA, but your ITIN ensures the withholding is credited to your account when you file Form 8288 (FIRPTA withholding declaration).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: Filing 1040-NR without an ITIN**  
The IRS will reject the return. Always apply for the ITIN first; allow 6–8 weeks for processing before filing season.

**Mistake 2: Inconsistent name spelling**  
Ensure your full name on W-7 matches exactly how it appears on your Canadian passport, your Georgia property deed, and your 1040-NR. Mismatches cause refund delays and correspondence issues.

**Mistake 3: Using a US address on W-7 if you don't live there**  
If you list a US mailing address, the IRS may classify you as a US resident. Always provide your Canadian address as your primary residence.

**Mistake 4: Failing to certify copies**  
Photocopies must be certified by a notary or CPA. The IRS does not accept uncertified scans or unverified copies.

**Mistake 5: Not coordinating with Canada**  
Some Canadian landlords inadvertently fail to report the ITIN on their Canadian T1 return, creating audit risk. Your Canadian accountant should be aware of your US filing obligations.
